{%Pauṇḍraka%} (I)¦ — (also Pauṇḍra): King of Kāśi; son of
Vasudeva, and Sutanu, daughter of Kāśirāja. When he
became king, he asserted that he was the real Vāsudeva,
and married Devakī the youngest sister of Kamsa. Besieged
Dvārakā during Kṛṣṇa's absence at night, and being put to
defeat by Balarāma and Sātyaki, went back to his capital.
Sent a messenger to Kṛṣṇa saying that he was the real Vāsu-
deva and that Kṛṣṇa was either to give up the title or offer
battle. Kṛṣṇa invaded Kāśi and killed him and his allies;<sup>1</sup>
was stationed by Jarāsandha during the siege of Mathurā
on its southern gate, and also during the siege of Gomanta
on the southern gate. Arrived at Kuṇḍina.<sup>2</sup> Father of
Sudakṣiṇā.<sup>3</sup> Attained {%mokṣa%} by hatred of Hari.<sup>4</sup>
