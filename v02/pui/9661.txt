{%Bhīmasena%} (I)¦ — (also Bhīma); a Pāṇḍava son of
Kuntī; father of Śrutasena; had another son Ghaṭotkaca
by Hidimbā, and a third Sarvagata by Kālī;<sup>1</sup> failed to hit the
fish in Lakṣmaṇā's {%svayamvara;%}<sup>2</sup> joy at Kṛṣṇa's visit to
Indraprastha and Kṛṣṇa's respects to him; was consoled by
Kṛṣṇa when banished to forest;<sup>3</sup> sent to the western terri-
tories with the Matsyas, Kekayas and Madrakas.<sup>4</sup> Advised
by Uddhava to go in Brahmana's disguise to Jarāsandha and
vanquish him; went with Kṛṣṇa and Arjuna to Girivraja.
After killing Jarāsandha, Bhīmasena returned to Hastinā-
pura.<sup>5</sup> Declined to follow Balarāma's advice not to fight;
fought with Duryodhana at Kurukṣetra and felled him with
his {%gadā;%}<sup>6</sup> was in charge of cooking in the Rājasūya of
Yudhiṣṭhira;<sup>7</sup> fed Dhṛtarāṣṭra; advocated killing of Aśvat-
thāma and felt sorry at Kṛṣṇa's separation;<sup>8</sup> put an end to
the Rākṣasas born of Krodhavaśā: propagator of {%Bhīmadvā-%}
{%daśīvrata;%}<sup>9</sup> gave Piṇḍa sitting on his left leg to Janārdhana,
[Page2-565+ 24]
and attained Brahmaloka with his brothers;<sup>10</sup> strength of;
depended on the good will of Kṛṣṇa.<sup>11</sup>
