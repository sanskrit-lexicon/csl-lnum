{%Mahāpadma%} (III)¦ — the son of Mahānandi by a Śūdra
woman; he was the universal emperor and brought the
earth under his umbrella; ruled for 88 (28 {%Vi. P.%}) years;
from him all kings became unrighteous; he was a scourge
of the Kṣatriyas, and just like Paraśurāma rooted out their
families; he had eight sons, Sumālya (Sumātī {%Vi. P.,%}
Sukalpa {%M. P.%}) and others, all of whom ruled altogether
for 100 (12 {%M. P.%}) years; then a Brahmana Kauṭalya {%M. P.%})
brought about their fall;<sup>1</sup> from Parīkṣit to Mahāpadma is
1050 years; from Mahāpadma to Puloma Andhra is 836
years.<sup>2</sup>
