{%Harikathā%}¦ — (also Nārāyaṇa Kathā) sung in the sabhās
of Brahmā and Śiva: sacred, never dull and always new;<sup>1</sup>
one way of getting divine knowledge: leads to virtue, nectar
itself: purifies men and women;<sup>2</sup> fit to listen to, and fit to
[Page3-748+ 40]
be told and listened to: would never give satiety: by listening
to it one gets proper vision and crosses the road of samsāra.
For instance hearing of the {%avatār%} of Vāmana leads one to
bliss and that of Matsya to world's welfare;<sup>3</sup> purifies the
worlds and destroys the sins of Kali;<sup>4</sup> faith in the Kathā im-
portant;<sup>5</sup> importance of, stated by Nṛsimha to Prahlāda, and
by Nārada to Yudhiṣṭira;<sup>6</sup> praise of;<sup>7</sup> Parīkṣit's deep interest
in, and Nārada singing of;<sup>8</sup> Ariṣtasena hears it and narrates
it to Kimpuruṣa;<sup>9</sup> narrated by Śuka;<sup>10</sup> by Gopis on the eve
of Kṛṣṇa's departure to Mathurā, and on hearing Kṛṣṇa's
message through Uddhava.<sup>11</sup>
