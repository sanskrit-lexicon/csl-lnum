{%Pradyumna%} (I)¦ — the eldest son of Kṛṣṇa by Rukminī;
in his previous birth the God of Love (Kāma, Smara);<sup>1</sup>
within ten days of his birth Śambara stole him and threw
the babe into the sea; was swallowed by a fish; fishermen
who caught the fish presented it to Śambara who asked the
cooks to cook it; when cut open there was the child inside
and Nārada asked Māyāvatī in charge of the kitchen to take
care of the child after telling her the truth about it; that it
was the re-incarnation of her husband Kāma; she brought
up the child more as a lover; asked by Pradyumna why she
was not behaving as a mother but as a wife she explained
that she was Ratī and himself Kāma, and also as to how he
came there by Śambara's act; taught Māyāvatī the {%vidyā%} of
[Page2-417+ 46]
{%Mahāmāyā:%} he invited Śambara to battle and cut off his
head; then Māyāvatī took him by air to Dvārakā the resi-
dence of Rukmiṇī and Kṛṣṇa; in Kṛṣṇa's palace, women
found the likeness of Kṛṣṇa in him and Rukminī thought of
her lost son; to their wonder and joy Nārada related the life
story of Pradyumna; the couple were embraced by all of
them and the whole Dvārakā was filled with satisfaction.<sup>2</sup>
Won his wife Rukmāvatī (Vaidarbhī) in a {%Svayamvara%}
after vanquishing his rivals; father of Aniruddha;<sup>3</sup> attended
his son's marriage at Bhojakaṭa;<sup>4</sup> Picture drawn by Citra-
lekha;<sup>5</sup> one among the Vṛṣṇis who went to attack Bāṇa's
city;<sup>6</sup> fought with Guha;<sup>7</sup> went along with his brothers to
play and seeing a huge lizard in a well reported the same to
Kṛṣṇa;<sup>8</sup> defended Dvārakā with other Yadus when Śālva
besieged it; defeated Śālva's commander-in-chief and did
other feats of valour; had a hit in the chest by Śālva's minis-
ter Dyumat and was taken out by his charioteer, whom he
chided for his action; the charioteer defended his action
under the ethics of war; attacked and vanquished Dyumat;<sup>9</sup>
went to Syamantapañcaka for the solar eclipse;<sup>10</sup> went with
Kṛṣṇa's sacrificial horse;<sup>11</sup> unable to recover the dead son
of the Dvārakā Brahmana;<sup>12</sup> fought with Sāmba at Prabhāsa
deluded by Kṛṣṇa;<sup>13</sup> at his death his wives burnt themselves
in fire.
