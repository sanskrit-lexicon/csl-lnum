{%Devakī%} (I)¦ — (Devakabhojaputrī Sunāmā) a Bhoja
princess; a daughter of Devaka, one of the wives of Vasu-
deva and mother of eight sons of whom Kṛṣṇa was one, all
of them comparable to the eight Vasus;<sup>1</sup> oppressed by Kamsa
and relieved by Kṛṣṇa; was about to be murdered by Kamsa
who heard a divine voice that her eighth child would be
the cause of his end; on Vasudeva's assurance to give up
the babies as they were born she was allowed to go. Her
first child Kīrtimat was taken to Kamsa who returned the
baby. She was imprisoned with Vasudeva to watch over
the birth of her sons. During her seventh pregnancy the
{%Dhāma%} of Hari in her womb was transferred by Yogamāyā
to that of Rohiṇī and it was said that Devakī aborted. Then
the {%amśa%} of Hari entered her womb and she grew beautiful
every day. Brahmā, Śiva and other Gods waited on her
on the eve of Kṛṣṇa's birth and said that her son was to kill
[Page2-114+ 38]
Kamsa: addressed the new-born Kṛṣṇa. In the Svāyam-
bhuva epoch she was Pṛśni and Vasudeva was Sutapa. In
another epoch she was Aditi and Vasudeva was Kaśyapa.
Yaśodā's daughter exchanged for Devakī's son and Kamsa
was told of the birth of a girl; was released from prison
along with Vasudeva; forgave and advised Kamsa; was
saluted by Kṛṣṇa and Rāma.<sup>2</sup>
<div n="P"/>Yudhiṣṭhira asked Arjuna about her welfare.<sup>3</sup> Kṛṣṇa's
respects to, after his return from Indra's abode.<sup>4</sup> Devakī
came to Rukmiṇī's palace with Vasudeva and Kṛṣṇa;<sup>5</sup> felt
concerned at Kṛṣṇa not returning from the cave of Jāmba-
vān and aided by others prayed to Durgā for Kṛṣṇa's
return;<sup>6</sup> wondered at the discourses between Nimi
and the sages, sons of Ṛṣabha;<sup>7</sup> embraced Yaśodā
at Syamantapañcaka and enquired after her welfare;<sup>8</sup>
praised Kṛṣṇa and Rāma and desired to see her sons
who had been killed by Kamsa; saw them with joy when
taken back from Sutala by Kṛṣṇa; in her presence they left
for heaven.<sup>9</sup> Her grief at the decease of Rāma, Kṛṣṇa and
others; entered fire.<sup>10</sup>
