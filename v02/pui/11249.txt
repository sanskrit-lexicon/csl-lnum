{%Yaśodā%} (I)¦ — the wife of Nanda (gopa);<sup>1</sup> gave birth to
a female child who was {%Yogamāyā%} incarnate. Without her
knowledge, Vasudeva placed Kṛṣṇa born on the same day
in her bed and removed the girl to his residence. This was
known to Nārada.<sup>2</sup> Regarded Kṛṣṇa as her own child; per-
formed {%rakṣā%} to Kṛṣṇa after his killing Pūtanā; her concern
at Kṛṣṇa's kicking the cart (Śakaṭāsura) upside down, and
śānti arranged; distressed at Kṛṣṇa being carried away by
the whirlwind (Tṛṇāvarta); glad to see him alive; her con-
cern at Kṛṣṇa's eating mud, and wonder when he revealed
his true form and the whole universe in his mouth; binding
of Kṛṣṇa with a rope for breaking the pot and stealing butter;
took home Kṛṣṇa playing on the river-bank; intense love
to Kṛṣṇa; joy at his escape from Kālīya; surprise at Kṛṣṇa
holding Govardhana; recalled his exploits at Uddhava's
visit, and gave him a hearty welcome.<sup>3</sup> Went to Syamanta-
pañcaka and was embraced with affection by Rohiṇī and
Devakī;<sup>4</sup> represents the Mother Earth;<sup>5</sup> remedy of, against
bāladoṣa;<sup>6</sup> prayer of, to Śakaṭa;<sup>7</sup> rope round the belly of
Kṛṣṇa for mischief.<sup>8</sup>
