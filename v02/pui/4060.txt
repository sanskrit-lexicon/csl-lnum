{%Gaya%} (IV)¦ — a son of Nakta and Druti: A rājaṛṣi and
an {%amśa%} of Hari; a mahāpuruṣa who ruled his kingdom
righteously and with devotion to Hari. His name is sung
in an ancient gāthā, as the upholder of {%dharma,%} Vedas,
Brahmaṇas and yajñas. His queen was Gāyantī who was
mother of three sons Citraratha and others. At the end of
[Page1-515+ 27]
his rule, he renounced the throne and sought refuge with
Hari.
