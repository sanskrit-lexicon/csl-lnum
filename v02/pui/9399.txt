<L>9399<pc>2-529<k1>BadrakAlI<k2>BadrakAlI
{%Bhadrakālī%}¦ — a name of Yogamāyā;<sup>1</sup> bestows good on
children when propitiated with human sacrifice; when the
Goddess found the man sacrificed was a virtuous Brahmana
like Bharata she became fiery and cut off the heads of the
Vṛṣala chief and his attendants who offered him to her;<sup>2</sup>
fought with Niśumbha and Śumbha in the Devāsura war;<sup>3</sup>
worshipped by the Gopīs at the end of their {%vrata.%}<sup>4</sup> Lion
as her riding animal; having three eyes and a trident: praised
by Paraśurāma;<sup>5</sup> came out of the wrath of Umā for the
destruction of the Dakṣa's {%yajña.%}<sup>6</sup>
