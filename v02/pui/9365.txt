{%Bhagīratha%}¦ — the son of Dilīpa and father of Śruta
(Suhotra {%Vi. P.%}); by penance on the banks of the Bindusaras
he succeeded in bringing the Ganges; was asked how she
was to dispose of the sins of men discharged into her waters;
Bhagīratha said that Sādhus and others among whom was
Hari would take off the sins from her: was asked to pray
to Śiva to check her course; so he prayed to Śiva and it was
heard; by this he was able to shake off the sins of his
ancestors;<sup>1</sup> a Rājaṛṣi; went to the forest of the Gaura hills in
search of Gangā;<sup>2</sup> his lust after more territory;<sup>3</sup> after him
[Page2-526+ 23]
Gangā became the Bhāgīrathī; so-called after the seventh
branch of the Ganges.<sup>4</sup>

26; Vā. 88. 167; Vi. IV. 4. 35-6.</F>
<F>2) Vā. 47. 24.</F>
<F>3) Bhā. XII. 3. 10.</F>
<F>4) Br. III. 54. 48-51; 63. 166-8; Vā. 47. 40.</F>
