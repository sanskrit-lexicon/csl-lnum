{%Varṇāśrama%}¦ — social polity;<sup>1</sup> truth, charity and self-
control are equal to {%tīrthas%} for home-keepers;<sup>2</sup> Yayāti speaks
of four Varṇas born of one body and their respective duties
of whom the Brāhmaṇa is superior;<sup>3</sup> lost in a period of anar-
chy;<sup>4</sup> in the Śākadvīpa;<sup>5</sup> said by Manu;<sup>6</sup> restrictions get
loosened in Dvāpara;<sup>7</sup> disappearance of, in Kali;<sup>8</sup> (see Varṇa
dharma). In Tretā Kṣatriyas followed the Brāhmaṇas, the
Vaiśyas the Kṣatriyas and the Śūdras the Vaiśyas; there was
thus peace everywhere and everything bore fruit;<sup>9</sup> leads to
enjoyment in heaven;<sup>10</sup> no such system in Kṛtayuga.<sup>11</sup>
