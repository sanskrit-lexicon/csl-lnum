{%Himavān%} (II)¦ — (also Himagiri): one of the six tra-
versed by Alakanandā;<sup>1</sup> Varṣaparvata of Himācala.
King of mountains; overlord of all hills;<sup>2</sup> noted for the
sacred pool of Suṣumṇa;<sup>3</sup> a description of the forests,
trees and the races inhabiting the mountain; water-
falls and caves in; cool with snow;<sup>4</sup> served as calf when the
mountains milked the earth for medicinal plants and gems;<sup>5</sup>
where Paraśurāma performed penance;<sup>6</sup> people towards the
end of Kaliyuga leave their homes and retire to forests and
caves in the Himālayan slopes;<sup>7</sup> residence of Rākṣasas, Piśācas
and Yakṣas;<sup>8</sup> Parīkṣit compared to, in worthiness: Dhṛta-
rāṣṭra spent here his last days.<sup>9</sup>
