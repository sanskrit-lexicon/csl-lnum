<L>5507<pc>2-029<k1>tumburu<k2>tumburu<h>I
{%Tumburu%} (I)¦ — an expert in divine music; had two
daughters Manovatī and Sukeśā;<sup>1</sup> a friend of Candrodaka
dundubhī.<sup>2</sup> A Gandharva disciple of Nārada, came with
the sage to see Yudhiṣṭhira, and returned to heaven with
him;<sup>3</sup> sang with Nārada the glories of Ananta;<sup>4</sup> praised
Hiraṇyakaśipu when he became the overlord of all worlds.<sup>5</sup>
Sang the praise of Kṛṣṇa when he held the Govardhana;<sup>6</sup>
presiding over the months of Madhu and Mādhava.<sup>7</sup> His two
daughters were celebrated as Pañcacūḍas;<sup>8</sup> residing in the
Sun's chariot in the months of Caitra and Madhu.<sup>9</sup>
