{%Bharadvāja%} (I)¦ — also called Vitatha: a Siddha;<sup>1</sup> be-
came a son of Bharata; when Mamatā, the wife of the brother
of Bṛhaspati was pregnant, Bṛhaspati had sexual inter-
course with her; the foetus obstructing him, he cursed the
child in embryo; fearing divorce by her husband, Mamatā
abandoned the child when Gods said {%“Bhara dvājam”%} i.e.
‘rear the child born of two’, and hence he became Bharad-
vāja; still she abandoned him; nourished by the Maruts he
was given over to Bharata;<sup>2</sup> father of Manyu;<sup>3</sup> a sage of the
Vaivasvata epoch;<sup>4</sup> invited for the Rājasūya of Yudhiṣṭhira;<sup>5</sup>
called on the dying Bhīṣma;<sup>6</sup> came to Syamantapañcaka to
[Page2-542+ 41]
see Kṛṣṇa;<sup>7</sup> came to see Parīkṣit practising Prāyopaveśa;<sup>8</sup>
officiated in Paraśurāma's sacrifice. Heard the Purāṇa from
Sṛñjaya and narrated it to Gautama.<sup>9</sup>
