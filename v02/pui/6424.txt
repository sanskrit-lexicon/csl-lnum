{%Draupadī%}¦ — the daughter of Yajñasena-Draupada, queen
of the Pāṇḍavas and mother of five sons born to five
brothers: Prativindhya to Yudhiṣṭhira, Śrutasena to Bhīma,
Śrutakīrti to Arjuna, Śrutānīka to Nakula and Śrutakarma
to Sahadeva;<sup>1</sup> paid respects to Kṛṣṇa; being newly married
was bashful;<sup>2</sup> consoled by Kṛṣṇa and Satyabhāmā when
banished to forest with her husbands;<sup>3</sup> joy at Kṛṣṇa's visit
to Indraprastha; welcomed Rukmiṇī and the other wives of
Kṛṣṇa; served food, etc., in the Rājasūya; performed Avabhṛta
with Yudhiṣṭhira after the Rājasūya; Duryodhana's mind on;
laughed at Duryodhana's fall in the Sabhā of Maya;<sup>4</sup> asked
Kṛṣṇa's wives about their marriage when all met at Syaman-
tapañcaka and was lost in wonder.<sup>5</sup> Her sons were killed
while asleep by Aśvatthāma; when the murderer was brought
before her, she ordered his release as he was a Brahmana
and the son of the preceptor; followed the funeral party to
the Ganges for the cremation of her dead sons;<sup>6</sup> was one
among the party that welcomed Vidura;<sup>7</sup> out of devotion to
Vāsudeva attained His lotus feet.<sup>8</sup> Felt sorry for the loss of
Duryodhana and others.<sup>9</sup>
