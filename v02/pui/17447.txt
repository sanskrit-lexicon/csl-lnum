{%Heti%} (III)¦ — a Rākṣasa, and a son of Brahmā; performed
severe austerities for a hundred thousand divine years
by living on air and dried leaf with face and hands turned
upwards and the little finger of foot planted on earth; got a
[Page3-779+ 30]
boon that he must not be killed by implements, or gods or
men; Heti defeated the gods and became Indra himself; the
gods gave the gadā to Viṣṇu to kill Heti and hence ādigadā-
dhara; Heti was killed and taken to heaven;<sup>1</sup> with the sun
in spring;<sup>2</sup> in the sun's chariot in the months of Caitra and
Madhu.<sup>3</sup>
