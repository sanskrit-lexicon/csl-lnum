{%Bhāratam varṣam%}¦ — after king Bharata, son of Ṛṣabha;
Viṣṇu in the form of Kūrma; south of the Himālayas and
north of the sea;<sup>1</sup> a Karmabhūmi;<sup>2</sup> 9000 yojanas in extent;<sup>3</sup>
the seven chief mountains in it are the Mahendra, Malaya,
Sahya, Śuktimat, Ṛkṣa, Vindhya and Pāriyātra; divided into
nine divisions. Indradvīpa, Kaśeru, Tāmraparṇa, Gabhas-
timat, Nāgadvīpa, Saumya, Gandharva, and Vāruṇa ({%c.%}
Aruna ?), besides the present land; a 1000 yojanas from
north to south; on the east are the Kirātas and on the west
the Yavanas; in the middle, men of the four castes; then
rivers are mentioned;<sup>4</sup> here time reckoned as four yugas; the
best of all territories in Jambūdvīpa; original name was Aja-
nābha;<sup>5</sup> conquered by Parīkṣit;<sup>6</sup> described.<sup>7</sup>
