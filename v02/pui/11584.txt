{%Rājasūyam%}¦ — the gift of {%Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa%} equal
to the performance of 1000 sacrifices.<sup>1</sup> The fruits
of this {%yajña%} are equal to fasting and praying to
Viṣṇu on the akṣayatṛtīya day;<sup>2</sup> a plunge in the Prayāgā is
equal to this {%yajña.%}<sup>3</sup> Sacrifice performed by Soma when
Viṣṇu was Brahmā, Śiva, the protector, Atri, the hota,
Bhṛgu, the {%adhvaryu,%} Brahmā, the udgāta, Sanaka and
others were the Sadasyas, ten Viśvedevas Camasādhvaryavas,
and the three worlds dakṣiṇa; after this sacrifice the nine
Devīs, Lakṣmī, Sinivālī, Kīrtī, Vasu, Dhṛtī and others left
their husbands and sported with Soma who acted as their
consort;<sup>4</sup> the title of Soma after the Rājasūya.<sup>5</sup>

<F>2) Ib. 65. 7.</F>
<F>3) Ib. 106. 21.</F>

239. 37; Vi. IV. 6. 8.</F>
<F>5) Vā. 90. 26.</F>
