{%Vināyaka%} (I)¦ — a name of Vighneśvara or Vighneśa
(s.v.)<sup>1</sup> in charge of Kailāsa hill<sup>2</sup> a graha.<sup>3</sup> Asked Paraśu-
rāma not to enter Śiva's abode as he was with Umā. When
he forced entry, Vināyaka made him wander all the
seven worlds. Paraśurāma knocked his teeth with
his axe to the enragement of Pārvatī. Śiva thought
of Kṛṣṇa who appeared on the scene with Rādhā.
Kṛṣṇa said that Vināyaka should be remembered on
all auspicious and other occasions to bring out the
desired results. The Śaiva Gaṇeśa became Vaiṣṇava:<sup>4</sup> wor-
shipped in all ceremonials as preliminary, in the Bhīṣma dvā-
daśi, in grahabali;<sup>5</sup> when Umā made out of earth an elephant-
faced doll and threw it in the Ganges, it became a huge
figure, adopted as son both by Umā and Gangā; came to be
known as Gāngeya and Gajānana; invested with overlord-
ship of all Vināyakas.<sup>6</sup> Icon of, with mūṣika mount Ṛddhi
and Buddhi on either side; worship of;<sup>7</sup> elephant shaped.<sup>8</sup>
