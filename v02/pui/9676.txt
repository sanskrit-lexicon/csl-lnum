{%Bhīṣma%}¦ — son of Śantanu; Grandsire of the Pāṇḍavas
and Gangā. The best of Bharatas, the foremost of the
Vasus, and versed in dharma: Learned, self-controlled, and
devoted to Hari. His prowess was praised even by Paraśu-
rāma. Leader of a thousand legions, called Sahasrāṇī;<sup>1</sup>
Informed by Uddhava of Balarāma's visit to Hastināpura:
was invited for the Rājasūya of Yudhiṣṭhira;<sup>2</sup> went to
Syamantapañcaka for the solar eclipse and there met Kṛṣṇa
and the Vṛṣṇis. Left it for his home.<sup>3</sup>
<div n="P"/>Joined Duryodhana's army and was commander for
ten days when he was mortally wounded; while on his death-
bed he welcomed the sages and the royal guests, who visit-
ed him;<sup>4</sup> told the Pāṇḍavas that they were safe under the
guidance of Kṛṣṇa;<sup>5</sup> Yudhiṣṭhira addressed B. lying on a
bed of arrows on the various aspects of dharma and
especially {%mokṣa dharma.%} The discourse was over when

fixed on Kṛṣṇa Vāsudeva. His praise of Kṛṣṇa;<sup>6</sup> was
