{%Brāhmaṇas%} (I)¦ — came out from the face of the Puruṣa;
Brahmavādins; a portion of Hari's body;<sup>1</sup> considered to be
equal to Gods, spoken so by Viṣṇu; views of Kṛṣṇa on; none
equal to their greatness;<sup>2</sup> an embodiment of the Vedas and
deserving of veneration and worship; the mouth of Hari as
embodying all the Vedas; disregard to them would result in
the defacement of the Vedic teachings and Vedic
Gods;<sup>3</sup> characteristics of; by birth a Brāhmaṇa is a
guru; to live by studying the Vedas; some devoted
[Page2-520+ 52]
to {%karma,%} some to {%tapas,%} some to Vedic studies,
some to teaching and others to {%jñāna%} and {%yoga;%}<sup>4</sup>
Kṛṣṇa on the need for keeping them contented;<sup>5</sup> Kṛṣṇa
on their property; however small it should not be
touched, but should be considered poison since its enjoy-
ment leads to shortness of life and hell;<sup>6</sup> their words always
become true;<sup>7</sup> showed to Nābhi Yajñeśa himself;<sup>8</sup> helped
Ṛṣabha in his administration;<sup>9</sup> Kamsa decided to kill all of
them and ordered his men to that effect.<sup>10</sup> Prohibited from
agriculture, trade, tending of cattle and from selling the
Vedas; from taking liquor but may take meat in times of
danger.<sup>11</sup> in Kali, in the service of Śūdras and Mlecchas and
become Śivasūlas;<sup>12</sup> adopt Śūdra practices.<sup>13</sup>
