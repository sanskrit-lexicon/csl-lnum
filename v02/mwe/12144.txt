FROM¦, {%prep.%} In phrases where it denotes procession, deriva-
-tion, deliverance, extraction, separation, distance, emis-
-sion, removal, the reason or motive of any act, and the
ground or cause of any thing, it is usually expressed by
the abl. case. It is also expressed in some phrases by
such words as {#sakASAt, maDyAt, praBfti, AraBya,#} or even by the
prep. {#A#} with the abl. c. The following are examples;
‘coming out from his house,’ {#niHmftya gfhAt;#} ‘from avarice
anger arises,’ {#loBAt kroDaH praBavati;#} ‘even from poison nectar
may be taken,’ {#vizAd apy amftaM grAhyaM;#} ‘he delivers me
from danger,’ {#trAyate mAM BayAt;#} ‘Krishna is a hundred kos
from Somanāth,’ {#kfzRaH SataM kroSAH somanATAt;#} ‘he separates
the good from the bad,’ {#sADUn duzweByaH pfTakkaroti;#} ‘having
discharged an arrow from the bow,’ {#vARaM DanuzaH prakzipya;#}
‘he rises from his seat,’ {#AsanAd uttizWati;#} ‘from fear of
punishment,’ {#daRqaBayAt;#} ‘from unsteadiness of mind,’
{#anavasTitacittatvAt;#} ‘from being founded on observation,’
{#pratyakzamUlatvAt#}. The abl. c. is also used to express ‘from,’
in the sense of {%since;%} as, ‘from childhood,’ {#bAlyAt#} or
{#bAlyataH;#} ‘from the earliest period of the world,’ {#jagadAramBa-#}
{#-kAlAt,#} but in these cases {#praBfti#} or {#AraBya#} or {#avaDi#} are often
added, or {#A#} may sometimes, but very rarely, be prefixed;
as, ‘from childhood,’ {#bAlyAt praBfti, AbAlyAt, AbAlyakAlAt;#}
‘from birth,’ {#janmataH praBfti, janmata AraBya, AjanmataH, janmAvaDi;#}
‘from the very first glance,’ {#praTamadarSanakzaRAt praBfti#}. The
word {#sakASAt#} is used in such phrases as, ‘from the
presence of the king,’ {#rAjasakASAt;#} ‘an order from the
council,’ {#saBAsakASAd AjYA#}. The word {#maDyAt#} in such
phrases as, ‘from among the soldiers,’ {#sEnyamaDyAt#}. Other
