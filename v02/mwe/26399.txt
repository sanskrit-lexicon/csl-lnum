{#ftuvargaH;#} ‘the six seasons,’ {#zaqftavaH#} {%m. pl.%} The year is di-
vided into six seasons, according to the Hindūs, viz.
1. Spring, {#vasantaH, vasantasamayaH,#} comprising, according to the
reckoning which refers to the Northern parts of India,
the two months of {#cEtraH#} and {#vESAKaH,#} {%i. e.%} from the middle
of March to the middle of May, {%see%} SPRING. 2. The hot
season or Summer, {#grIzmaH, grIzmakAlaH, nidAGakAlaH,#} compris-
ing the two months of {#jyezwaH#} and {#AzAQaH,#} {%i. e.%} from the
middle of May to the middle of July, {%see%} SUMMER. 3. The
rainy season, the rains, {#varzAH#} {%m. pl.,%} {#varzAkAlaH, vfzwikAlaH,#} com-
prising the two months {#SrAvaRaH#} and {#BAdraH,#} {%i. e.%} from the
middle of July to the middle of September, {%see%} under
RAIN, RAINY. 4. The autumnal season, {#Sarad, SaratkAlaH,#}
{#SaradftuH#} {%m.,%} comprising the two months {#ASvinaH#} and {#kArttikaH,#}
{%i. e.%} from the middle of September to the middle of No-
vember, {%see%} AUTUMN. 5. The cold season, {#hemantaH,#} comprising
the two months {#mArgaSIrzaH#} and {#pOzaH,#} {%i. e.%} from the middle of
November to the middle of January. 6. The dewy season
or season of hoar frost, {#SiSiraH, SiSirakAlaH,#} comprising the

two months {#mAGaH#} and {#PAlgunaH,#} {%i. e.%} from the middle of
January to the middle of March. According to more
modern authorities the season of Spring begins with the
month Phālguna, or the middle of February. This
reckoning will place each of the succeeding seasons a
month earlier. Sometimes the word {#AgamaH#} is used in
composition for ‘the coming on of a season,’ or for the
season itself, as ‘the rainy season,’ {#varzAgamaH, meGAgamaH;#} ‘the
