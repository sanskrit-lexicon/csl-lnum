BANDHUDĀYĀDA¦. A son who can claim to be a heir.
The Purāṇas state about six different kinds of Bandhu-
dāyādas.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Svayaṃjāta.%} A son born to one's wife without a
progenitor.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Praṇīta.%} A son born to one's wife by the blessing
of any holy man.
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Putrikāputra.%} Son of one's daughter.
<div n="NI"/>4) {%Paunarbhava.%} A son born after a re-marriage.
<div n="NI"/>5) {%Kānīna.%} A son born before marriage.
<div n="NI"/>6) {%Bhāñja.%} Son of one's sister. All these sons are
heirs. (Chapter 119, Adi Parva, M.B.).
