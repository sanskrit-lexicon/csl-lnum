MALAYA IV¦. A mountain in South India. The following
pieces of information are gathered about this mountain
from the Purāṇas:—
<div n="NI"/>(1) The sovereign deity of this mountain attends on
Kubera in Kubera's assembly. (M.B. Sabhā Parva,
Chapter 10, Verse 32)
<div n="NI"/>(2) The Pāṇḍya and Cola Kings collected Sandal Paste
essence from the Malaya and Dardura mountains, filled
them in golden pots and presented them to Yudhiṣṭhira.
(M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 52, Verse 33).
<div n="NI"/>(3) The monkeys who went in search of Sītā crossed
this mountain. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 289, Verse
44).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Malaya is considered as one of the seven chief
mountains of India. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 9,
Verse 11)
<div n="NI"/>(5) Once Mṛtyu deity performed tapas on this moun-
tain. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 54, Verse 26).
<div n="NI"/>(6) In Tripuradahana, Śiva used this mountain as the
flagstaff of his chariot. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter
102, Verse 73).
