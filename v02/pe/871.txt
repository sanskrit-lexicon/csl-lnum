BĀHYĀŚVAN¦. A king of the Pūru dynasty. He was the
son of King Purujāti. Five sons were born to Bāhyāś-
van called Mukula, Sṛñjaya, Bṛhadiṣṭha, Yavīnara and
Kṛmila. These five sons became famous as Pāñcālas.
Of these, the family of Mukula became the Mauku-
lyas, who were having temples and were of two classes.
A son named Pañcāśva was born to Mukula. A son
named Divodāsa and a daughter named Ahalyā were
born to Pañcāśva. Ahalyā got with child from the
hermit Śāradvata and gave birth to a son named Śatā-
nanda. (Agni Purāṇa, Chapter 278).
