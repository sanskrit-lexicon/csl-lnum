PŪRVADIŚĀ¦ (PŪRVADIK). The East.
<div n="NI"/>Of all the sides Pūrvadiśā is the most prominent.
<div n="NI"/>1) The Sun rises in the east.
<div n="NI"/>2) The sādhyas come and do severe penance in the east
at dusk.
<div n="NI"/>3) The moon rises here.
<div n="NI"/>4) The result of offering havya to the fire spreads to
the east.
<div n="NI"/>5) The daughters of Dakṣa after becoming the wives of
Kaśyapa delivered in the east.
<div n="NI"/>6) Devas made Indra the king of the east.
<div n="NI"/>7) Indra and the devas did penance jointly in the east.
<div n="NI"/>8) Because the devas accepted this side first (pūrva)
the side got the name Pūrvā.
<div n="NI"/>9) The sun gives away as gifts the yajur Mantras at
this place.
<div n="NI"/>(10) The devas drink the soma juice facing the east
<div n="NI"/>11) Varuṇa once entered Pātāla by the east and attain-
ed prosperity.
<div n="NI"/>12) Indra selected the sacrificial animals at this place.
<div n="NI"/>13) The soul of man comes to Svarga and the earth
through the east. (Chapter 108 Udyoga Parva).
