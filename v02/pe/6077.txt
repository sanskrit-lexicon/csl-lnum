ŚAMĪKA I¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} A muni. He remained with herds of cattle
and performed tapas feeding himself on the foam from
the mouth of calves drinking their mother's milk. He was
the father of Śṛṅgī, who cursed king Parīkṣit, who once
threw a dead snake on the neck of Śamīka. The curse
was that he would die within seven days of the incident
by snake-bite. For details see under Parīkṣit, Para 3).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other information.%}
<div n="NI"/>i) Śamīka worships Indra in his court. (Sabhā Parva,
Chapter 7, Verse 16).
<div n="NI"/>ii) Śamīka too was present on the occasion when Vyāsa
called up and showed Janamejaya the souls of dead
kings. (Aśvamedhika Parva, Chapter 35, Verse 8).
