ALAMBUṢA I¦. Son of the giant Ṛṣyaśṛṅga. He had
fought on the side of the Kauravas. (M.B., Udyoga Parva,
Chapter 167, Stanza 33). There was a combat between
Alambuṣa and Ghaṭotkaca, on the first day of the battle
of Kurukṣetra.
<div n="NI"/>“Alambuṣa shot ninety sharpened pointed arrows at the
son of Bhīmasena (Ghaṭotkaca), cut his body in several
places. Though he was full of wounds, he fought all the
more fiercely”. (Bhāṣā Bhārata, Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter
45, Stanzas 43, 44).
[Page024-b+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>Alambuṣa had engaged Abhimanyu, Sātyaki, Ghaṭot-
kaca, Kuntibhoja and Bhīmasena all mighty men of
arms, in single combat, in the Kaurava battle. It is seen
that he has got another name, Sālakaṭaṃka. He was
killed by Ghaṭotkaca in the battle. (M.B. Droṇa
Parva, Chapter 109, Stanzas 22 to 33).
