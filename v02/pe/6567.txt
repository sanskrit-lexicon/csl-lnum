ŚIBI II¦. Inhabitants of the country called Śibi; they are
called Śaibyas also. The following information about
the country is gathered from Mahābhārata.
<div n="NI"/>(i) Sunandā, mother of King Śantanu was a princess
of this country. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 95, Verse
44).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Govāsana, father-in-law of Yudhiṣṭhira, was a
King of Śibi. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 95, Verse 76).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Nakula, during his triumphal tour of the western
regions, conquered the kingdom of Śibi. (M.B. Sabhā
Parva, Chapter 7).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) People of Śibi attended the Rājasūya yajña
conducted by Yudhiṣṭhira with presents. (Sabhā Parva,
Chapter 52, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>(v) A King called Uśīnara, once ruled the country.
(Vana Parva, Chapter 131, Verse 21).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) The kingdom of Śibi was once under the jurisdic-
tion of Jayadratha. (Vana Parva, Chapter 267, Verse
11).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Arjuna annihilated the armies of the kingdom of
Śibi which followed Jayadratha to the battlefield.
(Vana Parva, Chapter 271, Verse 28).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) The mahārathas of Śibi, with their armies,
arrayed under the banner of Duryodhana to fight the
Pāṇḍavas. (Udyoga Parva, Chapter 195, Verse 7).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) Karṇa once subjugated the kingdom of Śibi.
(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 91, Verse 38).
[Page715-b+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>(x) The people of the country in earlier days were
illiterate and ignorant. (Karṇa Parva, Chapter 45,
Verse 34).
