ŚUKA VII¦. A Maharṣi who was the contemporary of
Aṇuha of Dakṣiṇa Pāñcāla and of king Brahmadatta.
This sage lived before the time of the other Śuka who
was the son of Vyāsa.
<div n="NI"/>This sage Śuka had six sons, named Bhūriśravas,
Śambhu, Prabhu, Kṛṣṇa, Saura (Sauraprabha) and
Devaśruta by his two wives Pīvarī and Ekaśṛṅgā.
(Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa, 3-8-93: Vāyu Purāṇa, 70-84;
Devī Bhāgavata, 1-14; Nārada. 1-58).
