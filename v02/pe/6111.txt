SAṂVARTA¦. Son of Aṅgiras.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} Aṅgiras had eight sons called Bṛhaspati,
Utathya, Saṃvarta, Payasya, Śānti, Ghora, Virūpa and
Sudhanvā. (Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 85, Verse 30).
Saṃvarta was the third among the sons and he lived on
inimical terms with his eldest brother Bṛhaspati. When
once Bṛhaspati forsook king Marutta it was Saṃvarta,
who managed for the king his yajña. (Śānti Parva,
Chapter 29, Verse 20).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other information.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Saṃvarta is a member of Indra's court. (Sabhā
Parva, Chapter 7, Verse 19).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) He lives in Brahmā's court and worships him.
(Sabhā Parva, Chapter 11, Verse 12).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) He once got conducted at Plakṣāvataraṇa tīrtha
for King Marutta a yajña. (Vana Parva, Chapter 129,
Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Saṃvarta and Bṛhaspati disliked each other.
(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 55, Verse 38; Śānti Parva,
Chapter 29, Verse 29).
<div n="NI"/>(v) He was one of those who visited Bhīṣma on his bed
of arrows. (Śānti Parva, Chapter 47, Verse 9).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) It was he, who recited the praises of Śiva to king
Marutta so that the latter got gold. (M.B. Southern text,
Aśvamedha Parva, Chapter 8).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Once he paralysed Indra's Vajrāyudha. (Aśva-
medha Parva, Chapter 10, Verse 17).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) It was he, who invited and got down Indra to the
yajña conducted by Marutta. (Aśvamedha Parva,
Chapter 10, Verse 25).
