CAṆḌAKAUŚIKA¦. A muni, son of Kakśivān, the son of
Gautama. Bṛhadratha, King of Magadha, was having no
issues when, one day, Caṇḍakauśika went to the palace
and blessed the King as a result of which a son called
Jarāsandha was born to him. Caṇḍakauśika continued to
live at Magadha for long as the preceptor of the kings of
that land. (See Jarāsandha).
