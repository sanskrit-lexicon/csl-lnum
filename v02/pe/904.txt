BALĀKṢA¦. An ancient king of Bhārata. While the
Pāṇḍavas were living incognito in the kingdom of Virāṭa,
Duryodhana and his brothers stole the cows of Virāṭa,
in consequence of which there was a battle. The devas
(gods) came in planes to see the fight between Arjuna
and the teacher Kṛpa. It is mentioned in the Mahā-
[Page102-b+ 64]
bhārata, Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 56, that the King Balākṣa
was there with the gods when they came to see the fight.
