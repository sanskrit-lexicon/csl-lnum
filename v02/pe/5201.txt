PIŚĀCA I¦. (Devil, Satan)
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Origin.%} A malevolent being which is the very
manifestation of evil. Everybody, everywhere in the
world, from the very birth of this universe believed in
the presence of such a wicked soul. According to Hindu
Purāṇas, Piśāca is a creation of Brahmā. In the begin-
ning Brahmā created the eighteen prajāpatis headed by
Dakṣa, the Yakṣas, the Gandharva and the Piśācas.
(Chapter 1, Ādi Parva). This wicked being is called
in English a ‘Devil’. This word is derived from the
Greek word ‘diabolos’. People of the West and East
equally believe that Piśāca (Satan) is an enemy of men
and gods alike.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Bible and the Piśāca.%} It is not clearly stated in Bible
how Satan was born but it is being referred to at several
places as a wicked soul which leads men to evil.
<div n="NI"/>3) {%The Bhāratīya Saṅkalpa.%} Pisāca, the creation of
Brahmā, has taken important roles in the Purāṇic
stories. Though Piśāca is the instigator of all evils its
manifestation appears in many contexts in the Purāṇas:—
<div n="NI"/>(i) Piśāca lives in the court of Kubera and worships
him. (Śloka 16, Chapter 10, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Piśāca lives in the court of Brahmā and worships
him. (Śloka 49, Chapter 11, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Piśāca lives in the Gokarṇatīrtha and worships
Śiva. (Śloka 25, Chapter 85. Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Piśāca is the head of all evil spirits. Marīci and
sages like him have created many evil spirits. (Śloka 46,
Chapter 272, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(v) The bhūtas (evil spirits) made Rāvaṇa their king.
(Śloka 88, Chapter 275, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) The food of Piśāca is flesh and its drink, blood.
(Śloka 9, Chapter 50, Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) In the battle af Bhārata, the horses attached to
the chariot of Alambuṣa were Piśācas. (Śloka 38,
Chapter 167, Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) The Piśācas fought Karṇa acting as helpers to
Ghaṭotkaca. (Śloka 109, Chapter 175, Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) Arjuna conquered the Piśācas at the time of
[Page590-b+ 65]
Khāṇḍavadāha. (Śloka 37, Chapter, 37; Karṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(x) The Piśācas were present during the fight of
Arjuna with Karṇa (Śloka 50, Chapter 87, Karṇa
Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(xi) The Piśācas worship Pārvatī and Parameśvara
doing penance on the top of the mountain Muñjavān.
(Śloka 5, Chapter 8, Āśvamedhika Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(xii) During the time of Mahābhārata many Piśācas
incarnated as kings. (Śloka 6, Chapter 31, Āśramvāsika
Parva).
