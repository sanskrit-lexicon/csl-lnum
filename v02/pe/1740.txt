<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} Danu, the daughter of Dakṣa
was married to Kaśyapa Prajāpati. It is mentioned in
Mahābhārata that the Dānavas (demons) were born
from Danu.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Sons.%} One hundred sons were born to Danu. The
following are the important among them.
<div n="NI"/>1. Vipracitti 3. Namuci
<div n="NI"/>2. Śibara 4. Pulomā

<div n="NI"/>5. Asilomā 20. Aśvagrīvan
<div n="NI"/>6. Keśi 21. Sūkṣma
<div n="NI"/>7. Durjaya 22. Tuhuṇḍa
<div n="NI"/>8. Ayaśśiras 23. Ekapād
<div n="NI"/>9. Aśvaśiras 24. Ekacakra
<div n="NI"/>10. Aśvaśaṅku 25. Virūpākṣa
<div n="NI"/>11. Garga 26. Harihara
<div n="NI"/>12. Amūrdhā 27. Nicandra
<div n="NI"/>13. Vegavān 28. Nikumbha
<div n="NI"/>14. Ketumān 29. Kapaṭa
<div n="NI"/>15. Svarbhānu 30. Śarabha
<div n="NI"/>16. Aśva 31. Śalabha
<div n="NI"/>17. Aśvapati 32. Sūrya
<div n="NI"/>18. Vṛṣaparvan 33. Candra.
<div n="NI"/>19. Ajaka
<div n="NI"/>(This sun and the moon (Sūrya and Candra) are not

<div n="NI"/>From the sons named above ten families of Dānavas
(asuras) arose. The founders of the families are mention-
ed below:
<div n="NI"/>1. Ekākṣa 6. Tapana
<div n="NI"/>2. Amṛtapa 7. Śara
<div n="NI"/>3. Pralamba 8. Mahāhanu
<div n="NI"/>4. Naraka 9. Garviṣṭha
<div n="NI"/>5. Vātāpi 10. Dīrghajihva
<div n="NI"/>All the dānavas or Asuras belong to one of these ten
families. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 65).
