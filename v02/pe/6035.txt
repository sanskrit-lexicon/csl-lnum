SAMANTAPAÑCAKA¦. A holy tīrtha (bath) founded
by Paraśurāma.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} See under Paraśurāma, Para 7.
[Page677-a+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Paraśurāma made here five rivers through which
blood flowed. There, with hands dipped in blood, he
offered oblations to the manes. (M.B. Ādi Parva,
Chapter 2, Verse 4).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) By the blessings of the ancestors of Paraśurāma
this place became a holy bath. (M.B. Ādi Parva,
Chapter 2, Verse 8).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) At the ending and the beginning periods of the
two yugas Dvāpara and Kali, the Kauravas and the
Pāṇḍavas fought the great battle at this place. Because
of that this place got the name Samantapañcaka.
(M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 2, Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) It was according to the advice of Balabhadrarāma
that this place was selected as the battlefield. (Śalya
Parva, Chapter 55, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Duryodhana was killed at Samantapañcaka.
(Śalya Parva, Chapter 89, Verse 40).
