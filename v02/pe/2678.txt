HIMAVĀN¦. (THE HIMĀLAYAS).
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} The great mountain on the northern
borders of India. In the literature and the religious
thought of India the Himālayas occupy a position of
universal respect and adulation. The Indian belief is
that the mountain has got a divine soul. (e.g. it is
referred to as “devatātmā” in Kālidāsa's Kumārasaṃ-
bhava). The Himālayas are referred to very often in
the Purāṇas and epics.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other information from Mahābhārata.%} (1) The
Bālakhilyas had performed tapas on the Himālayas.
(Ādi Parva, Chapter 30, Verse 3).
<div n="NI"/>(2) The Nāga named Śeṣa once resided there to
practise control of the mind in solitude. (Ādi Parva,
Chapter 36, Verse 3).
<div n="NI"/>(3) Vyāsa performed tapas there. (Ādi Parva, Chapter
114, Verse 24).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Pāṇḍu, father of the Pāṇḍavas had to cross the
Kālakūṭa mountain and the Himālayas to reach
Gandhamādana. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 148, Verse 48).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Bhārgava women hid themselves in the Himālayas
at the time when the Kṣatriyas were hunting out people
of the Bhṛgu dynasty. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 177, Verse
20).
<div n="NI"/>(6) The whole fire of the yajña conducted by sage Parāś,
ara to annihilate the Rākṣasas was deposited in the
extensive forest near the Himālayas, (Ādi Parva-
Chapter 180, Verse 22).
[Page313-a+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>(7) Arjuna once sojourned on the Himālayas. (Ādi
Parva, Chapter 214, Verse 1).
<div n="NI"/>(8) The Devatā of the mountain worships Kubera in
his assembly. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter 10, Verse 31).
<div n="NI"/>(9) As suggested by Sūrya, once Nārada did penance
for 1000 years on the peaks of the Himālayas so that
he might see Indra's assembly. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter
11, Verse 8).
<div n="NI"/>(10) Arjuna once crossed the Himālayas and encamped
at Dhavalagiri. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter 27, Verse 29).
<div n="NI"/>(11) Bhīmasena once stopped for a short time near
the Himālayas imagining himself to be emperor of the
whole world. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter 30, Verse 4).
<div n="NI"/>(12) It was at the Himālayas that Merusāvarṇi impart-
ed advice to Yudhiṣṭhira. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter 78,
Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>(13) Bhagīratha performed tapas on the Himālayas.
(Vana Parva, Chapter 108, Verse 3).
<div n="NI"/>(14) The kingdom of Subāhu, King of Kalinda was on
the plains of the Himālayas. The Pāṇḍavas stopped
there for a night and left for the Himālayas the next
day. (Vana Parva, Chapter 140, Verse 24).
<div n="NI"/>(15) The Pāṇḍavas, on the seventeenth day of their
life in exile in the forest came to the top of the
Himālayas, and visited the āśrama of Vṛṣaparvan there.
(Vana Paṛva, Chapter 18).
<div n="NI"/>(16) It was while Bhīmasena was hunting in the
Hiṃālayas and appreciating the beauties there that
a python caught him by the leg. (Vana Parva, Chapter
178).
<div n="NI"/>(17) Sage Mārkaṇḍeya once saw in the stomach of
child Kṛṣṇa mountains like the Himālayas, Hemakūṭa
etc. (Vana Parva, Chapter 188, Verse 112).
<div n="NI"/>(18) Much anterior to sage Mārkaṇḍeya an owl called
Prāvārakarṇa had lived on the Himālayas. (Vana
Parva, Chapter 199, Verse 4).
<div n="NI"/>(19) Karṇa conquered all the kingdoms on the
Himālayas and collected taxes from them all. (Vana
Parva, Chapter 254, Verse 4).
<div n="NI"/>(20) On the northern heights of the Himālayas live
Śiva and Pārvatī for ever. (Udyoga Parva, Chapter
115, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>(21) Himavān is one of the six rain-producing
mountains stretching from the east to the west. (Bhīṣma
Parva, Chapter 6, Verse 3).
<div n="NI"/>(22) Arjuna during his tour of Kailāsa in dream with
Śrī Kṛṣṇa saw all the peaks of Himavān. (Droṇa Parva,
Chapter 80, Verse 23).
<div n="NI"/>(23) When Śiva burnt to ashes the Tripuras, Himavān
and Vindhya served as the axles of Śiva's chariot.
(Karṇa Parva, Chapter 34, Verse 22).
<div n="NI"/>(24) The River Gaṅgā deposited Śiva's semen on a
high peak of Himavān and Subrahmaṇya was born from
it. (Karṇa Parva, Chapter 44, Verse 9).
<div n="NI"/>(25) Himavān, the Devatā of the mountain, also was
present at the installation of Subrahmaṇya as chief of
the army. (Śalya Parva, Chapter 45, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>(26) Himavān presented to Subrahmaṇya two atten-
dants called Suvarcas and Ativarcas. (Śalya Parva,
Chapter 45, Verse 46).
<div n="NI"/>(27) Śrī Kṛṣṇa once performed tapas on Himavān,
and as a result of it was born his son Pradyumna from
Rukmiṇī. (Sauptika Parva, Chapter 12, Verse 30).
<div n="NI"/>(28) Himavān presented inexhaustible wealth to empe-
ror Pṛthu. (Śānti Parva, Chapter 59, Verse 113).
[Page313-b+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>(29) The peaks of Himavān have an area of 100
yojanas; Brahmā once conducted a yajña there. (Śānti
Parva, Chapter 166, Verse 32).
<div n="NI"/>(30) Dakṣaprajāpati once conducted a yajña at the
place called Gaṅgādvāra on the slopes of Himavān.
(Śānti Parva, Chapter 284, Verse 3).
<div n="NI"/>(31) In Chapter 327 of the Śānti Parva the following
statement occurs about Himavān. According to the
advice of King Janaka Śuka brahmarṣi mounted the
Himālayas. Siddha-cāraṇas lived on the mountain.
Celestial women walked all over the place. The moun-
tain always reverberated with the noise of different
varieties of living beings. The noise produced by Kin-
naras, peacocks and many other birds could always be
heard there. Himavān was the permanent abode of
Garuḍa. The Aṣṭadikpālakas also lived there.
<div n="NI"/>(32) When Śuka moved up to the world above it
appeared as though the Himavān was being cut open.
He saw two divine peaks of mountains on the two
sides of the path; one of them was the peak of the
Mahāmeru and the other that of Himavān. Both the
peaks gave way to Śuka. (Śānti Parva, Chapter 333).
<div n="NI"/>(33) Śiva desired to secure Umā, the daughter of
Himavān as his wife. Meantime sage Bhṛgu demanded
that Umā be wedded to him. When Himavān told the
sage that it had already been decided to gave Umā in
marriage to Śiva, the Sage cursed that there would not
be, in future, gems in the Himavān. (Śānti Parva,
Chapter 342, Verse 62).
<div n="NI"/>(34) Viṣṇu and Śiva once fought with each other on
the Himavān, and then it seemed as though the moun-
tain was being cleft into pieces. (Śānti Parva, Chapter
342, Verse 122).
<div n="NI"/>(35) Nārada had his āśrama there, on the Himavān.
(Śānti Parva, Chapter 346, Verse 3).
<div n="NI"/>(36) Śrī Kṛṣṇa once visited the āśrama of Upamanyu
on the Himavān. (Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 14, Verse
43).
<div n="NI"/>(37) King Marutta performed a yajña on the Himāla-
yas and brahmins went away leaving a lot of wealth
there. (Āśvamedhika Parva, Chapter 3, Verse 20).
<div n="NI"/>(38) Sañjaya left for the Himālayas after Dhṛtarāṣṭra
and Gāndhārī were burnt to death in a wild fire. (Āśra-
mavāsika Parva, Chapter 37, Verse 33).
<div n="NI"/>(39) During their mahāprasthāna (great journey) the
Pāṇḍavas travelled by the Himālayas and Dharmaputra
ascended to heaven from the top of the Himālayan peak.
(Mahāprāsthānika Parva, Chapter 2, Verse 1).
