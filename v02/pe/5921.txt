ŚAIBYA II¦. King of the Śibi land. Mahābhārata gives
the following pieces of information about him:—
<div n="NI"/>(i) Govāsa, King of Śibi land was the father-in-law of
Yudhiṣṭhira. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 95, Verse 76).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Śaibya adorned Yudhiṣṭhira's assembly. (M.B.
Sabhā Parva, Chapter 4, Verse 25).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Śaibya and the King of Kāśī had come to Upa-
plavya city with an “Akṣauhiṇī” (division of the army)
to attend the marriage of Abhimanyu. (M.B. Virāṭa
Parva, Chapter 72, Verse 16).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Duryodhana admitted that Śaibya was the greatest
archer in the army of the Pāṇḍavas. (M.B. Bhīṣma
Parva, Chapter 20, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>(v) During the Bhārata Yuddha, Śaibya and the King
of Kāśī were standing to protect Dhṛṣṭadyumna's
“Krauñcavyūha”. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 50,
Verse 56).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) This Śaibya was the grandson of Uśīnara. (M.B.
Droṇa Parva, Chapter 10, Verse 64).
