ŚĀRṄGA¦. Śrī Kṛṣṇa's bow. The following facts about
the bow are gathered from the Mahābhārata.
<div n="NI"/>(i) When Kṛṣṇa exhibited his Viśvarūpa (cosmic form)
in the assembly of the Kauravas he had held in one
hand this bow. (Udyoga Parva, Chapter 131, Verse 10).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Kṛṣṇa's Śārṅga is on a par with Indra's bow called
Vijaya. (Udyoga Parva, Chapter 158, Verse 4).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Śārṅga is one of the three divine bows. (Udyoga
Parva, Chapter 158, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Śārṅga was made by Brahmā and presented to
Kṛṣṇa. (M.B. Southern text, Anuśāsana Parva, Chap-
ter 141).
