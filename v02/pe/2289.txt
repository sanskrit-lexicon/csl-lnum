ELU¦. This word in Malayālam means ‘Seven’.
<div n="NI"/>(1) The seven qualities which would enhance prosperity
are the following: Dhairya (courage), Śama (forbea-
rance), Dama (control of the sense), Śucitva (cleanli-
ness), Dayā (compassion), Mṛduvāk (geniality of
speech) and Ahiṃsā (not giving injury to your friends).
<div n="NI"/>(2) The seven divisions of Kerala are Venāṭ, Pūlināṭ,
Karkanāṭ, Cītanāṭ, Kuṭṭanāṭ, Kuṭanāṭ and Malaya-
manāṭ.
<div n="NI"/>(3) The seven Cittaśalyas are Daylight Candra, Woman
past the prime of life, a pond without lotuses, a lovely
person without learning, a lord greedy of wealth, a
virtuous but poor man and a cruel royal attendant.
<div n="NI"/>(4) Seven Karmas: Bravery, skill, courage, resource-
fulness, non-withdrawal from war, magnanimity and
commanding power.
<div n="NI"/>(5) Seven solemnities: Māmmodīsa, Mūron, Kurbāna,
Kumbasāra, Vivāha, Paṭṭatva and Tailābhiṣeka of the
sick.
<div n="NI"/>(6) Seven Cirañjīvis (People who never die): Aśvat-
thāmā, Bali, Vyāsa, Hanūmān, Vibhīṣaṇa, Kṛpa and
Paraśurāma.
<div n="NI"/>(7) Jīvāvasthās: Ajñāna, Āvaraṇa, Vikṣepa, Parokṣa-
jñāna, Aparokṣajñāna, Anarthanivṛtti and Ānanda-
prāpti.
<div n="NI"/>(8) Seven islands: Jambudvīpa, Plakṣadvīpa, Puṣkara-
dvīpa, Krauñcadvīpa, Śākadvīpa, Śālmaladvīpa, and
Kuśadvīpa.
<div n="NI"/>(9) Seven Dhātus: Rasa, Rakta, Māṃsa, Medas,
Asthi, Majjā and Śukla.
<div n="NI"/>(10) Seven rivers: Gaṅgā, Yamunā, Godāvarī, Saras-
vatī, Narmadā, Sindhu and Kāverī.
<div n="NI"/>(11) Seven Nāḍīs: Iḍā, Piṅgalā, Suṣumnā, Muṣā;
Alaṃbuṣā, Asthijihvā, Gāndhārī.
<div n="NI"/>(12) Seven wives of Vasudeva: Devakī, Śrutadevā,
Yaśodā, Śrutiśravā, Śrīdevā, Upadevā and Surūpā.
<div n="NI"/>(13) Seven mountains: Himavān, Niṣadha, Vindhya,
Mālyavān, Pāriyātra, Gandhamādana and Hemakūṭa.
<div n="NI"/>(14) Seven Pātālas: Atala, Vitala, Sutala, Nitala, Ṭalā-
tala, Rasātala, and Mahātala.
<div n="NI"/>(15) Seven holy places: Ayodhyā, Mathurā, Māyā,
Kāśī, Kāñcī, Avantī and Dvāravatī.

<div n="NI"/>(16) Seven Maruts: Āvaha, Saṃvaha, Pravaha, Ud-
vaha, Vivaha, Parivaha and Parāvaha.
<div n="NI"/>(17) Seven Vargas: King, Minister, Country, fort,
treasury, army and strength of friends and relatives.
<div n="NI"/>(18) Seven colours: White, black, red, green, yellow,
blue and violet.
<div n="NI"/>(19) Seven forests: Kāmyaka, Aditi, Vyāsa, Phalkī,
Sūrya, Madhu and Śītā.
<div n="NI"/>(20) Seven doṣas (defects): Laziness, arrogance, igno-
rance, profligacy, Hauteur, pride and selfishness.
<div n="NI"/>(21) Seven vices: Hunting, dice, womanising, drinking
squandering, harshness of speech and cruelty.
<div n="NI"/>(22) Seven seas: Lavaṇa, Ikṣu, Surā, Sarpiṣ, Dadhi,
Kṣīra and Śuddhodaka.
<div n="NI"/>(23) Seven notes: Ṣadja, Ṛṣabha, Gāndhāra, Madh-
yama, Pañcama, Dhaivata and Niṣāda.
