MAGHĀ II¦. One of the twentyseven constellations. The
importance of this constellation is mentioned in the
Mahābhārata in several places.
<div n="NI"/>(i) When the planet Kuja (Mars), during its retrograde
motion, comes near the constellation of Maghā many
unhappy incidents occur in the world. (Śloka 14,
Chapter 3, Bhīṣma Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) If Candra stays near Maghā it is an ill omen. (Śloka
2, Chapter 17, Bhīṣma Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) If one gives away land in charity on the day of
Maghā one will be rich in children and cattle. (Śloka
12, Chapter 64, Anuśāsana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) If one gives pudding to the poor on the day of
Maghā which comes in the black half of the month
of Tulā (October) the Manes will be pleased. (Śloka
7, Chapter 88, Anuśāsana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(v) If one worships the Manes on the day of Maghā
sitting in the shade of an elephant, the manes will be
satisfied. (Śloka 8, Chapter 88, Anuśāsana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) If one conducts the obsequial rites and acts of
charity on the day of Maghā one would become the
most excellent member of the family. (Śloka 5. Chapter
89, Anuśāsana Parva).
