SARAYŪ I¦. A river very famous in the Purāṇas. The
most important things associated with the river are
given below:
<div n="NI"/>(i) Seven tributaries of Gaṅgā originate from the
golden peaks of the Himālayas and Sarayū is one of
them. Those who bathe in the river will be absolved
from sins. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 169, Verse 20)
<div n="NI"/>(ii) The river exists in Varuṇa's court worshipping him.
(Ādi Parva, Chapter 8).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Śrī Kṛṣṇa, Arjuna and Bhīma, on their way to
Girivraja from Indraprastha crossed this river. (Sabhā
Parva, Chapter 20, Verse 28).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) It was at Gotāra (or Gopratara) in this river that
Śrī Rāma drowned himself to death and attained
Viṣṇupāda. (Vana Parva, Chapter 84, Verse 70).
<div n="NI"/>(v) This river is the source of Agni (fire). (Vana
Parva, Chapter 222, Verse 22).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Vasiṣṭha once blocked the course of Gaṅgā on its
way to Kailāsa at Mānasasarovara. But, Gaṅgā broke
the obstruction and flowed on, and Sarayū is the
stream that started from there. (Anuśāsana Parva,
Chapter 155, Verse 23).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) It is one of the rivers to be remembered both at
dawn and dusk. (Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 165, Verse
21).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) The city of Ayodhyā is situated on the banks of
Sarayū. (Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa, Bālakāṇḍa, Canto 5,
Verse 5).
