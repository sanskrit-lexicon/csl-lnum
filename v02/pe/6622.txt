SINĪVĀLĪ I¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Birth.%} A daughter of Aṅgiras. The wife of Aṅgiras
was Smṛti. She gave birth to four daughters named
Kuhū, Rākā, Anumati and Sinīvālī. (Viṣṇu Purāṇa,
Aṃśa 1, Chapter 10).
<div n="NI"/>(It is mentioned in Bhāgavata, Skandha 6, that
Sinīvālī was the third daughter of Aṅgiras by his wife
Śraddhā.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Dṛśyādṛśyā is another name of Sinīvālī. Her body
is small. So sometimes she can be seen and sometimes
she cannot be seen. It is in this meaning that she
gets the name Dṛśyādṛśyā (seen and unseen). Śiva
bears her in his forehead. So she has another name
Rudrasutā. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 218, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Śiva used Sinīvālī as the yoke of the horses of his
chariot in the battle with Tripuras. (M.B. Karṇa
Parva, Chapter 34, Verse 32).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) At the time of his birth Sinīvālī had come to see
the child Subrahmaṇya. (M.B. Śalya Parva, Chapter
45, Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) It is mentioned in Atharva Veda that Sinīvālī is a
wife of Mahāviṣṇu.
