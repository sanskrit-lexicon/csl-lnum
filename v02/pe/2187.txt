DURMUKHA II¦. One of the hundred sons of Dhṛtarā-
ṣṭra. The following information about him is gathered
from the Mahābhārata.
<div n="NI"/>(1) He was present at the Svayaṃvara of Draupadī.
(Ādi Parva, Chapter 185, Verse 1).
<div n="NI"/>(2) During the procession to Dvaitavana, Gandharvas
took him captive. (Vana Parva, Chapter 242, Verse
12).
<div n="NI"/>(3) In the great war he fought with Sahadeva. (Bhīṣma
Parva, Chapter 85, Verse 25).
[Page256-a+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>(4) He killed Arjuna's charioteer. (Bhīṣma Parva,
Chapter 47, Verse 12).
<div n="NI"/>(5) He defeated Śrutakarman. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chap-
ter 79, Verse 35).
<div n="NI"/>(6) He was defeated by Abhimanyu. (Bhīṣma Parva,
Chapter 84, Verse 42).
<div n="NI"/>(7) A duel was fought between Durmukha and
Ghaṭotkaca. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 110, Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>(8) Duel fought between Durmukha and Dhṛṣṭadyumna.
(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 20, Verse 26).
<div n="NI"/>(9) He fought with Purujit. (Droṇa Parva, Chapter
106, Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>(10) He was defeated in fight with Sahadeva. (Droṇa
Parva, Chapter 107, Verse 25).
<div n="NI"/>(11) Bhīmasena killed him in war. (Droṇa Parva, Chap-
ter 134, Verse 20).
<div n="NI"/>(12) After the war his beautiful palace was occupied
by Sahadeva. (Śānti Parva, Chapter 44, Verse 12).
