CEDI¦. A king of the Yaduvaṃśa.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} Descending in order from Viṣṇu:—Brahmā-
Atri—Candra—Budha—Purūravas—Āyus—Nahuṣa—
Yayāti—Yadu—Kroṣṭhā—Vrajīnavān—Śvāhi—
Ruśeka—Citraratha—Śaśabindu—Pṛthuśravas—
Dharma—Rucaka-Jyāmagha-Vidarbha—Romapāda—
Bhadru—Kṛta—Uśika—Cedi. The Cedi dynasty begins
from Cedi and the land ruled over by Cedi came to be
known as Cedirājya.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details regarding King Cedi.%}
<div n="NI"/>1) Once Uparicaravasu conquered his land. (Śloka 2,
Chapter 63, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>2) Kareṇumatī, wife of Nakula, was a princess of
Cedi rājya. (Śloka 79, Chapter 95, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>3) Once Śiśupāla reigned over this country. After his
death his son Dhṛṣṭaketu was crowned king. (Śloka 36,
Chapter 45, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>4) During the period of Nala, Cedirājya was ruled
by king Subāhu. Damayantī lived in his palace very
comfortably. (Śloka 44, Chapter 65, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>5) Dhṛṣṭaketu, a king of Cedi, came to the help of the
Pāṇḍavas with an akṣauhiṇī (an army of 21870 chariots,
equal number of elephants, 65610 horses and 10930
infantry men.). (Śloka 7, Chapter 19, Udyoga Parva).
<div n="NI"/>6) The Kṣatriya warriors of this state helped Śrī
Kṛṣṇa in many ways. (Śloka 11, Chapter 28, Udyoga
Parva).
<div n="NI"/>7) Cedi was counted as one of the prominent kingdoms
of ancient Bhārata. (Śloka 40, Chapter 9, Bhīṣma
Parva).
