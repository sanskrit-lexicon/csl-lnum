GANDHAMĀDANA¦(M). A mountain famed in the
Purāṇas and lying to the east of the Himālayas. (Bhā-
gavata, Pañcama Skandha). The purānic importance of
the mountain may be summarised as follows:—
<div n="NI"/>(1) Kaśyapaprajāpati did tapas at this mountain. (Ādi
Parva, Chapter 30, Verse 10).
<div n="NI"/>(2) Ananta (Ādiśeṣa) had once done tapas here. (Ādi
Parva, Chapter 36, Verse 3).
<div n="NI"/>(3) Once Pāṇḍu went to the Śataśṛṅga mountain
along with his wives Kuntī and Mādrī for doing tapas,
and on that occasion they visited Gandhamādana also.
(Ādi Parva, Chapter 118, Verse 48).
<div n="NI"/>(4) This mountain assumes the form of a divine per-
son. attends Kubera's assembly and worships him.
(Sabhā Parva, Chapter 10, Verse 32).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Śrī Kṛṣṇa in the company of Nārāyaṇarṣi spent
10,000 years on this mountain as an anchorite. (Vana
Parva, Chapter 12, Verse 11).
<div n="NI"/>(6) Arjuna on his way to Mount Kailāsa to do tapas
crossed the Gandhamādana mountain. (Vana Parva,
Chapter 37, Verse 41).
<div n="NI"/>(7) Only those who possess power got by tapas will
[Page274-b+ 65]
be able to climb this mountain. (Vana Parva, Chapter
140, Verse 22).
<div n="NI"/>(8) There is the Badari tree and under it there is an
āśrama of Naranārāyaṇas on the heights of this moun-
tain, and Yakṣas stay there always. (Vana Parva,
Chapter 141, Verse 22).
<div n="NI"/>(9) Once the Pāṇḍavas, with the aid of Ghaṭotkaca
mounted this mountain. (Vana Parva, Chapter
145).
<div n="NI"/>(10) It was here, at this mountain that Bhīma killed
Maṇimān, a friend of Kubera. (Vana Parva, Chapter
160, Verse 76).
<div n="NI"/>(11) Kubera, who was driven out of Laṅkā stayed at
this mountain during the initial periods. (Vana Parva,
Chapter 275, Verse 33).
