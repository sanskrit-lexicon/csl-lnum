CĀMARA¦(M). A rod (handle) with a large tuft of
hair, fibre or feathers at the end. A symbol used by
kings and brahmins. Cāmara of the king should have a
golden handle, and it should be made of the wings of
the swan, the pea cock, the Balākā bird etc. But, the
wings of different birds shall not be intermixed. Circu-
lar in shape, the Cāmara should have on its handle
3, 4, 5, 6, 7, or 8 sandhis (joints, knots). (Agni-
purāṇa, Chapter 245).
