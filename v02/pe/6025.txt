SAMĀ¦. A populous centre in front of the Puṣkara
island (Puṣkaradvīpa). In Purāṇic days there were
thirtythree regions in this centre. Devas such as
Vāmana, Airāvata, Supratīka, Añjana and so on dwell
[Page676-a+ 64]
here. The people of this place live by inhaling the
breath coming from the noses of these gods. (M.B.
Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 12, Verse 32).
