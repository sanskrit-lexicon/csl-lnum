KUNTIBHOJA¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} A King of the Yadu dynasty; son of the
sister of Śūrasena, who was the father of Vasudeva and
grandfather of Śrī Kṛṣṇa. (For genealogy see under
Śrī Kṛṣṇa). Kuntibhoja was also the foster-father of
Kuntī, the daughter of Śūrasena. (See Para 1, under
Kuntī 1).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other information.%} (i) Sahadeva, during his
triumphal march over the southern kingdoms subjugat-
ed Kuntibhoja. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter 31, Verse
16).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) He participated in the Rājasūya Yajña of Yudhi-
ṣṭhira. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter 34, Verse 12).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) The son of Kuntibhoja also became famous under
the same name, and Purujit was the son of this
Kuntibhoja. Both of them were uncles of the
Pāṇḍavas. (Karṇa Parva, Chapter 6, Verse 22).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) On the first day of the Kurukṣetra war Kunti-
bhoja and his sons fought with Vinda and Anuvinda.
(Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 45, Verse 72),
<div n="NI"/>(v) It was Kuntibhoja who occupied the netrasthāna
(eye-position) of the Krauñcavyūha set up by
Dhṛṣṭadyumna. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 50, Verse
47).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Kuntibhoja and Śatānīka occupied the Pāda-
sthāna (foot position) of the Makaravyūha on the
Pāṇḍava side. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 75, Verse 11).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) He possessed a noble and high-bred horse.
(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 23, Verse 46).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) In the great war he fought with Alambuṣa.
(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 16, Verse 183).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) Ten of his children were killed by Aśvatthāmā.
(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 96, Verse 18).
