SIṂHALA¦. Modern Ceylon. In ancient days this place
was called Siṃhala and the inhabitants were called
Siṃhalas. The following statements about Siṃhala
occur in the Mahābhārata.
[Page718-a+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>(1) The low caste people of the Siṃhalas originated
from the sides of Nandinī, the cow of the Devas. (M.B.
Ādi Parva, Chapter 174, Verse 37).
<div n="NI"/>(2) The king of Siṃhala had taken part in the sacrifice
Rājasūya of Yudhiṣṭhira. (Mahābhārata, Sabhā Parva,
Chapter 34, Verse 12).
<div n="NI"/>(3) The Kṣatriyas of Siṃhala gave Yudhiṣṭhira, Chry-
soprases, pearls and such other wealth of the sea as pre-
sent. The people of Siṃhala wore clothes studded with
jewels. They were of dark complexion with eyes the
ends of which were red. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter
58, Verse 35).
<div n="NI"/>(4) The Siṃhalas took the side of the Kauravas in the
battle of Bhārata. In the Garuḍa vyūha formed by
Droṇa, the Siṃhalas were stationed in the position of
the neck. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 20, Verse 6).
