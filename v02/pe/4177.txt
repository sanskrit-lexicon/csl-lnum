MAHENDRA II¦. One of the holy mountains. This
mountain has great Purāṇic importance.
<div n="NI"/>(1) After slaughtering the Kṣatriyas to extinction sage
Paraśurāma made Mahendra his place of abode. (Śloka
53, Chapter 129, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(2) Once Arjuna visited the mountain Mahendra.
(Śloka 13, Chapter 214, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(3) The presiding deity of this mountain sits in the
court of Kubera worshipping him. (Śloka 30, Chapter
10, Sabhā Parva).
[Page465-a+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>(4) If one bathes in the pond of Rāmatīrtha on the
top of this mountain one would get the benefit of per-
forming an Aśvamedhayāga. (Śloka 16, Chapter 85,
Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Brahmā once went to this mountain and conducted
a yāga there. (Śloka 22, Chapter 87, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(6) Yudhiṣṭhira during his pilgrimage visited this
mountain. (Śloka 30, Chapter 114, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(7) Paraśurāma gave a darśana (Interview) to Yudhiṣ-
ṭhira on a Caturdaśī day on this mountain. (Śloka 16,
Chapter 117, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(8) Hanūmān when he was going in search of Sītā
visited this mountain also along with the other monkeys.
(Sundara Kāṇḍa, Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa).
