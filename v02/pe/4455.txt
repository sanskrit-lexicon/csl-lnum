MATSYA II¦. An ancient country of Purāṇic fame. The
people of this country are called Matsyas. The details
available regarding Matsyadeśa from the Mahābhārata
are given below:
<div n="NI"/>(1) The Pāṇḍavas wandering through the forests came
to Matsyadeśa. (Śloka 2, Chapter 155, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(2) The people of Matsyadeśa in fear of Jarāsandha
migrated from the north to the south. (Śloka 28,
Chapter 14, Sabhā Parva).
[Page494-a+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>(3) Bhīmasena during his victory march to the east
conquered this country. (Śloka 8, Chapter 30, Sabhā
Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Sahadeva during his victory march to the south
conquered the people of Matsyadeśa. (Śloka 4, Chapter
31, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Matsyadeśa was one of the countries suggested by
Arjuna to spend their life incognito. (Śloka 12,
Chapter 1, Virāṭa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(6) Virāṭa was the chief of Matsyadeśa during the
time of Mahābhārata. (Śloka 17, Chapter 1, Virāṭa
Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(7) In the Kurukṣetra battle Virāṭa, King of Matsya-
deśa, came to the help of Yudhiṣṭhira with an Akṣau-
hiṇī (army). (Śloka 12, Chapter 18, Udyoga Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(8) The Pāṇḍavas spent a year of their life incognito
at the palace of Virāṭa, king of Matsyadeśa. (Chapter
7, Virāṭa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(9) Matsyarājya was one of the prominent kingdoms of
ancient Bhārata. (Śloka 40, Chapter 9, Bhīṣma Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(10) Bhīṣma slaughtered many soldiers from Matsya-
deśa.
<div n="NI"/>(11) Droṇācārya killed at a stretch five hundred
Matsyas in the great battle. (Śloka 31 Chapter 190,
Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(12) Karṇa once conquered Matsyadeśa. (Śloka 18,
Chapter 8, Karṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(13) The people of Matsyadeśa were honest and charit-
able. (Chapter 45, Śloka 28, Karṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(14) Those who were left out among the Matsyas in
the great battle were killed by Aśvatthāmā. (Śloka 158,
Chapter 8, Sauptika Parva).
