JAMBHA¦. A Daitya (Asura). He was the chief among
those who snatched away Amṛta from the hands of
Dhanvantari. (Agni Purāṇa, Chapter 3).
<div n="NI"/>In the Purāṇas several Asuras (demons) bear-
ing the name Jambha are mentioned. It is stated in
Mahābhārata, Sabhā Parva, Dākṣiṇātyapāṭha, Chapter
[Page342-b+ 65]
38, that Śrī Kṛṣṇa killed an Asura named Jambha.
Mention is made in Mahābhārata, Sabhā Parva,
Chapter 98, Stanza 49 that the teacher Śukra refused
to help a Jambha whom Indra killed later. It occurs
in Mahābhārata, Vana Parva, Chapter 102, Stanza
24, that Mahāviṣṇu had killed an asura called Jambha.
It is stated in Mahābhārata, Vana Parva, Chapter 285,
Stanza 2, that a group of Jambhāsuras, who had
undergone training under Rāvaṇa, once attacked
Hanūmān. Another Jambhāsura had been killed by
Arjuna, as stated in Mahābhārata, Udyoga Parva,
Chapter 49. All these Dānavas (Asuras) were sons of
Kaśyapa born of his wife Danu.
