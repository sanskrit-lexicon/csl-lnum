JANADEVA¦. A Janaka King who ruled over Mithilā
(Śānti Parva, Chapter 218, Verse 3).
<div n="NI"/>(1) Mahāviṣṇu, once in the guise of a brahmin, tested
the King and pleased with him granted him certain
boons. (Śānti Parva, Chapter 219).
<div n="NI"/>(2) None of the 100 ācāryas in his court could answer
his questions about salvation satisfactorily. But, Pañca-
śikha, who casually came to the palace advised the
King about the means to attain salvation. (Śānti Parva
Chapter 211).
