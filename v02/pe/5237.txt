PRABHĀSA¦ II The sacred place called Prabhāsatīrtha.
This place of Purāṇic importance is situated in Saurā-
ṣṭra on the west coast. This is called Somatīrtha also.
Some facts about this tīrtha from the Purāṇas are
given below:
<div n="NI"/>(i) During the pilgrimage of Arjuna he did penance
for a thousand divine years standing on one foot at Pra-
bhāsa, Śloka 15, Chapter 12, Vana Parva).
[Page592-b+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Agnideva resides in this place. One who bathes in
the holy pond there would get the benefit of doing
Agniṣṭomayajña. (Śloka 58, Chapter 82, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Once Yudhiṣṭhira along with his brothers came
and did penance here. (Śloka 15, Chapter 118, Vana
Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Prabhāsatīrtha is dear to Indra. (Śloka 7, Chapter
130, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Those who bathe in the holy pond in Prabhāsa are
taken to heaven in Vimānas and they then by songs of
praise wake up the celestial maidens there. (Śloka 9,
Chapter 25, Anuśāsana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) It was at this Prabhāsatīrtha that the Yādavas
fought against one another and destroyed themselves.
(Śloka 10, Chapter 3, Mausala Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Śrī Kṛṣṇa and Balarāma merged with the Supreme
spirit at this place. (Chapter 4, Mausala Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) The son of sage Sāndīpani was kidnapped by
Pañcajana while the former was bathing at this tīrtha.
(10th Skandha, Bhāgavata).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) It was while standing on the shore of the Prabhāsa
pond that Arjuna first heard about Subhadrā. (10th
Skandha, Bhāgavata).
