{@Kaṇikavākya(ṃ)@}¦ (“the speech of Kaṇika”). § 212
[Page381-a+ 63]
(Sambhavap.). {%Dhṛtarāṣṭra%} consulted the minister {%Kaṇika%},
who, after having expounded some very Machiavellian
principles of policy, and told the fable of the jackal, the
tiger, the mouse, the wolf, and the mongoose, concluded by
advising him to destroy the {%Pāṇḍavas%} (I, 140).
