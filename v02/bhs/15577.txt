{@saṃgraha-vastu@}¦, <lex>nt.</lex> (= <lang>Pali</lang> saṃgahavatthu), {%article of attraction%}, means by which a Buddha or (more often in <lang>BHS</lang>) a Bodhisattva {%attracts%}, draws to himself and to religious life, creatures. The literal <ab>mg.</ab> has been much disputed; it seems to me clarified by <ls>LV</ls> 〔429.13〕 where reference is made to the Buddhaʼs {%cleverness in drawing-in creatures%} (sattvasaṃgrahakauśalyaṃ) {%by the net%} (-jālena) {%of the 4 saṃgrahavastu%}; with this <ab>cf.</ab> <ls>Gv</ls> 〔495.20〕, cited <ab>s.v.</ab> {@saṃgraha@}. As in <lang>Pali</lang>, there are four of these: dāna, {@priya-vacana@} (or the like), {@artha-caryā@} (or {@-kriyā@}), and {@samānārthatā@} (or {@samānasukhaduḥkhatā@}), <ab>qq.v.</ab>; they are listed <ls>LV</ls> 〔38.16—17〕; 〔160.6—7〕; 〔182.6〕; 〔429.12—13〕; <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.3.11—12〕; 〔ii.395.8—9〕 (without use of the term saṃgraha-vastu); <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔924—928〕; <ls>Dharmas</ls> 〔19〕; <ls>Bbh</ls> 〔217.2—230.19〕, the inclusive term 〔227.5〕; 〔230.19〕; without lists of the 4 items, <ls>SP</ls> 〔259.5〕; <ls>LV</ls> 〔35.9〕; 〔437.15〕; <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔793〕; <ls>Divy</ls> 〔95.15〕; 〔264.29〕; <ls>Śikṣ</ls> 〔50.11〕; <ls>Av</ls> 〔i.16.12〕; <ls>Gv</ls> 〔495.17〕 (in most of these the number {%four%} is specified). Sometimes replaced by {@saṃgraha@}, <ab>q.v.</ab>, alone.
