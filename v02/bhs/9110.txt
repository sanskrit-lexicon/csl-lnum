{@paribhoga@}¦, <lex>m.</lex> (commonly, as in <lang>Skt.</lang> and <lang>Pali</lang>, {%enjoyment, usufruct, use%}, also {%article of enjoyment%}; <ab>e.g.</ab> bodhisattvasya paribhogārtham <ls>LV</ls> 〔95.15〕, °gāya 16), {%property to be enjoyed%}, in <ls>LV</ls> 〔60.18 ff.〕 … ratnavyūhaṃ bodhisattvaparibhogaṃ draṣṭuṃ yo mātuḥ kukṣigatasya bodhisattvasya paribhogo 'bhūt, refers to a physical (<ab>tho</ab> of course magical) structure, described in detail 〔63.1 ff.〕, which houses the Bodhisattva in his motherʼs womb; it had three turrets or apartments, kūṭāgāra, one within the other, and within the third a couch or litter, paryaṅka; it was hard ‘like vajra’ yet soft to the touch (〔64.7—8〕); all the bhavana-vyūha of the kāmāvacara gods appeared in it (〔64.9〕); it always develops or appears in the right side of a Bodhisattvaʼs mother in his last earthly existence (〔65.20〕, here called {@ratnavyūhaḥ@}, <ab>q.v.</ab>, kūṭāgāro); in 〔73.3〕 referred to again, drakṣasi (so text and <ab>app.</ab> all <ab>mss.</ab>) tvam Ānanda ratnavyūhaṃ bodhisattvaparibhogaṃ yatra bodhisattvo mātuḥ kukṣigato vyāhārṣīt. <lang>Tib.</lang> on <ls>LV</ls> 〔60.18〕 renders literally, {%complete enjoyment%}, <tib>yoṅs su spyad pa</tib>, <ab>cf.</ab> <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔7369〕 paribhogaḥ = <lang>Tib.</lang> <tib>yoṅs su spyod</tib>. <ls>SP</ls> 〔337.13〕. kūṭāgāraparibhogeṣu cātra bodhisattvān nivasato drakṣyati, [Page328-b] does not refer to this magical structure in the womb, but to structures occupied by bodhisattvas while attending on the Buddha Śākyamuni.
