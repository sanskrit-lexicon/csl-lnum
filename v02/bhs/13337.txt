{@vartanā@}¦ ({@-veṇī@}; for <lang>Pali</lang> see below), {%(string of) bead(s)%}. Note that veṇī in <lang>Pali</lang>, and at least veṇikā in <lang>Skt.</lang>, are used in the <ab>fig.</ab> sense of {%line, string%}; <lang>Tib.</lang> on <ls>LV</ls> (both times) renders <tib>lan bu</tib> (or, <tib>bur</tib>) <tib>blas pa</tib>, rendered by <ls>Foucaux</ls> {%<fr>le tissu dʼune tresse</fr>%}; I cannot find <tib>blas pa</tib> in <lang>Tib.</lang> <ab>Dictt.</ab>, but <tib>lan bu</tib> does indeed seem to mean {%braid of hair%}, the normal <lang>Skt.</lang> meaning of veṇī; it may be that <lang>Tib.</lang> mistranslated. Occurs twice in <ls>LV</ls>, in the same comparison: 〔254.13〕 (tad yathāpi nāma) vartanyā veṇy (read vartanāveṇy) unnatāvanatā bhavati samaviṣamā, evaṃ me pṛṣṭhīkaṇṭako …; and 〔256.1〕 (tad yathā karkaṭakapārśukā) vāhanaśālāyāṃ vā gopānāsī pārśve (<ab>cf.</ab> 〔254.9—11〕) dvipari-(see this!)vartanā veṇīvat (read as one <ab>cpd.</ab> word) pṛṣṭhīkaṇṭakaḥ. The <ls>Mv</ls> parallels clearly had {@vaṭṭanāvalī@}, <ab>q.v.</ab>, in accord with the <lang>Pali</lang>. The <lang>Pali</lang> <ls>MN</ls> 〔i.80.15〕 (<ab>cf.</ab> also 〔81.12〕; 〔245.30〕) has: (seyyathā) pi nāma vaṭṭanāvaḷī evam eva ssu me piṭṭhikaṇṭako unnatāvanato hoti; <ab>comm.</ab> 〔ii.50.5〕, yathā rajjuyā āvuṇitvā katā vaṭṭanāvaḷī vaṭṭanānaṃ antarantarā ninnā hoti, vaṭṭanaṭṭhānesu unnatā … See also {@vartita@} (°tā … veṇī).
