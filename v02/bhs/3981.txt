{@ūrdhvāgra-@}¦ (ūrdhvāṅga-, ūrdhvaṃga-, ūrdhvaga-; also written ūrdhā° without v, see {@ūrdha-@}; for other variants see below) {@-roma(n)@}, <lex>adj.</lex> (= <lang>Pali</lang> uddhaggaloma), {%having (body-)hair standing up%}, <ab>lit.</ab> {%with upright tips%}, one of the 32 {@lakṣaṇa@}, <ab>q.v.</ab> The form ūrdh(v)āgra-, supported by <lang>Pali</lang>, is <ab>prob.</ab> <ab>orig.</ab> (so <ls>Burnouf, Lotus</ls>, 〔571〕); it is found in <ls>Dharmas</ls> 〔83〕; <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.226.18〕 (<ab>mss.</ab> kṛtvāgra); 〔ii.305.15〕 (<ab>mss.</ab> ūrddhāgraromarājino or uddhā°); but in 〔ii.30.2〕 <ab>mss.</ab> ūrddhaṃga, urddhaṃ ca (<ls>Senart</ls> ūrdhvāgra). <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔257〕 has ūrdhva-ga-romaḥ, but <ls>Mironov</ls> ūrdhvaṃga° (both {%going upward%}; <ab>cf.</ab> <ab>mss.</ab> in <ls>Mv</ls> 〔ii.30.2〕); and <ls>Bbh</ls> 〔375.17〕; 〔381.24〕 also ūrdhvaṃga-(romā, <lex>adj.</lex>; romatā, <lex>subst.</lex>). In <ls>Bbh</ls> 〔379.10〕 the <ab>ms.</ab> has the further change to ūrdhvāṅga-(romatāṃ; <ab>ed.</ab> <ab>em.</ab> ūrdhvaṃga), as if ūrdhva-aṅga-roma-; so <ls>Gv</ls> 〔402.9—10〕 ūrdhvāṅgaromā (<ab>Bhvr.</ab>); <ls>LV</ls> 〔429.15〕 ūrdhvāṅga-(dakṣiṇāvarta-)-romakūpa(ḥ). Finally in <ls>LV</ls> 〔105.21〕 there is great confusion; <ls>Lefm.</ls> (only with B <ab>mss.</ab>) ūrdhāgrā(bhipradakṣiṇāvarta)-romāḥ (read °mā, <ab>Bhvr.</ab>); other <ab>mss.</ab> u(d)dhāṃgā°, uccāśā° (ucca-āśā); <lang>Tib.</lang> obscure, <tib>yan lag gi spu gyas phyogs nas gyen du ḥkhyil ba</tib>, <ab>lit.</ab> {%hair of member(s), from (after) right direction%} (generally = diś or āśā), {%upward twist%} (generally = āvarta). As <ls>Burnouf</ls> points out, <ls>Foucauxʼs transl.</ls> is inaccurate. The expression (abhi-pra-)dakṣiṇāvarta is inserted here in the epithet only in <ls>LV</ls> (both passages); it is attached to the lakṣaṇa ekaikaroma(n) in <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔256〕 and <ls>Dharmas</ls> 〔83〕, and in the commentarial expansion of the latter <ls>Gv</ls> 〔402.9〕; but <lang>Pali</lang> commentarial expansions of uddhaggaloma have a corresponding term, <ls>Burnouf, Lotus</ls>, 〔571〕.
