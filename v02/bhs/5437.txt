{@khaḍga-viṣāṇa@}¦, {%rhinoceros%}; {@-kalpa@}, <lex>adj.</lex> (= <lang>Pali</lang> khagga-visāṇa-kappa), {%like a rhinoceros%}, <ab>i.e.</ab> {%living a lonely life%}; <ab>esp.</ab> (as in <lang>Pali</lang>) <ab>ep.</ab> of a Pratyekabuddha: <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔1006〕; <ls>Divy</ls> 〔582.8〕; ekacarā °kalpā <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.301.4〕, and in a <ab>vs</ab> 〔357.15〕, otherwise eko care °kalpo 〔21 ff.〕, or ekaś caret °kalpaḥ <ls>Divy</ls> 〔294.15〕, refrain of the stanzas of <lang>Pali</lang> <ls>Sn</ls> 〔i.3〕 (<ab>vss</ab> 35—75: eko care khagga-visāṇakappo), a number of which are reproduced in <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.357.12—359.15〕, and one in <ls>Divy</ls> <ab>l. c.</ab>; they are designated khaḍgaviṣāṇa-gāthāḥ <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.359.16〕. Since <lang>Skt.</lang> khaḍga and <lang>Pali</lang> khagga mean {%rhinoceros%}, the <lang>Pali</lang> <ab>comm.</ab> on <ls>Sn</ls> paraphrases kh°-visāṇa by {%rhinoceros-horn%}. But actually the <ab>cpd.</ab> means {%rhinoceros%}, = <lang>Skt.</lang> khaḍgin, originally {%having a sword%} (-like) {%horn%}. The comparison is to the animal, not to its horn. <ab>Cf.</ab> <ls>Samādh</ls> 〔19.29〕 khaḍga-samā; <ls>RP</ls> 〔13.7〕 khaḍga-vimalāḥ [Page203-a] (cited <ls>Śikṣ</ls> 〔196.4〕 as khaḍga-sadṛśāḥ); <ls>RP</ls> 〔16.7〕 (<ab>vs</ab>) eka viharati yathaiva khaḍgo. In all these khaḍga is the precise equivalent of khaḍgaviṣāṇa (and khaḍgin), {%rhinoceros%}.
