{@upahāra@}¦, <lex>m.</lex> (<lex>nt.</lex> once; = <lang>Skt.</lang>, <lang>Pali</lang>, <lang>Pkt.</lang> <ab>id.</ab>), fundamentally {%gift%}, (loving) {%present%}, in <lang>BHS</lang> as elsewhere; <ab>e.g.</ab> (with change of gender) <ls>Av</ls> 〔i.378.2〕 (prose) sarvopahārāṇi copaḍhaukitāni. In a <ls>Mv</ls> passage beginning 〔i.177.13〕 used repeatedly in a somewhat peculiar way which misled <ls>Senart</ls>, who in two notes (〔i.518 f.〕, 〔523 f.〕) offers three different interpretations, all wrong (the passage cited 〔519〕 from <ls>SP</ls> contains a false reading; the <ls>SP</ls> <ab>ed.</ab> 〔476.5—6〕 does not contain the word cited by <ls>Senart</ls>, not even as a <ab>v.l.</ab>). In this <ls>Mv</ls> passage, the word applies to various acts by which the Buddha miraculously intervenes to save someone from an intended grievous sin or to convert him from a dangerously false heresy. It should be rendered by something like (kindly or compassionate) {%favor, gift, beneficial service%}: <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.177.13—15〕 introduces the detailed stories of specific acts called upahāra thus: paropahārāṃś ca … upaharanti ({%they present beneficial favors to others%}) samyaksaṃbuddhāḥ sattvānām anugrahārthaṃ. tad yathā, kaliṅgarājñaḥ kusumāye devyā paropahāraṃ bhagavāṃ vṛttavāṃ, dhruvasya śreṣṭhino vacanopahāraṃ ({%a benefit by spoken words%}) bhagavāṃ vṛttavāṃ; the <ab>cpd.</ab> paropa° also 〔178.5〕; 〔180.12〕; vacanopa° also 〔178.2〕. Other occurrences: 〔178.7—8〕 (<ab>vs</ab>) etāṃ sarvāṃ pravakṣyāmi upahārāṃ manoramāṃ, tasya sattvapradhānasya śṛṇu vikrīḍitaṃ śubhaṃ; 〔184.(17—)18〕 (<ab>vs</ab>) (koṭīyo dvādaśa muniḥ) mānuṣāṇāṃ vinayati, upahāro ayaṃ iti; 〔185.3〕 upahāro vidhātavyo (to prevent Dhruva from burning his parents); 〔188.(10—)11〕 (<ab>vs</ab>) (yat tasya parikarma tat …) tam āhur upahāro ti; 〔192.(10—)11〕 (<ab>vs</ab>) (ye tatra nirmitā bhiksūḥ na caite bhikṣuṇo matā,) upahāraṃ vadanty etaṃ jinā śāstraviśāradāḥ.
