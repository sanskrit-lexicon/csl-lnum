{@antara-kalpa@}¦, <lex>m.</lex> (= <lang>Pali</lang> °kappa), {%internal%} (sub-division of a) {%kalpa%} or {%aeon%}, or {%intermediate kalpa%} (period between major kalpas). In <ls>AbhidhK.</ls> 〔iii.181〕 the <ab>var.</ab> antaḥkalpa is recorded, along with this; it seems to point to the first definition above, and <ls>LaVallée Poussin</ls>, <ab>ad loc.</ab>, considers this the only correct definition; there are 80 in a mahākalpa, <ab>op. cit.</ab> 〔187〕 (in <lang>Pali</lang> 〔64〕, <ls>CPD</ls>). On the other hand <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔8281〕 renders antara-(kalpa) by <lang>Tib.</lang> <tib>bar gyi</tib>, {%intermediate%}, and similarly śastrāntara-, rogāntara-, durbhikṣāntara-k° 〔8282—4〕; these suggest that antarakalpas are periods of destruction or disaster for people, <ab>cf.</ab> <ls>CPD</ls> <ab>s.v.</ab> °kappa, {%a%} (short) {%intermediate period%} (of destruction of mankind). <ls>CPD</ls> recognizes both the above meanings, and this seems provisionally probable. It is often not clear which appears in specific cases; in <ls>Gv</ls> 〔325.15〕 perhaps the second: dvādaśānām antarakalpānām atyayena <ls>SP</ls> 〔67.1〕; prabhāṣate taj jina agradharmān … antarakalpaṣaṣṭim <ls>SP</ls> 〔25.8〕; <ab>cf.</ab> <ls>Dharmas</ls> 〔87〕 (see <ab>s.v.</ab> {@kalpa@} 4); <ls>SP</ls> 〔159.3 ff.〕; <ls>Mmk</ls> 〔295.9〕 (°paṃ jīvati). In <ls>SP</ls> 〔68.10〕 text manujānam abhyantara-kalpa (= antara°), but <ab>prob.</ab> read °jān' apī antara° (see <ls>WT</ls>).
