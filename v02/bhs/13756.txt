{@vijñāna@}¦, <lex>nt.</lex> (in <ab>mgs.</ab> 3—5 = <lang>Pali</lang> viññāna; <ab>orig.</ab> in <lang>Skt.</lang>, {%practical knowledge%}, <ab>opp.</ab> to {@jñāna@}, {%theoretical knowledge%}, see <ls>Edgerton, Festschrift Winternitz</ls>, 〔217 ff.〕), ({@1@}) {%practical knowledge, applied knowledge%}, as in <lang>Skt.</lang>, still clearly in <ls>LV</ls> 〔422.13〕 durvijñānaṃ (or <ab>v.l.</ab> °jñeyaṃ) tac cakraṃ jñāna-vijñāna-samatānubaddhatvāt, {%this wheel%} (of the Doctrine) {%is hard to know%} (practically), {%because it is%} [Page486-a] {%inseparably connected with identify of theoretical and practical knowledge%} (<ab>i.e.</ab> can only be known by one whose theoretical knowledge is at the same time applied in practice); in <ls>Laṅk</ls> 〔156.11 ff.〕 a dissertation on the difference between jñāna and vi°, rendered by <ls>Suzuki, Studies</ls> 〔272〕, {%transcendental%} (<ab>i.e.</ab> absolute) {%and%} (merely) {%relative knowledge%}; I should prefer {%abstract%} and {%applied knowledge%}; but it is true that to the author of <ls>Laṅk</ls> {%applied knowledge%} (vij°) has only {%practical%}, and hence in the last analysis no {%real%}, application; the passage cited makes this very clear.
