{@tāyin@}¦, <lex>m.</lex> (= <lang>AMg.</lang> tāi, defined as {%attaining salvation%}, <ab>i.e.</ab> {%holy, religious%}; also {%who protects himself and others%}, <ab>i.e.</ab> a Jina; <lang>Pali</lang> tādi, see below), originally Prakritic for <lang>Pali</lang> tādi(n) = <lang>Skt.</lang> tādṛś; see {@tādṛ(n), tādṛśa(ka)@}. The identity of the two words can hardly be questioned. The [Page252-a] <ab>mg.</ab> of <lang>Pali</lang> tādi(n) is also quite clear, {%such%} (= tādṛś); doubt remains only as to whether this meant originally {%such as the Buddha%}, of the same quality as He, or {%such as a religious man ought to be%}, thus {%holy, following the path of true religion%}. On the <lang>Pali</lang> see <ab>esp.</ab> <ls>Childers</ls> <ab>s.v.</ab>, also <ls>Senart</ls> on <ls>Mv</ls> 〔ii.256.9〕, where <lang>BHS</lang> uses evaṃrūpa in nearly the same sense; <ls>Senart</ls> points out that tathārūpa glosses tādi in <ls>Dhp. comm.</ls> <ab>Acc.</ab> to <ls>Childers</ls> tādi is usually applied to {%holy men%}, only rarely to Buddha. In <lang>BHS</lang> tāyin most often applies to Buddhas, but also fairly often to Bodhisattvas, and occasionally to other holy men. Once, at least, {@tādṛśaka@}, <ab>q.v.</ab>, is clearly applied to a Buddha, like tāyin. These facts all together make it seem clear to me that the <ab>etym.</ab> and original <ab>mg.</ab> are as stated above. It is true that <lang>Tib.</lang> (see on <ls>Mvy</ls> below) interprets tāyin as if for trāyin, rendering {%protector%} or the like; some modern scholars (<ls>Burnouf, Lotus</ls> 〔16〕, on <ab>vs</ab> 73 of <ab>Chap.</ab> 1; <ls>pw</ls> 〔7.345〕 ‘<ger>wohl nur fehlerhaft für trāyin</ger>’) assume that this was the <ab>etym.</ab> and primary <ab>mg.</ab> of tāyin. But against this stands not only <lang>Pali</lang> but <lang>BHS</lang> {@tādṛśa(ka), tādṛ(n)-@}. <lang>Tib.</lang> doubtless has a secondary popular etymology. The forms are typical of in-stems: tāyī <ab>n. sg.</ab> (<ls>SP</ls> 〔45.13〕; <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔15, 1746〕), tāyinaḥ <lex>gen.</lex> <ab>sg.</ab> (<ls>SP</ls> 〔69.2〕; 〔208.7〕, <ab>etc.</ab>), °nām <lex>gen.</lex> <ab>pl.</ab> (<ls>SP</ls> 〔176.8〕 <ab>etc.</ab>), tāyibhiḥ (<ls>LV</ls> 〔388.13〕), <ab>etc.</ab> Occasionally the <ab>mss.</ab> write corruptly tāpin (so <ls>Mv</ls> 〔ii.349.12〕 text, <ab>prob.</ab> mere misprint; <ls>Mmk</ls> 〔98.8〕; 〔499.19〕; 〔599.20〕; 〔600.17〕). ({@1@}) Used of others than Bodhisattvas and Buddhas, {%holy%}: anigho tāyi tam āhu śrotriyaṃ ti <ls>Mv</ls> 〔iii.400.2〕; ariyo (<ab>mss.</ab>, <ls>Senart</ls> āryo) tāyi pravuccati tathatvā 〔400.6〕; in 〔iii.397.2〕 read, śramaṇo tāyi (<ab>mss.</ab> bhāvayi, <ls>Senart</ls> tādi, <ab>cf.</ab> 〔400.6〕) pravuccati tathatvā, {%he is called in truth a monk, a holy man%} (or, {%one such as the Buddha%}, or, {%such as he should be%}); tāyī sa sarvāṃ (read °vaṃ) prajahāti duḥkham <ls>Ud</ls> 〔xix.1〕, in later version of line which in oldest <ab>ms.</ab> reads prahāsate (= °syate) sarvabhavāni tādṛśāḥ, {%the holy man (men) gets (will get) rid of all misery (states of being)%}; ({@2@}) used of Bodhisattvas (other than Śākyamuni in his last existence, when in laudations he is often given epithets of a Buddha, even before his enlightenment): <ls>SP</ls> 〔304.5〕 (<ab>vs</ab>; buddhaputrasya tāyinaḥ), 12; 〔306.1〕; <ls>Mv</ls> 〔ii.370.1〕 (<ab>vs</ab>, buddhaputrāṇa tāyināṃ); <ls>Sādh</ls> 〔93.10〕 (of Mañjuvajra = Mañjuśrī); <ls>Dbh</ls> 〔29(55).14〕 (?reference not clear); ({@3@}) but most commonly of the Buddha (Śākyamuni) or of any or all Buddhas: <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔15〕 (text trāyī, tāyī, but <ls>Mironov</ls> tāyī without <ab>v.l.</ab>; <lang>Tib.</lang> <tib>skyob pa</tib>, {%protector%}); 〔1746〕 (tāyī; <lang>Tib.</lang> <tib>skyob ston</tib>, {%protector-teacher%}); <ls>SP</ls> 〔25.1〕; 〔45.13〕; 〔69.2〕; 〔116.9〕 (tāyinaḥ, {%for the Buddha%}; wrongly <ls>Kern</ls>); 〔176.8〕; 〔208.7〕; 〔303.13〕; 〔331.8〕; <ls>LV</ls> 〔122.20〕 (tāyino with <ab>v.l.</ab> for kāyi no); 〔388.13〕; 〔421.5〕; <ls>Mv</ls> 〔ii.349.12〕 = 〔iii.273.11〕; 〔ii.351.8, 14〕; 〔352.15〕; 〔353.17〕; 〔iii.109.20〕; 〔124.20〕; 〔445.17〕; <ls>Av</ls> 〔ii.199.4〕; <ls>Suv</ls> 〔17.11〕; <ls>Śikṣ</ls> 〔260.11〕; <ls>Mmk</ls> 〔98.8〕; 〔125.15〕; 〔320.14〕; 〔375.10, 15〕; 〔442.9〕; 〔499.19〕; 〔599.20〕; 〔600.17〕; nikṣiptaḥ sādhu tāyinā <ls>Divy</ls> 〔712.7〕. All these passages (except <ls>Mvy</ls> where the word is cited alone) are verses; they are not exhaustive, but it is doubtful whether tāyin occurs anywhere in prose.
