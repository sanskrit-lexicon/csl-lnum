{@deva@}¦ (<lang>Skt.</lang>), often also {@devaputra@} (rare in <lang>Skt.</lang>, common in <lang>Pali</lang> devaputta), {%god%}. More or less complete and corresponding lists of the classes of Buddhist gods are given in <lang>Pali</lang> in <ls>MN</ls> 〔iii.100 ff.〕; <ls>Kvu</ls> 〔207, 208〕; <ls>Abhidh-s.</ls> (here called <ab n="Abhidh-s.">Abh</ab>) 〔21〕; <ls>Childers</ls> (<ab n="Childers">Ch</ab>) 〔467〕 has a list nearly agreeing with the last; in <lang>BHS</lang>, in <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔3075 ff.〕; <ls>Dharmas</ls> 〔127 ff.〕; <ls>Mv</ls> (1) 〔ii.314.4 ff.〕; (2) 〔348.16 ff.〕; <ls>LV</ls> 〔150.2 ff.〕; <ls>Av</ls> 〔i.5.1 ff.〕 (<lat><ab>et alibi</ab></lat>); <ls>Divy</ls> (1) 〔68.12 ff.〕; (2) 〔138.19 ff.〕; (3) 〔367.9 ff.〕; (4) 〔568.24 ff.〕; <ls>Bbh</ls> 〔61.27 ff.〕; <ls>Gv</ls> 〔249.10 ff.〕 (the last in reverse order). There are other lists, mostly fragmentary, and often so confused as to be hardly usable. The gods fall into three grand divisions: {@kāmāvacara@}, living in the {@kāmadhātu@} (<ab>cf.</ab> <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔3071—4〕), usually six in number; {@rūpāvacara@}, in the {@rūpadhātu; ārūpyāvacara@}, in the {@ārūpyadhātu@}. <ab>Cf.</ab> also the 9 {@sattvāvāsa@}, <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔2288 ff.〕 (<lang>Pali</lang> <ls>DN</ls> 〔3.263〕). —{@Kāmāvacara@} (deva). The standard list is: {@cāturmahārājika@} (<lang>BHS</lang> sometimes cat°); often {@°rājakāyika@}, so in <ls>Mvy</ls>, <ls>Dharmas</ls>, <ls>Divy</ls> 〔2〕, <ls>Bbh</ls>; {@trāyastriṃśa@} (<ls>Av</ls>, <ls>Divy</ls>, <ls>Bbh</ls>, {@trayas°@}); {@yāma@} (<ls>Bbh</ls> yama); {@tuṣita@} (in <ls>Dharmas</ls> before yāma); {@nirmāṇarati@} (see also {@nirmita@}); {@paranirmitavaśavartin@}. The <ls>Gv</ls> list omits 1 and 2. Before 1, <ls>Mvy</ls> inserts {@bhauma@} and {@āntarikṣavāsin@}, making 8 instead of 6. So <ls>Mv</ls> 〔2〕 prefixes bhūmyāṃ (see <ab>s.v.</ab> {@bhūmi@}) va carā (devāḥ; so read with <ab>mss.</ab>; va = eva) and {@antarīkṣecarā@}(ḥ). While no other of the above lists has these two items, {@bhauma@} and {@antarīkṣa@} (or {@antarikṣa@}; adjective) devas are associated with shorter lists (generally of kāmāvacara, or some of them, alone) in <ls>LV</ls> 〔266.1〕; 〔367.7〕 and 〔368.3〕; 〔396.14〕; 〔401.1〕; and correspondingly {@bhūmya@} and {@antarīkṣecara@} (deva) in <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.40.14〕; 〔229.14, 15〕; 〔240.3, 4〕; 〔ii.138.12〕 and elsewhere. —{@Rūpāvacara@} (deva) dwell in the rūpadhātu (<ls>Mvy</ls> 〔3073〕), divided into the four {@dhyāna-@}bhūmi (<ls>Mvy</ls> 〔3084 ff.〕, see {@dhyāna@}), the last of which includes, as its final and highest group, the {@śuddhāvāsakāyika@} gods, in five sub-groups. These being counted separately, the standard list of rūpāvacara contains usually 18 items in <lang>BHS</lang>, 16 in <lang>Pali</lang>, as follows. First dhyāna-bhūmi, usually 4 items, in <lang>Pali</lang> 3: {@brahmakāyika@} (not in <lang>Pali</lang> lists, but the word occurs, <ab>acc.</ab> to <ls>Childers</ls> as an inclusive term for the classes of the first dhyāna-bhūmi; perhaps rather for all the rūpāvacara, or for them plus the arūpāvacara, since they all inhabit brahmalokas, <ls>Childers</ls> <ab>s.v.</ab>; in <ls>Gv</ls> also omitted; in <ls>Mv</ls> put second, {@brahmā@} (devā) being first in <ls>Mv</ls> 〔1〕, and {@mahābrahmā@} in <ls>Mv</ls> 〔2〕); {@brahmapāriṣadya@} (<ls>Dharmas</ls>, <ls>LV</ls>, <ls>Divy</ls> 〔4〕 {@°pārṣadya@}, <ls>Gv</ls> {@°pārṣada@}; <ab>om.</ab> <ls>Mv</ls> 〔1〕, <ls>Divy</ls> 〔1—3〕, <ls>Av</ls>, <ls>Bbh</ls>; after brahmapurohita in <ls>Dharmas</ls>, <ls>LV</ls>, <ls>Mv</ls> 〔2〕, <ls>Divy</ls> 〔4〕; our order is that of <ls>Mvy</ls>, [Page270-b] <ls>Gv</ls>, <lang>Pali</lang>); {@brahmapurohita@} (<ls>Gv</ls> omits brahma by haplography), {@mahābrahman@} (or °hma; as 〔no. 1〕 in <ls>Mv</ls> 〔2〕). Second dhyāna-bhūmi, regularly 3 items in <lang>BHS</lang> and <lang>Pali</lang>; but <ls>Mv</ls> 〔1 and 2〕 and <lang>Pali</lang> <ls>MN</ls> 〔iii.102.25〕 prefix another, {@ābhā(ḥ)@} (misprinted abhā in <ls>MN</ls>) devā(ḥ): {@parīttābha@} (<ls>Divy</ls> 1—3 <ab>mss.</ab> {@parītā°@}; <ls>Mv</ls> 〔1〕 omits); {@apramāṇābha@} (<ls>Mv</ls> 〔1〕 omits); {@ābhāsvara@} (<ls>Divy</ls> 〔3〕 {@apramāṇābhāsvara@}). Third dhyāna-bhūmi, regularly 3 items in <lang>BHS</lang> and <lang>Pali</lang>; <ls>Mv</ls> and <lang>Pali</lang> <ls>MN</ls> 〔iii.102.30〕 add another, {@śubhā@} devāḥ (<lang>Pali</lang> subhā devā): {@parīttaśubha@} (<ls>Mv</ls> 〔2〕 omits); {@apramāṇaśubha@} (<ls>Dharmas</ls> omits), {@śubhakṛtsna@}. Fourth dhyāna-bhūmi, usually three items in <lang>BHS</lang>, one in <lang>Pali</lang>, plus (sometimes <ab>app.</ab> included in the 4th <ab n="dhyāna-bhūmi">dhy. bh.</ab> but sometimes not, <ab>cf.</ab> <ls>Childers</ls> <ab>s.v.</ab> jhānaṃ) the five śuddhāvāsa(kāyika), who are usually not given this separate group-designation in the lists (but are so designated <ab>e.g.</ab> in <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔3101〕 and <ls>Abh</ls>): {@anabhraka@} (<lang>Pali</lang> and <ls>Mv</ls> omit); {@puṇyaprasava@} (<lang>Pali</lang> and <ls>Mv</ls> omit); {@vṛhatphala@} (so only <ls>Mvy</ls> and <ls>Mv</ls> 〔2〕 in <lang>BHS</lang>, others {@bṛh°@}, but <lang>Pali</lang> vehapphala); only in <ls>Dharmas</ls>, <ls>LV</ls>, and <ls>Abh</ls> of <lang>Pali</lang> there follows {@asaṃjñisattva@}, <lang>Pali</lang> (<ls>Abh</ls>) asaññasatta, a term which occurs as the 5th sattāvāsa in the <lang>Pali</lang> list of these <ls>DN</ls> 〔iii.263.9 ff.〕 (preceding the arūpadhātu), while in the corresponding list of 9 sattvāvāsa in <ls>Mvy</ls> (〔2297〕) it is made the 9th and highest, above the arūpadhātu (an obvious error of <ls>Mvy</ls>); this item is also given in <ls>Childers</ls>ʼs list and is needed to make up the traditional <lang>Pali</lang> number of 16 rūpa-brahmaloka. Then all lists have the five {@śuddhāvāsakāyika: avṛha@} (<ls>Av</ls>, <ls>Divy</ls> 〔1—3〕, <ls>Bbh</ls> {@abṛha@}, <ls>Gv</ls> {@abṛhat@}); {@atapa@} (<ls>Gv</ls> atapo, as s-stem); {@sudṛśa@} (<ls>Mv</ls> 〔1〕 omits); {@sudarśana@} (<ls>Mv</ls> 〔2〕 omits; <ls>Divy</ls> 〔1〕 {@sudarśa@}); {@akaniṣṭha@} (for the <lang>Pali</lang> forms see these words). Here <ls>Mvy</ls> alone adds as additional stages of śuddhāvāsakāyika, {@aghaniṣṭha@} and {@mahāmaheśvarāyatanam@} (see these words). —The {@ārūpyāvacara@} gods dwell in the four {@ārūpyadhātu@} bhūmi listed (only in <ls>Mvy</ls> and <ls>Dharmas</ls>, and in <lang>Pali</lang> <ls>MN</ls>, <ls>Abh.</ls> and <ls>Childers</ls>) as {@ākāśānantyāyatana, vijñānānantyāyatana, ākiṃcanyāyatana@}, and {@naivasaṃjñānāsaṃjñāyatana@}; the gods dwelling in them are described in <ls>Dharmas</ls> as {@°āyatanopagāḥ@} (see <ab>s.v.</ab> {@upaga@}; similarly <lang>Pali</lang> °āyatanūpagā). In <ls>Suv</ls> 〔86.11 ff.〕, curiously, the first three ‘stages’, ending °āyatana, are personified as gods (°āyatanānāṃ koṭiśataṃ), while only with the last °āyatanopagatānāṃ (for °nopagānāṃ) is used. The first of the four is found in the <lang>Pali</lang> <ls>Kvu</ls> list also. For the <lang>Pali</lang> forms of the names see <ab>s.vv.</ab>
