{@mahārāja(n)@}¦, (= <lang>Pali</lang> <ab>id.</ab>), ({@1@}) {%one of the four guardians of the cardinal directions%}, corresponding to <lang>Skt.</lang> lokapāla, and called by this name <ls>Dharmas</ls> 〔7〕, <ls>MSV</ls> 〔i.255.17〕, and <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔3145〕 as sometimes in <lang>Pali</lang>, but usually mahārāja(n) in both <lang>Pali</lang> and <lang>BHS</lang>: catvāro (°raś ca) mahārājāno or °rājā <ls>LV</ls> 〔202.13〕; 〔366.11〕; <ls>Divy</ls> 〔218.9〕; <ls>Av</ls> 〔i.67.10〕; 〔ii.104.2, 12〕; <lex>instr.</lex> <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.230.12〕; often, as in some of the above, included in lists of other gods; mahārāja-sthānam <ls>SP</ls> 〔264.12〕, {%the position of world-guardian%}, as one of five states [Page425-b] which a woman cannot attain. <lang>Pali</lang> and <lang>BHS</lang> differ from <lang>Skt.</lang> in the names assigned to the four individuals, except that Kuvera (Kubera), oftener called Vaiśravaṇa (<lang>Pali</lang> Vessavaṇa), sometimes Dhanada or other synonyms, is the guardian of the north, as usually in <lang>Skt.</lang> The other three (in <lang>Skt.</lang> normally Indra, Yama, and Varuṇa) are {@Dhṛtarāṣṭra@} (east), {@Virūḍhaka@} (rarely Virūḍha; south), and {@Virūpākṣa@} (west; <lang>Pali</lang> Dhataraṭṭha, Virūḷha(ka), and Virūpakkha). These four are named, <ls>Dharmas</ls> 〔7〕 (as lokapāla); <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔3146—9〕 (as <ab>prec.</ab>, lokapālaḥ 〔3145〕); <ls>SP</ls> 〔4.6—7〕; <ls>LV</ls> 〔217.16, 20〕; 〔218.4, 9〕; <ls>Suv</ls> 〔64.2—3〕; <ls>LV</ls> 〔382.19 ff.〕 (names 〔384.6, 10, 11, 15, 16, 20, 21〕; 〔385.3〕); <ls>LV</ls> 〔388.3, 389.1, 21, 390.19〕 = <ls>Mv</ls> 〔iii.306.9, 307.13, 308.13, 309.13〕; <ls>Mmk</ls> 〔654.21〕; the four are mentioned as devādhipatayaḥ among the kāmāvacara gods whom Māra perceives as favoring the Bodhisattva, <ls>LV</ls> 〔302.5—6〕; and elsewhere in lists of other gods, <ab>e.g.</ab> <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.245.8—9〕 where Vaiśravaṇa is separated in the list of gods from the other three; sometimes not all are named, thus only Virūḍhaka, Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Kubera (with other gods) <ls>Mmk</ls> 〔434.27—435.1〕; only Vaiśravaṇa and Virūḍhaka, both styled mahārājo, <ls>SP</ls> 〔398.8, 399.5〕; only Virūḍhaka <ls>Mv</ls> 〔iii.72.3〕 (mahārājo), and 〔84.1, 6〕; Indro Virūḍhakādyāś ca (-ādya including the other three?) <ls>Laṅk</ls> 〔367.14〕; in some of the above the fact is mentioned that Vaiśravaṇa is lord of the yakṣas, Dhṛtarāṣṭra of gandharvas, Virūḍhaka of kumbhāṇḍas, and Virūpākṣa of nāgas; these functions, rather than their position as mahārāja (lokapāla), are exclusively noted in <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔3367〕 (Vaiśr°, first of yakṣas), 〔3381〕 (Dhṛt°, of gandharvas), 〔3437〕 (Virūḍhaka, of kumbhāṇḍas; no similar statement in <ls>Mvy</ls> about Virūp°); and, in longer lists of gods, also in <ls>Divy</ls> 〔126.6—8〕; 〔148.18—19〕; <ls>Av</ls> 〔i.108.8—9〕; <ls>Gv</ls> 〔249.24—250.2〕. The forms of the names Dhṛtarāṣṭra and Virūpākṣa never vary; Virūḍha without final -ka (also recorded in <lang>Pali</lang>) has been noted only <ls>Av</ls> 〔i.108.9〕 and <ls>Mmk</ls> 〔654.21〕 (here <ab>vs</ab>, perhaps <ab>m.c.</ab>); Vaiśravaṇa (see also °ramaṇa) is the regular form, for which Kuvera or Kub° is found <ls>Dharmas</ls> 〔7〕; <ls>LV</ls> 〔218.9〕; 〔390.19〕 = <ls>Mv</ls> 〔iii.309.13〕; <ls>Mmk</ls> 〔435.1〕; Dhanada <ls>Divy</ls> 〔126.8〕; 〔148.19〕; <ls>Av</ls> 〔i.108.9〕; yakṣarāṭ <ls>Mmk</ls> 〔654.21〕 (<ab>vs</ab>). In <ls>MSV</ls> 〔i.258.2 ff.〕 Dhṛtarāṣṭra and Virūḍhaka are said to be Aryans (ārya-jātīya), Virūpākṣa and Vaiśravaṇa Dasyus; the Buddha resolves to convert them using āryā vāc and dasyu-vāc respectively; he then preaches to the first two in normal <lang>BHS</lang>, to the others (〔259.2, 7〕) using what appear to be meaningless groups of sounds. ({@2@}) <ab>n.</ab> of a former Buddha: °jaḥ <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.138.14〕.
