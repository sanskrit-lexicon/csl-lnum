{@ucchaṅkha-, ucchaṅga-, utsaṅga-@}¦, <ab>cpd.</ab> with -pāda (or -caraṇa), (= <lang>Pali</lang> ussaṅkha-pāda,) <ab>ep.</ab> of a mahā-puruṣa [Page118-b] (<ab>esp.</ab> Buddha), <ab>no.</ab> 7 of the 32 lakṣaṇa; <ab>orig.</ab> form, <ab>etym.</ab>, and <ab>mg.</ab> obscure; <ab>acc.</ab> to <lang>Pali</lang> <ls>DN comm.</ls> 〔ii.446.28 ff.〕 it means that the soles of the feet can be seen as they walk, because ‘the ankles are fixed high’; if from utsaṅga, {%having feet characterized by a ‘lap’%} (an up-curve under the foot, making the sole visible?). <lang>Tib.</lang> on <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔260〕 says {%having the ankle-bone%} (or, {%joint of the ankle-bone%}) {%not visible%} (so one <lang>Chin.</lang> version, and <lang>Jap.</lang>); but <lang>Tib.</lang> on <ls>Bbh</ls> 〔375.14〕, cited by <ls>Wogihara</ls>, {%having feet not uneven%}; another <lang>Chin.</lang> gloss (also cited in <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔260〕, and elsewhere, <ls>Burnouf</ls> infra) refers the epithet to the {%knees%}; <ls>Gv</ls> 〔399.24〕 glosses suvyaktaparamopaśobhitopari-pādacchavikusumagarbhātirekaprabhāsvarā (not very clear or specific). These northern interpretations make the impression of floundering in a morass of ignorance. See <ls>Burnouf, Lotus</ls>, 〔573〕.
