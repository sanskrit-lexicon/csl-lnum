{@kāma-guṇa@}¦, <lex>m.</lex> <ab>pl.</ab> (= <lang>Pali</lang> <ab>id.</ab>, defined as {%the objects of the five senses%}, <ab>e.g.</ab> <ls>AN</ls> 〔iii.411.4 ff.〕), {%qualities of desire%}, regularly five in number, in some passages clearly understood as the objects of the five senses as in <lang>Pali</lang>; so <ls>Mv</ls> 〔iii.417.2〕 pañca kāmaguṇe (<ab>n. pl.</ab>; read °ṇā?) loke manaḥṣaṣṭhā praveditā (so read with <ab>mss.</ab>), tatra me vigato chando …; <ls>SP</ls> 〔79.8〕 (mā … 'bhiramadhvaṃ hīneṣu) rūpaśabda-gandha-rasa-sparśeṣu; atra hi yūyaṃ traidhātuke 'bhiratāḥ pañca-kāmaguṇa-sahagatayā tṛṣṇayā dahyatha; yet in <ls>Mv</ls> 〔ii.116.17〕 pañca kāmaguṇāṃ (<lex>acc.</lex> <ab>pl.</ab>), specifically listed as nāṭyaṃ gītaṃ vāditaṃ tūryaṃ striyo; the fact seems to be that the phrase became a stock formula or cliché, often used without definite association with any list; so <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔5378, 7373〕 mentions the 5 kāmaguṇa, but never lists them (in 〔871〕 even the number 5 is lacking).
