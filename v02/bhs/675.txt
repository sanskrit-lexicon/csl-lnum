<L>675<pc>023,1<k1>anAyUha<k2>an-AyUha
{@an-āyūha@}¦, <lex>adj.</lex> (<lang>Pali</lang> <ab>id.</ab>; see also {@anāvyūha@}), {%effortless; free from exertion or striving%}; usually <ab>cpd.</ab> with {@aniryūha@}, {%without abandonment, riddance, giving up%}; the two terms together seem clearly to be opposites, and to mean about the same as a-pravṛtti, a-nivṛtti, {%without activity or abstention from it%} (so <ls>Suzuki</ls>, ‘neither taking birth nor … going out’, on <ls>Laṅk</ls> 〔115.11—12〕; 〔196.3〕). However, <lang>Tib.</lang> (<ab>e.g.</ab> on <ls>LV</ls> 〔423.4〕 and on <ls>Laṅk</ls>) renders an-āyūha by <tib>blaṅ ba med pa</tib>, or the like, and aniryūha by <tib>dor ba med pa</tib>, which seem most naturally to mean {%without%} (intellectual) {%acceptance%} or {%rejection%} respectively; <ls>LaVallée Poussin, note on MadhK</ls> 〔517.20〕, see {@āvyūhati@}, gives his <lang>Tib.</lang> versions as <tib>mi len</tib> and <tib>mi ḥdor</tib> (which are equivalent to the above), and equates ā(v)yūha and nir(v)yūha with <lang>Skt.</lang> samāropa and apavāda. I find no support in <lang>BHS</lang> texts for this interpretation; whether the <lang>Tib.</lang> terms must necessarily be so understood I do not venture to say. Without aniryūha the word occurs <ls>Gv</ls> 〔17.13〕 anāyūha-sarvajñatā-bhūmi-gagana-vīryāḥ (of Bodhisattvas); anāyūhān 〔25.19〕 (<ab>id.</ab>), {%effortless, unstriving%} (in complimentary sense, like {@anābhoga@}; substantially {%unparticipating, impassive%}); anāyūhaviyūho (read with <ed>2d ed.</ed> °viyūha-, <ab>cpd.</ab> with next, if not niryūha-)-gatir bodhisattvānāṃ kāyacittāsaṃpravaṇatayā (see {@asaṃpravaṇa@}) 〔525.11〕, {%the course of <ab n="Bodhisattva">B.</ab>ʼs is free from effort and striving, because they are not interested in%} (their own) {%bodies or minds%}; anāyūhāniryūha- <ls>LV</ls> 〔423.4〕 (-cakram); °ham aniryūham (dharmacakram) <ls>LV</ls> 〔436.13〕; apratiṣṭhānāyūhāniryūha(ḥ) <ls>LV</ls> 〔424.7—8〕 (tathāgataḥ); anāyūhāniryūhāḥ (sarvadharmāḥ) <ls>Laṅk</ls> 〔115.11—12〕; °hāniryūha-tā (<ab>sc.</ab> sarvadharmāṇām) <ls>ŚsP</ls> 〔283.3〕 (text by error °niyūhatā).
