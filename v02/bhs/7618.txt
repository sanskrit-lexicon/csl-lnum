{@dvāra-koṣṭhaka@}¦, <lex>m.</lex> (= <lang>Pali</lang> °koṭṭhaka; <ab>cf.</ab> {@caraṇakoṣṭha@}), <ab>lit.</ab> {%gate-room%}; a room, or (often) roofed but open space, over a gate or entrance (to a private house, religious [Page274-a] edifice, or city); such spaces, guarded by railings and covered but open in front, are seen in the ‘cave-temples’ of Ajanta <ab>etc.</ab> Also, perhaps by extension, seems to be used in the sense of {%gate, entrance%}; and sometimes it is hard to say which is meant: °ke sthitvā <ls>Divy</ls> 〔17.12〕, {%at the gate%} (entrance, to a house); bhagavāṃs tasya (<ab>sc.</ab> of a private person, at his house) dvārakoṣṭhakam anuprāptaḥ; dauvārikapuruṣeṇāsya niveditaṃ, bhagavān dvāre (note! = °koṣṭhake) tiṣṭhatīti <ls>Av</ls> 〔i.31.10〕, {%is standing at the gate%}; <ls>Divy</ls> 〔535.11 ff.〕, here <ab>app.</ab> a city-gate; bahirdvārakoṣṭhaka (= <lang>Pali</lang> bahidvārakoṭṭhaka), {%the space outside the gate%}: °kasyaikānte <ls>Bhīk</ls> 〔3b.2〕 (here <ab>prob.</ab> of the vihāra-gate); jetavanaṃ gataḥ … dvāra°ke sthitvāgaruṃ dhūpitavān <ls>Av</ls> 〔i.24.2〕; in the last it is hard to say whether the gateway, or the space over it, is meant; <ab>app.</ab> of the space over the entrance to a vihāra, °ke pañcagaṇḍakaṃ cakraṃ kārayitavyam <ls>Divy</ls> 〔300.8, 9, 25〕; (stūpasya …) catvāro °kā māpitāḥ <ls>Divy</ls> 〔244.17〕; (dvitīyamaṇḍale) °ke <ls>Mmk</ls> 〔47.26〕; others, <ls>Jm</ls> 〔19.17〕; 〔20.1〕; <ls>MSV</ls> 〔i.168.12〕 <ab>etc.</ab>
