{@Śuklodana@}¦ (<lang>Pali</lang> Sukko°), <ab>n.</ab> of a brother of Śuddhodana: <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.352.13〕; 〔355.19〕; 〔iii.176.14〕 (father of Ānanda, Upadhāna, and Devadatta); <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔3600〕. In <ls>Mv</ls> 〔iii.177.1〕, <ab>acc.</ab> to <ab>mss.</ab>, another Śuklodana, also brother of Śuddhodana, is named (just after 〔iii.176.14〕 above), as the father of Nandana and Nandika (<ab>v.l.</ab> Nandaka); <ls>Senart</ls> <ab>em.</ab> to Śukrodana, referring to <ls>Kern, Der Buddhismus</ls> 〔310〕, who alleges among Śuddhodanaʼs brothers, besides Śuklodana, also ‘Śukrodana or Śuklodana’. The source of <ls>Kern</ls>ʼs statement is not clear to me; it would have to be a northern (<lang>Skt.</lang> or <lang>BHS</lang>) text; both forms would be Sukkodana in <lang>MIndic</lang>. In any case I think that <ls>Mv</ls> 〔iii.177.1〕 has carelessly substituted a repetition of Śuklo° for Dhautodana of 〔i.352.13〕 and 〔355.20〕 (or Droṇodana of <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔3601〕), the correct name of the fourth of the brothers, otherwise missing in this passage.
