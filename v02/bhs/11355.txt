In <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔1847〕 bhautika-rūpam appears to be parallel and complementary to 〔1846〕 {@upādāya-rūpam@}, <ab>q.v.</ab>; this suggests that bhautikarūpam = <lang>Pali</lang> bhūta-rūpa (<ls>Childers</ls> and <ls>PTSD</ls> <ab>s.v.</ab> rūpa), which <ab>acc.</ab> to <ls>Vism.</ls> = the four mahābhūtāni, earth, water, fire, and air (listed <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔1838—1841〕), contrary to <ls>Dharmas</ls> and the <lang>Chin.</lang> as cited by <ls>Suzuki</ls>, above; for the viṣayas are included among the 24 upādā(ya)-rūpa of <lang>Pali</lang>. On the basis of <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔1846—7〕 we might conjecture that in <ls>Laṅk</ls> 〔17.5〕 (see {@abhauti@}) abhautika = upādāya(-rūpam), and bhautika = bhautika (<lang>Pali</lang> bhūta)-rūpam. But if <ls>Dharmas</ls> and <lang>Chin.</lang> are right, bhautika would mean virtually the opposite of <lang>Pali</lang> bhūta(-rūpa).
