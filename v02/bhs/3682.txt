{@upadhi@}¦ (<lex>m.</lex>; = <lang>Pali</lang> upadhi, and also <lang>Pali</lang> upādi), ({@1@}) {%substratum%} of continued existence; {%attachment, bond%} uniting one to existence. <ab>Acc.</ab> to <ls>Childers</ls> upādi means the khandhas alone, while upadhi includes also kilesa (with which <ls>PTSD</ls> makes it ‘almost synonymous’), kāma, and kamma; but <ab>acc.</ab> to <ls>PTSD</ls> upadhi is sometimes equated with the pañca-kkhandhā. In <lang>Pali</lang>, upādi is, <ab>acc.</ab> to <ls>PTSD</ls>, used only in <ab>comp.</ab> with -sesa, in <ab>cpds.</ab> usually beginning sa- or an- and regularly epithets of nibbāna (-dhātu); these are represented in <lang>BHS</lang> by {@anupadhiśeṣa, nirupa°, sopa°@}, <ab>qq.v.</ab> But <lang>BHS</lang> also has upadhi and {@nir-up°@} (<ab>m.c.</ab> {@niropadhi@}) = <lang>Pali</lang> (nir-)upadhi. The passages here listed belong exclusively to this latter class, = <lang>Pali</lang> upadhi. (But it seems that even in <lang>Pali</lang>, upadhi and upādi are not always clearly distinguished.) upadhī-kṣīṇā <ls>LV</ls> 〔358.18〕 (<ab>vs</ab>); sarvopadhi-pratiniḥsarga {%the getting rid of all up°%} <ls>LV</ls> 〔31.21〕; <ls>Mv</ls> 〔ii.285.20〕; 〔iii.314.4〕; sarvopadhi-niḥsarga (<ab>Bhvr.</ab>, with dharma) <ls>LV</ls> 〔392.11〕; 〔395.21〕; sarvopadhikṣaya- <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.115.8〕; <ab>cf.</ab> 〔ii.418.10〕 upadhi (<ab>mss.</ab>, <ls>Senart</ls> <ab>em.</ab> °dhiṃ) pratītya duḥkhasya saṃbhavo sarvaśopadhikṣayato (<ab>mss.</ab>, <ls>Senart</ls> <ab>em.</ab> sarvopa°) … nāsti duḥkhasya saṃbhavo; <ls>Mv</ls> 〔iii.282.6〕 upadhi-saṃkṣaye; <ls>Divy</ls> 〔224.20〕 śalyam upadhiṃ viditvā; <ls>Ud</ls> 〔ii.20〕 upadhiṃ hi loke śalyam iti matvā, {%<fr>lʼattachement … cʼest la misère …</fr>%} Others <ab>s.v.</ab> {@niropadhi@}. In <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔6499〕 upadhi has three <lang>Tib.</lang> definitions; the first, <tib>phuṅ po</tib>, regularly = {@skandha@} (as <lang>Pali</lang> upādi = khandha); the third, <tib>ñon moṅs pa</tib>, regularly = {@kleśa@} (as <lang>Pali</lang> upadhi, ‘almost <ab>syn.</ab> with kilesa,’ <ls>PTSD</ls>); while the second, <tib>rdzas</tib>, {%thing, substance, matter%}, belongs to a meaning of the word <ab>app.</ab> unknown to <lang>Pali</lang>, <ab>viz.</ab> ({@2@}) {%material thing, ‘<fr>chose matérielle</fr>’%} (<ls>Lévi</ls>, = <lang>Tib.</lang> <tib>dṅos</tib>, which also = <lang>Skt.</lang> vastu), <ls>Sūtrāl.</ls> 〔xvii.3〕 (〔n. 1〕 in <ls>Transl.</ls>); see also <ls>LaVallée Poussin, AbhidhK.</ls> 〔iv.15 with n. 1〕: ‘<fr>Par upadhi, il faut entendre la chose</fr> (ārāma, vihāra, <ab>etc.</ab>) <fr>donnée à un moine ou au Saṃgha: le mérite qui procède (tadbhava) de cet upadhi sʼappelle</fr> [Page136-a] {@aupadhika@}’ (<ab>q.v.</ab>). Hence, ({@3@}) in <ls>Divy</ls> 〔50.28〕 bhagavān upadhau vartate, {%the Lord was acting in regard to material things%} (of the assembly of monks), <ab>i.e.</ab> in the function of an {@upadhi-vārika@}, <ab>q.v.</ab> (= {@aupadhike@} <ls>Divy</ls> 〔542.17〕).
