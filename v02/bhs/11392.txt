{@maṅku, maṅgu, madgu@}¦, <lex>adj.</lex>, also in <ab>comp.</ab> with bhāva, -bhūta (= <lang>Pali</lang> maṅku; <ab>cf.</ab> also {@durmaṅku@}; maṅku once in <lang>Vedic</lang>, <ls>ŚB</ls>, <ab>app.</ab> {%staggering%}, <ls>BR</ls>, but <ab>perh.</ab> not the same word): {%mentally disturbed, upset, abashed, out of countenance%}; all three forms are clearly variants of each other (<ab>cf.</ab> pudgala: puṃgala <ab>etc.</ab>, 〔§ 3.4〕), as is shown notably by a cliché, tūṣṇībhūto (once °tvā, <ls>Divy</ls> 〔633.24〕; not in <ls>Mvy</ls>) madgubhūtaḥ srastaskandhaḥ adhomukho niṣpratibhānaḥ (<ls>Divy</ls> °pratibhaḥ; in <ls>Mvy</ls> before adho°) pradhyānaparamaḥ (<ls>Mvy</ls> °paraḥ) <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔7122—26〕; <ls>Divy</ls> 〔633.24, 27〕 (here <lex>accs.</lex>); 〔636.7〕; <ls>Av</ls> 〔i.48.10〕, in which, for madgu- of the other texts, <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔7122〕 reads maṅgu-, or with <ls>Mironov</ls> maṅku-(<ab>v.l.</ab> maṅgu-). The form madgu also in <ls>Av</ls> 〔i.286.5〕 vyāpadyate madguḥ pratitiṣṭhati kopaṃ saṃjanayati; in <ls>MSV</ls> 〔ii.86.2〕 madguvo (<ab>n. pl.</ab> <lex>f.</lex>); see also {@amadgu@}; and in <ls>Bbh</ls> 〔123.10〕 (na ca bodhisattvo) yācanakam avahasati … na madgubhāvam (so <ab>ms.</ab>, <ab>ed.</ab> <ab>em.</ab> maṅku°) asyopasaṃharati; but maṅku elsewhere in <ls>Bbh</ls>, (bhūtaṃ ca) doṣaṃ (of someone else) pratichādayati, na vivṛṇoti, yenāsya syān maṅkubhāvaḥ 〔254.15〕, {%so that he would be embarrassed%}; maṅku-bhā(vam …, lacuna) 〔150.4〕, filled by <lang>Tib.</lang> <tib>bag ḥkhums pa</tib>, {%timidity, ‘little-mindedness’%}, and elsewhere: maṅkur bhavati, <ls>Hoernle, JRAS</ls> 〔1916.711〕 (= <lang>Pali</lang> <ls>Sn</ls> 〔818〕 maṅku hoti), {%is upset, disturbed%} (by the criticism of others); abhīru acchambhina-m (! <ab>n. sg.</ab> <lex>m.</lex> required; ‘hiatus-bridging’ m?) a-maṅku-bhūtaḥ dṛḍhavīryaḥ <ls>Mmk</ls> 〔93.24〕. There seems to be even, once, an apparently related {@madgībhūta@}, <ab>q.v.</ab>, implying a stem madga, but this is doubtful.
