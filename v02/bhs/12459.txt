{@yāna@}¦, <lex>nt.</lex> (sometimes with <lex>m.</lex> endings), {%vehicle%}, as in <lang>Skt.</lang> and <lang>Pali</lang>; in <lang>Pali</lang> also used of the 8-fold Noble Path, as the {%vehicle%} to salvation; by extension of this use, in <lang>BHS</lang> applied to the two vehicles ({@mahā°, hīna°@}), or three, with pratyeka(buddha)-yāna between the two; that is, {%religious methods%}, within the fold of Buddhism. See <ls>SP</ls> 〔75.11, 76.2 ff.〕 (parable of the burning house, the 3 yānas compared to {%carts%} of different sizes); for mahā-y° synonyms are buddha-y°, bodhisattva-y°, eka-y° (because, <ls>SP</ls> 〔40.13 f.〕 says, this is really the {%only vehicle%}, na kiṃcic … dvitīyaṃ vā tṛtīyaṃ vā yānaṃ saṃvidyate); eka-y° also <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔1255〕; {@agra-yāna@}, <ab>q.v.</ab>, <ab>id.</ab>; triyānam ekayānaṃ ca <ls>Laṅk</ls> 〔155.14〕; the 3 yānas mentioned, but not named, <ls>Mv</ls> 〔ii.362.8 f.〕, where it is specifically stated that one can attain parinirvāṇa by any of them, and no preference is expressed; in <ls>SP</ls> 〔43.7〕 (in times of corruption, the Tathāgatas) upāyakauśalyena tad evaikaṃ buddhayānaṃ triyānanirdeśena nirdiśanti; synonym of hīna-y° is also {@śrāvaka-y°@}; see the various terms, also {@nava-(acira-)yāna-saṃprasthita@}.
