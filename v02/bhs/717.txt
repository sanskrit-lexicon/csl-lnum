{@aniñjya@}¦: variants {@āniñja, an°, āniñjya, ānijya, āneñj(y)a, an°@}, <ab>qq.v.</ab> The nasalless ānijya occurs without <ab>v.l.</ab> only once and may be an accidental corruption (anusvāra omitted); but see 〔§ 2.73〕. The omission of y after j is called ‘<fr>une orthographe prâcritisante</fr>’ by <ls>Senart</ls>, <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.399〕; at any rate it occurs frequently in the <ab>mss.</ab> In <lang>Pali</lang> the same word is written ānejja, ānañja, āneñja, and with short initial a- (stigmatized as wrong reading in <ls>CPD</ls>); forms with i in the <ab>penult</ab> are very rare in <lang>Pali</lang>, but <ls>CPD</ls> records aniñja-ppatta once; this surely cannot be separated from the common āneñja- (ānejja-, ānañja-)ppatta. <lang>BHS</lang> has no ānañj- or ānejj-, which are not infrequent in <lang>Pali</lang>. The wild variety of spellings in <lang>Pali</lang> itself shows that it early became an obscure word in the tradition. <lang>BHS</lang> occurrences of the spelling aniñjya include <ls>SP</ls> 〔161.10〕; <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔554〕, <ls>Śikṣ</ls> 〔237.1〕 (and 〔223.3, 4〕 in the <ab>ms.</ab>); perhaps <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.133.13〕 (<ls>Senart</ls> āniñjya, <ab>em.</ab>, <ab>mss.</ab> mostly anīca [{%not low?%}], one aniṃca, perhaps intending aniñj[y]a); either an- or ān- (uncertain because of saṃdhi) <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.228.11, 15〕; 〔229.6〕; 〔ii.132.15〕 (here <ab>v.l.</ab> °nijya-). For examples of the other spellings see <ab>s.vv.</ab> There seems to be no difference [Page024-b] in <ab>mg.</ab> between the various spellings; any of them may be an <lex>adj.</lex>, {%immobile, immovable%}, or a <lex>nt.</lex> <lex>n.</lex>, {%immovability%}. As <lex>adj.</lex>, āniṃjehi vihārehi <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.34.11〕; 〔ii.419.11〕, {%in immovable conditions%}; <ab>ep.</ab> of citta <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.133.13〕 (see above on form); oftener °niñjyaprāpta, of citta, <ls>LV</ls> 〔344.6〕; <ls>Mv</ls> 〔i.228.11, 15〕; 〔229.6〕; 〔ii.132.15〕; aniñja-prāpta, of citta, <ls>SP</ls> 〔5.10〕; aniñjya-bhūta, of citta, <ls>SP</ls> 〔161.10〕; ānijyāṃ śāntim <ls>Av</ls> 〔ii.199.5〕; aniñjyo (<ls>ŚsP</ls> aniñjo) nāma samādhiḥ <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔554〕; <ls>ŚsP</ls> 〔1418.22〕; virajasko (a)neñjyaś ca <ls>Mmk</ls> 〔476.14〕; clearly noun, āniñjyam <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔6387〕 = <lang>Tib.</lang> <tib>mi gyo ba</tib>, {%not moving%}; doubtless noun also in <ab>cpds.</ab> with -prāpta (<lang>Pali</lang> -ppatta) and -bhūta, above; apparently primarily <lex>adj.</lex> as third of a triad, the first two members of which are puṇya and apuṇya, or kuśala and akuśala; all three are applied to {%deeds%} (karman), or the {%performance%} or {%accumulation%} thereof ({@abhisaṃskāra@}, <ab>q.v.</ab>), or the {%underlying conditions%} (saṃskāra; <ab>cf.</ab> <ls>Śāl</ls> 〔81.5〕, abhisaṃskārārthena saṃskārāḥ), or finally the {%states of existence%} (dharmāḥ) to which such various acts lead as fruit. The first two, meritorious and demeritorious, relate to good and bad deeds as leading to rebirth in the kāmāvacara and (only good deeds) in the rūpāvacara states, or to these resulting states; the word aniñj(y)a, aneñj(y)a, or ā°, refers to deeds leading to rebirth in the arūpāvacara states, or to those states themselves (the dhyāna states), <ab>cf.</ab> <ls>Bbh</ls> 〔90.16〕 āniñjya-caturtha-dhyāna-vihāraḥ; on the subject in <lang>Pali</lang> see <ls>Warren, B. in Tr.</ls> 〔179 f.〕 (from <ls>Vism.</ls>), and in general <ls>LaVallée-Poussin</ls>, note on <ls>MadhK</ls> 〔334.8〕; a good <lang>Pali</lang> passage is <ls>DN</ls> 〔iii.217.25〕 tayo saṃkhārā, puññābhisaṃkhāro, apuññā°, ānañjā° (see <ab>Comm.</ab> 〔iii.998.29〕). In <lang>BHS</lang>, <ls>Śāl</ls> 〔82.3—4〕 (cited <ls>Śikṣ</ls> 〔222.11—223.1〕) evam avidyāyāṃ satyāṃ trividhāḥ saṃskārā abhinivartante, puṇyopagā apuṇyo° āneñjyo° (<ls>Śikṣ</ls> āniñjyo°); <ls>Dbh</ls> 〔48.7〕 puṇyāpuṇyāneñjyān abhisaṃskārān; karmāṇi kuśalākuśalāneñjāni <ls>MadhK</ls> 〔334.7—8〕 (<ab>cf.</ab> 〔543.1〕 with note); (dharmāḥ) kuśalā vā 'kuśalā vā aniñjyā vā <ls>Śikṣ</ls> 〔237.1〕.
