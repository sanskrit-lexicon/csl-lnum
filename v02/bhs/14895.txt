{@Śākī@}¦, <ab>n.</ab> of a brahman woman, <ab>app.</ab> an ascetic, who entertained the Bodhisattva: atha bodhisattvo yenaiva Śākyā brāhmaṇyā āśramas tenopasaṃkrāmat; sā bodhisattvaṃ vāsena bhaktena copanimantrayate sma <ls>LV</ls> 〔238.5〕 (prose); see <ls>Weller</ls> 〔29〕. But <lang>Tib.</lang> lacks the name: <tib>bram ze rigs ldan</tib> (see below) <tib>kyi gnas</tib>, {%the dwelling of a person of brahman family(?)%}. Note that immediately after this, line 7, the Bodhisattva visits and is entertained by another brahman woman, named {@Padmā@} (this time so named in <lang>Tib.</lang>); the two statements are closely parallel. The <lang>Tib.</lang> <tib>rigs ldan</tib> could mean {%noble%}, or represent a <lang>Skt.</lang> <ab>n. pr.</ab> such as Kulikā.
