{@samāpatti@}¦, <lex>f.</lex>, primarily (as in <lang>Skt.</lang>) {%attainment%} (to {@samāpadyate@}, <lang>Skt.</lang> and <lang>BHS</lang>); <ab>esp.</ab> used (as in <lang>Pali</lang>) of the {%attainment%} of the nine successive {@anupūrvavihāra@} (<ab>q.v.</ab> for list) or ‘successive states induced by the ecstatic meditation’ (<ls>Childers</ls>, who lists only eight, as sometimes in <lang>Pali</lang>; add as ninth [saññāvedayita-] nirodha-sam°); these states are also called {@samādhi@}; navānupūrvasamādhi-samāpattayaḥ <ls>Dharmas</ls> 〔82〕 clearly = {%attainment of the nine successive samādhi%}; so also <ls>Laṅk</ls> 〔100.9〕 samādhisamāpatty-adhiṣṭhānena, see {@adhiṣṭhāna@}; and in some other such <ab>cpds.</ab> But from this old association, in <lang>Pali</lang> and <lang>BHS</lang> alike, the {%attainment%} comes to mean the {%state attained%}, <ab>viz.</ab> one of the above nine (in <lang>Pali</lang> sometimes only eight, the nirodha-sa° being omitted) ‘modes of abstract meditation’ (<ls>Hardy ap. Childers</ls>), or {%<fr>recueillements</fr>%}, <ls>LaV-P.</ls> in <ls>AbhidhK</ls>, see <ab>esp.</ab> 〔viii.182 n. 4〕 for a report of discordant Buddhist attempts to distinguish between {@samādhi@} and {@samāpatti@}. The fact seems to be that these two words are fundamentally and substantially identical in <ab>mg.</ab>, and that the attempts to differentiate are scholastic pedantry. They are however used in dvandva <ab>cpds.</ab>, sometimes with dhyāna too (note that the 4 {@dhyāna@} are included in the 9 samāpatti): dhyāna-samādhi-°ttīnāṃ <ls>LV</ls> 〔244.3〕, <lang>Tib.</lang> <tib>bsam gtan (dhyāna) daṅ tiṅ ṅe ḥdzin</tib> (samādhi) <tib>daṅ sñom par ḥjug pa</tib>, as a three-member dvandva. This is the regular <lang>Tib.</lang> rendering of samāpatti, [Page570-a] {%entering%} (<tib>ḥjug pa</tib>) {%into equanimity, evenness or calmness of mind%} (<tib>sñom par</tib>). This <lang>Tib.</lang> expression is, however, not always parallel with <tib>tiṅ ṅe ḥdzin</tib> = samādhi, which may depend upon it, as in <ls>LV</ls> 〔3.11〕 where samādhiṃ samāpanno 'bhūt, containing the cognate <lex>ppp.</lex>, is rendered <tib>tiṅ ṅe ḥdzin la sñoms par zhugs par gyur to</tib>, {%arrived at equanimity-entrance into samādhi%}, or {%at entrance into equanimity%} (leading) {%to samādhi%}. <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔1491—1502〕 contains eleven (in <ls>Mironov</ls> only ten, 〔1499〕 being omitted) miscellaneous and unsystematic ‘{%names of%} (various sorts of) {%samāpatti%}’; 〔1492—5〕 are brief statements of the ārūpyasamā° (see {@ārūpya@} for other citations); the others are {@vyutkrāntaka-sa°, vyāskandaka-sa°@}, (nava-){@anupūrvavihārasa°, samāpatti-skandhaḥ@} (<ab>om.</ab> <ls>Mironov</ls>), {@nirodha-@} (more fully {@saṃjñāvedayita-nirodha-@}){@sa°, mahābhūtasamatāsādhanam@}, and {@asaṃjñā-sa°@}, <ab>qq.v.</ab>; (bodhisattvasya … sarvasamādhiparicayaviśeṣeṇa ca dhyānapramukhāni) sarvāṇi laukikāni lokottarāṇi samāpatti-śatāny āmukhībhavanti sma <ls>LV</ls> 〔244.21〕 ({%attainments%}, or more specifically {%abstract meditations, trances, <fr>recueillements</fr>?%} <lang>Tib.</lang> <tib>sñoms par ḥjug pa</tib>); in many other occurrences, like the last, I find it hard to draw a line between the two shades of <ab>mg.</ab>; (mārgo yas tvayā parigṛhītaḥ) samādhi-kauśala-samāpattyā <ls>Laṅk</ls> 〔11.1〕; sukhasamādhi-samāpatti-vihāras 〔12.7〕; (indriyabala-bodhyaṅga-dhyāna-vimokṣa-)-samādhi-samāpattibhiś ca mahatīṃ ratiṃ pratyanubhaviṣyatha <ls>SP</ls> 〔80.2〕; dhyānavimokṣa-samādhi-samāpattīr (<lex>acc.</lex> <ab>pl.</ab>) 〔82.2〕; <ab>etc.</ab> See also next items.
