{@anudharma@}¦ (= <lang>Pali</lang> anudhamma). <ab>Acc.</ab> to <ls>PTSD</ls>, <lang>Pali</lang> dhammānudhamma is used as a distributive or intensive <ab>cpd.</ab>, see under <hom>1</hom> {@anu@}, {%the Law in all its parts%}; while <ls>Childers</ls> understands anudh° as {%lesser or inferior dhamma%} (against which see <ls>Lévi on Sūtrāl.</ls> 〔xiii.1〕 <ab>comm.</ab>). <ab>Acc.</ab> to <ls>CPD</ls> anudhamma means {%(right) method%} and is a ‘hypostasis of anu-dhammaṃ’ (<lex>adv.</lex>, {%in accordance with dhamma%}). In <lang>BHS</lang> (as also in <lang>Pali</lang>, <ab>e.g.</ab> <ls>Dhp.</ls> 〔20〕) there is some evidence suggesting that dharmānudharma is equivalent to dharmasya anu°, perhaps {%the true method of the law%} or the like: <ls>Bbh</ls> 〔284.1〕 na pratipadyante dharmasyānudharmam, {%they do not attain (? practise) the anudh° of the dh°%}; 〔197.16〕 dharmasyānudharmaṃ pratipadyamānasya (<ab>sc.</ab> Bodhisattvasya; not agreeing with dharmasya, which depends on anudh°); <ls>Gv</ls> 〔383.5〕 dharmasya cānudharmaṃ pratipadye, {%and I attain (? practise) the anudh° of the dh°%}. Without dharma in the context, we find anudharma-cārin <ls>Divy</ls> 〔617.16〕 (°riṇī, <lex>f.</lex>), anudharma-praticārin <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔1123〕 (°rī). So we should probably interpret all <ab>cpds.</ab> of dharmānudharma-, which usually parallel the like <ab>cpds.</ab> in <lang>Pali</lang>: dharmānudharma-cārin <ls>Dbh</ls> 〔32.21〕; °rma-pratipatti, {%attainment (? practice) of the anudh° of the dh°%} <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔1801〕 (<ab>mss.</ab> erroneously omit dharma after anu; <ab>em.</ab> <ed>Kyoto ed.</ed>); <ls>Dbh</ls> 〔33.29〕; <ls>Bbh</ls> 〔204.7〕; <ls>KP</ls> 〔9.2〕; <ls>LV</ls> 〔32.16〕; °rmapratipanna <ls>Bbh</ls> 〔84.4〕; <ls>LV</ls> 〔179.12〕; 〔377.14〕; <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔1124〕. In <ls>Mvy</ls> 〔9321〕 are mentioned ṣaḍ anudharmāḥ, after 〔9320〕 ṣaḍ dharmāḥ; so also <ls>Bhīk</ls> 〔16b.4〕 ṣaṭsu dharmeṣu ṣaṭsv anudharmeṣu śikṣā; in neither place do I find any clue to what the six anudharma (or dharma) are. I have not noted any <lex>adv.</lex> anudharmaṃ (parallel to <lang>Pali</lang> anudhammaṃ).
