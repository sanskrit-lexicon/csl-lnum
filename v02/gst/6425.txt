{#aBizava#}¦ Tatpur. 1. m. ({#-vaH#}) <sup>1</sup> The religious act of pressing
out the juice of the Soma plant or Asclepias acida (see
also {#somABizava#}), or in default of this plant, of its substi-
tute, the Pūtīkā or Cæsalpinia bonducella (see also {#pUtI-#}
{#kABizava#}); it constitutes an essential ceremony of the
Soma sacrifices (see {#jyotizwoma#}), and is performed the day
after the sacrificial acts called {#upasad#} (q. v.), by means
of stones ({#grAvan#}) which press on two boards (called
{#aDizavaRa#}); the act takes place in the southern part of a
building erected in the western part of the Uttaravedi near
a cart which is placed there and bears, like the building,
the name {#havirDAna#}. (Comp. e. g. {%Mādhava vv. ll.:%} {#yadi#}
