{#aparAnta#}¦ Karmadh. m. ({#-ntaH#}) The western extremity, the
western border, the western sea-shore; e. g. {#so'parAntezu#}
{#tIrTAni puRyAnyAyatanAni ca . tAni sarvAni gatvA sa praBAsa-#}
{#mupajagmivAn#} ({%Arjunam.%} {#aparAntezu = samudrasamIpezu paScimezu;#}
{%Nīlak.%} = {#paScimasamudratIrezu#}); or {#aparAntAdahaM kfzRa saMpratI-#}
{#hAgataH#} ({%Rāmānanda:%} {#aparAntAt = paScimasamudratIrAt#}).--
The plur. {#aparAntAH#} is the name of the country or the in-
habitants of the western borders.--{%Wilson%} ({%Viṣṇup.%} p.
189, n. 60) renders {#aparAntAH#} ‘the people not beyond the
borders’, as opposed to {#parAntAH#} ‘the people beyond the
borders’ (i. e. of {#parA#} and {#anta#}); but as {#para#} when opposed to
{#apara#} assumes meanings corresponding with those of {#pUrva#} (see
s. v. {#apara#} II.), {#parAntAH,#} as contradistinguished from {#apa-#}
{#rAntAH,#} may mean perhaps ‘the people of the eastern borders’
when {#aparAntAH#} would retain the sense given before. See
the following.--<sup>2</sup> Last term, completion, e. g. in the {%Yoga-%}
{%Sūtra:%} {#kzaRapratiyogI pariRAmo'parAntanirgrAhyaH kramaH#} ‘ma-
turity (or change) is the reverse of what is but a moment;
by the completion (of maturity) the order (of the moments)
becomes comprehensible’. <sup>3</sup> Death. See the Yoga term {#a-#}
{#parAntajYAna#} and comp. {#pUrvAnta#} and {#parAnta#}. E. {#apara#} and {#anta#}.
