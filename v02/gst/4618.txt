{#apadeSa#}¦ Tatpur. m. ({#-SaH#}) <sup>1</sup> Stating, adducing, as a reason
&c.; e. g. in the {%Nyāya S.%} {#hetvapadeSAtpratijYAyAH punarvacanaM#}
{#nigamanam#} (comm. {#apadeSaH kaTanam#}); or {#notpattikAraRAnapade-#}
{#SAt#} (comm. {#anapadeSAdakaTanAt#}). <sup>2</sup> An argument, a reason;
e. g. in the {%Nyāya S.%} {#upalaByamAne cAnupalabDerasattvAdanapadeSaH#}
(comm. {#heturanapadeSo'sADakaH#}); or in the {%Kaṇāda Sūtras:%}
{#arTAntaraM hyarTAntarasyAnapadeSaH;#} or {#aprasidDo'napadeSo'saM-#}
{#digDo vAnapadeSaH ..#}.--In the terminology of the {%Vaiśe-%}
{%shika%} philosophy {#apadeSa#} is the second of the five parts
of a syllogism; the same as the {#hetu#} of the Nyāya; com-
pare the instance s. v. {#anusanDAna#}.--{%Suśruta%} defines and
exemplifies it thus: {#anena kAraRenetyapadeSaH . yaTApadiSyate#}
{#maDureRa SlezmABivarData iti ..#}. <sup>3</sup> A pretence, a pretext, a
feint (this is the more usual meaning in which the word
occurs). <sup>4</sup> Disguise; also metaphorically; e. g. {#vikArApade-#}
{#SopadeSAtsaMSayaH#} (where the {#vikAra#} refers to the change
of vowels into semivowels). <sup>5</sup> A butt, a mark; e. g. {#labDa-#}
{#sApadeSaH#}. <sup>6</sup> Name, reputation; e. g. {#yaM svapadeSaM kamitaH sva-#}
{#jano'yaM prApya janapadeSaM#} &c. (comm. {#apadeSo nAma . tAdfSaM#}
{#SoBanAKyaM yaM#} &c.). <sup>7</sup> Place, quarter; e. g. {#tyaktApadeSo yatiH#}.
