{#antya#}¦ 1. m. f. n. ({#-ntyaH-ntyA-ntyam#}) <sup>1</sup> Last, ultimate. (In the
Vaiśeṣika philos., {#antyo viSezaH#} is called ‘the ultimate
degree of particularity or divisibility, which is the condition
of eternal substances only’, viz. the condition of simple,
non-compound substances, of atoms, since there is an end
of discrimination when this last degree of elementary
existence has been arrived at; compare also {#antyAvasTiti#}.)
<sup>2</sup> Final (as the letter of a word). <sup>3</sup> Nearest, e. g. {#navAntya#}
nearest to nine i. e. ten. Comp. {#antima#} and the words
named s. v. {#antika#}. <sup>4</sup> Inferior, lowest, belonging to the
lowest tribe (see the following meanings). [Tatpur. com-
pounds--not Karmadh.--the latter part of which is
{#antya#} have the udātta accent on the first syllable.]
<div n="P">2. m. ({#-ntyaH#}) <sup>1</sup> A man of the lowest tribe. See {#antyaja#}.
<sup>2</sup> A Mlechchha or foreigner. See {#antyaja#}. <sup>3</sup> (In Astronomy.)
The twelfth or last lunar month, called Phālguna. <sup>4</sup> A
fragrant grass (Cyperus rotundus). See {#mustA#}. <sup>5</sup> m. pl.
({#-ntyAH#}) The name of a people or country; read also {#anDAH#}
or {#anDrAH#} or {#aDyAH#}.
<div n="P">3. f. ({#-ntyA#}) <sup>1</sup> A woman of the lowest tribe. See {#antyaja#}.

<div n="P">4. n. ({#-ntyam#}) <sup>1</sup> (In Arithm.) A measure of number,
