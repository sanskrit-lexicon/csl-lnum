{#anyapadArTa#}¦ Tatpur. m. ({#-rTaH#}) (In Grammar.) The sense of
another word; this term is used by Pāṇini and his com-
mentators to qualify the nature of a {%Bahuvrihi%} compound
which always depends on, or refers to, the meaning of an-
other word, the word {#anyapadArTa#} being thus used in contra-
distinction of {#svapadArTa;#} e. g. Patanjali: {#aTAnyagrahaRaM kima-#}
{#rTam . anekaM padArTe . itIyatyucyamAne svapadArTe'pi bahuvrIhiH#}
{#syAt . rAjapuruzaH . takzapuruza iti nEtadasti prayojanam . tatpu-#}
{#ruzaH svapadArTe bADako Bavizyati . ;#} Kaiyyaṭa: {#aTAnyagrahaRa-#}
{#miti . antaraNgatvAtsvapadArTe samAse sidDe padArTagrahaRasAmarTyA-#}
{#danyapadArTo lapsyata iti praSnaH . svapadArTe'pIti . apiSabdA-#}
{#danyapadArTe'pItyarTaH . padArTagrahaRaM vyAptisamASrayaRArTaM syA-#}
{#diti svapadArTAnyapadArTayoH samAsaH syAt . tatpuruza iti .#}
