{#agnimAruti#}¦ Karmadh. m. ({#-tiH#}) A name of the saint Agastya.
E. {#agni#} fire, i. e. the fire of the stomach, the digestive fa-
culty, and {#mAruti#} a name of Hanumat; because the saint
could digest as well as the monkey: the epithet alludes to
a story of his devouring and digesting Vātāpi, a demon,
who had assumed the form of a sheep.
