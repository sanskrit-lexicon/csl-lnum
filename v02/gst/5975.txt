{#aBijana#}¦ Tatpur. m. ({#-naH#}) <sup>1</sup> Native country; e. g. {%Bhaṭṭik.:%}
{#tataH pragaditA vAkyaM miTilABijanA nfpam#}. <sup>2</sup> The country
of the ancestors; (this is the sense given to the word by
{%Patanjali%} to {%Pāṇ.%} IV. 3. 90. in distinction from {#nivAsa,#} IV.
3. 89., which would mean according to him the country where
one lives oneself: {#aTa nivAsABijanayoH ko viSezaH . ni-#}
{#vAso nAma yatra saMpratyuzyate . aBijano nAma yatra pUrvEruzitam#}).
<sup>3</sup> The ancestors; (according to {%Kaiyyaṭa%}--hence {%Kāśikā%}
and {%Vardhamāna%}--on the preceding quotation: {#aBijana-#}
{#Sabdena pUrve bAnDavA ucyante#} &c.). <sup>4</sup> Race, family in general;
e. g. {%Lāṭyāyana:%} {#ya ezAmaDyayane'BikrAntitamaH syAdaBijanena#}
{#vA#} ({%Agnisvāmin:%} {#aBijanaH kulam#}). <sup>5</sup> The head or ornament
of a family. <sup>6</sup> Fame, notoriety. E. {#jan#} with {#aBi,#} kṛt
aff. {#GaY#} (according to the commentators of the {%Amarak.;%}
kṛt aff. {#ac,#} according to {%Bharatas.%} on the {%Bhaṭṭik.%}).
