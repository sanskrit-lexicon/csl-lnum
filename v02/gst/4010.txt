{#antyAvasAyin#}¦ Tatpur. m. ({#-yI#}) A man of a degraded tribe,
the offspring of a Niṣādī woman by a Chāṇḍāla male. Ac-
cording to Angiras this term comprehends seven classes, viz.
{#cARqAla, Svapaca, kzattf, sUta, vEdehaka, mAgaDa#} and {#Ayogava#}
qq. vv.; Vijnāneśvara applies it to Chāṇḍālas and men who
have become outcastes in general: {#‘cARqAlapatitAdInAm’#}.
Comp. {#antAvasAyin#} and {#antyaja#}. E. {#antya#} and {#avasAyin#}
lit. ‘engaged in the lowest occupations’.
