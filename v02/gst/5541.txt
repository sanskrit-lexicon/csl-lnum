{#apramodamAna#}¦ Tatpur. n. ({#-nam#}) (In the Sāṅkhya philosophy.)
The reverse of {#pramodamAna#} q. v., a technical term to denote
the incapacity of removing the pains that are produced by
superhuman, i. e. divine or atmospheric influences (cold,
heat, wind, rain, lightning &c.), by not attending for this
purpose a holy teacher (the removal of such pains being
required for the attainment of final liberation); it is amongst
the seventeen {#budDibaDa#} or obstructions of intellect one of
the eight which are the converse of the {#sidDi#} or perfections.
Comp. {#apramudita#} and {#apramoda#}. E. {#a#} neg. and {#apramodamAna#}.
