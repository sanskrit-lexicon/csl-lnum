{#aBi#}¦ ind. (see {#nipAta, upasarga, gati, karmapravacanIya#}) A particle
implying the notion of proximity, hence of conjunction,
relation, superiority, excess, in their literal and metapho-
rical acceptations (and thus becoming in many instances
synonymous as well with {#A, anu#} as with {#aDi, api,#}
{#ati#}); it is used either as a prefix to verbs, and as such,
in the Vedas and archaic passages of the classical literature,
also detached from the verb (very much in the same man-
ner as German prepositions when connected with verbs):
or as a separable preposition: or, in the Vedas and archaic
passages of the classical literature, as an adverb. With
nouns it may form Tatpur., Bahuvr. and Avyayībh. com-
pounds. It appears, too, in the protracted form {#aBI;#} e. g.
(ved.) {#aBI zu RaH,#} and comp. {#aBIka, aBIGAta, aBIzaNga#} &c.
<div n="P">1. ({%as a prefix to verbs%} it means) <sup>1</sup> Towards, to (im-
plying proximity); e. g. {#gam#} or {#i#} or {#yA,#} with {#aBi,#} ‘to
go towards, to approach’; {#kram#} or {#DAv,#} with {#aBi,#} ‘to
attack’; {#vah#} with {#aBi,#} ‘to carry towards’; {#vad#} with {#aBi,#}
‘to salute’; in many instances where the radical itself im-
plies this notion, it increases merely the power of the verbal
meaning and becomes often untranslateable in English; e. g.
{#pIq#} ‘to squeeze, to vex’ has the same meaning, but in an
increased degree, with {#aBi, grah#} with {#aBi#} means, like
{#grah,#} ‘to receive’; {#Barts#} ‘to reproach, to threaten’ has the
same sense with {#aBi; han#} with {#aBi#} means the same as
{#han#} &c. <sup>2</sup> Towards, for, against (implying relation, when
the same remark applies as before); e. g. {#laz#} with {#aBi,#}
‘to desire, to wish for’, {#rAD#} with {#aBi,#} ‘to be favourable
towards’, {#druh#} with {#aBi,#} ‘to have animosity against’;
{#yuj#} with {#aBi#} (esp. pass.), ‘to bring an action against,
to accuse’. <sup>3</sup> On, upon (implying contiguity and answer-
ing frequently the cognate English be-); e. g. {#su#} with {#aBi,#}
‘to pour the juice of the Soma upon’, {#sic#} with {#aBi,#}
‘to sprinkle upon, to be-sprinkle, {#jan#} with {#aBi,#} ‘to be
be-got’. <sup>4</sup> On, above, over (implying superiority or master-
ship); e. g. {#BU#} or {#as#} (cl. 2.) with {#aBi,#} ‘to overpower’;
{#as#} (cl. 1. or cl. 4.) with {#aBi,#} ‘to study’ (comp. {#gam, i#}
&c. with {#aDi#}).
