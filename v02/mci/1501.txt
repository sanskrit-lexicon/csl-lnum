{@Mandākinī<sup>1</sup>@}¦ f.: Name of a river.
{@A.@} Location: Listed by Saṃjaya among
the rivers of the Bhāratavarṣa; people use
its water for drinking 6. 10. 33, 13; on the
Citrakūṭa mountain in the Janasthāna 13.
26. 27; 3. 83. 55. {@B.@} Description: Des-
cribed as a river that frees one from sins
({%pāpapramocanī%}) 3. 83. 55; all the rivers
listed by Saṃjaya are described as mothers of
the universe and very strong 6. 10. 35 (for
citation see {@Atikṛṣṇā@}). {@C.@} Holiness:
(1) By bathing there and by being engaged
in worshipping gods and manes ({%tatrābhiṣekaḥ%}
{%kurvāṇaḥ pitṛdevārcane rataḥ%}) one gets the
fruit of an Aśvamedha and the highest state
[Page406-a+ 40]
(after death) ({%gatiṃ ca paramāṃ vrajet%})
3. 83. 56; (2) One who fasts and bathes
there obtains royal glory ({%vigāhya vai nirā-%}
{%hāro rājalakṣmīṃ nigacchati%}) 13. 26. 27.
{@D.@} Importance: Finds place in the Daivata-
Ṛṣi-Vaṃśa 13. 151. 17, 2.
