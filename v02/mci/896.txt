{@Pūrvā Phalgunī@}¦ f. (du., pl.): Name of
a double {%nakṣatra%} distinguished as Pūrvā
and Uttarā. [Referred to also simply as
{@Phalgunī@} 13. 89. 6].
{@A.@} Epic event: Arjuna was born on
mountain Himavant under the junction of
the two constellations Pūrvā Phalgunī and
Uttarā Phalgunī and hence he was called
Phalguna ({%uttarābhyāṃ ca pūrvābhyāṃ%}
{%phalgunībhyām ahaṃ divā / jāto … tena%}
{%māṃ phalgunaṃ viduḥ%}) 4. 39. 14 (Nī., who
reads (Bom. Ed. 4. 44. 16) {%uttarābhyāṃ%}
{%phalagunībhyāṃ nakṣatrābhyām%} explains it
as {%nakṣatrābhyāṃ tārābhyāṃ tatra sthite%}
[Page258-a+ 40]
{%candre ity arthaḥ%}). {@B.@} Religious rites:
(1) {%dāna:%} Nārada told Devakī (13. 63. 2-4)
that if one, after observing fast, gave to Brāh-
maṇas under Pūrvā Phalgunī ({%phalgunī-%}
{%pūrvasamaye%}) food mixed with condensed
sugar cane juice ({%bhakṣān phāṇitasaṃyuk-%}
{%tān%}), one obtained prosperity ({%saubhāgyam%}
{%ṛcchati%}) 13. 63. 13; (2) {%śrāddha:%} Yama
told Śaśabindu (13. 89. 1) that if one offered
a {%kāmya śrāddha%} under (Pūrvā) Phalgunī
one became prosperous ({%phalgunīṣu dadac%}
{%chrāddhaṃ subhagaḥ śrāddhado bhavet%}) 13.
89. 6.
