{@Su-bhadrikā@}¦ occurs in the Aśvamedha (‘horse sacrifice’)
section of the Yajurveda<sup>1</sup> as in some way connected with the
rite. Weber<sup>2</sup> thinks that a proper name, that of the wife of the
king of {@Kāmpīla,@} is intended, but Mahīdhara<sup>3</sup> explains the
word merely as a lady with many lovers or a courtezan, a view
followed by Roth.<sup>4</sup> Since the Taittirīya<sup>5</sup> and Kāṭhaka<sup>6</sup> Saṃ-
hitās have no Subhadrikā, but a vocative {%subhage%} (see {@Subhagā@}),
the sense remains very doubtful.
