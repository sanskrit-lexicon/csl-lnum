{@Jarāyu@}¦ is found once in the Atharvaveda<sup>1</sup> in the sense of
a ‘serpent's skin.’ Usually<sup>2</sup> it denotes the outer covering
(chorion) of the embryo, as opposed to the {%ulva,%} the inner
covering (amnion).
<div n="P"/>Living things are occasionally classified according to their
mode of origin. In the Chāndogya Upaniṣad<sup>3</sup> they are divided
into {%(a) āṇḍa-ja,%} ‘egg-born’; {%(b) jīva-ja,%} ‘born alive,’ or born
from the womb; {%(c) udbhij-ja,%} ‘propagated by sprouts.’ In the
Aitareya Āraṇyaka<sup>4</sup> the division is fourfold: {%(a) āṇḍa-ja;%}
{%(b) jāru-ja,%} that is, {%jarāyu-ja%} (found in the Atharvaveda,<sup>5</sup> and
needlessly read here by Böhtlingk);<sup>6</sup> {%(c) udbhij-ja;%} and {%(d)%}
{%svcda-ja,%} ‘sweat-born,’ explained as ‘insects.’
