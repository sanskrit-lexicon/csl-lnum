<L>1398<pc>1-383<k1>drapsa<k2>drapsa
{@Drapsa@}¦ is a common word from the Rigveda onwards<sup>1</sup> for
a ‘drop’: according to Sāyaṇa,<sup>2</sup> a ‘thick drop’ as opposed to
{%stoka,%} a ‘small drop.’ Hence there frequently occurs the ex-
pression {%dadḥi-drapsa,%}’ ‘drop of curds.’<sup>3</sup> In the Rigveda<sup>4</sup> the word
normally denotes the thick drops of Soma or the Soma itself.
<div n="P"/>In two passages<sup>5</sup> Roth<sup>6</sup> sees the sense of ‘banner,’ which is
adopted by Oldenberg.<sup>7</sup> Geldner,<sup>8</sup> on the other hand, con-
siders that ‘dust’ is meant, but this interpretation is not very
probable. Max Müller<sup>9</sup> renders the word ‘rain-drop’ in one
of the passages.
