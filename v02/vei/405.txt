{@Uṣṭi, Uṣṭra.@}¦ — Both of these words, of which the former is
quite rare,<sup>1</sup> must have the same sense. Roth<sup>2</sup> and Aufrecht<sup>3</sup>
hold that in the Rigveda<sup>4</sup> and the Brāhmaṇas<sup>5</sup> the sense is
‘humped bull’ or ‘buffalo,’ but the former thinks that in the
Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā<sup>6</sup> the sense is doubtful, and ‘camel’ may be
meant. Hopkins<sup>7</sup> is decidedly of opinion that the sense in
every case is ‘camel.’ The animal was used as a beast of
burden yoked in fours.<sup>8</sup>
