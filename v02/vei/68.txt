{@Anu, Ānava.@}¦ — Grassmann and Roth<sup>1</sup> see in these words
designations of people foreign to the Aryans. But it is clear<sup>2</sup>
that they denote a special people, the Anus, who are mentioned
with the {@Yadus, Turvaśas, Druhyus,@} and {@Pūrus,@}<sup>3</sup> with the
Druhyus,<sup>4</sup> and with the Turvaśas, Yadus, and Druhyus.<sup>5</sup> It
is also a fair conclusion from their mention in a passage of the
Rigveda<sup>6</sup> that they dwelt on the Paruṣṇī. The inference that
the Bhṛgus were connected with this tribe is much more
doubtful,<sup>7</sup> for it rests solely on the fact that in one place<sup>8</sup> the
Bhṛgus and Druhyus are mentioned together, and not Anus and
Druhyus. Ānava is used as an epithet of Agni,<sup>9</sup> but also in the
sense of ‘Anu prince,’<sup>10</sup> in one case in conjunction with Turvaśa.<sup>11</sup>
