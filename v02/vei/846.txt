<L>846<pc>1-220<k1>garga<k2>garga
{@Garga@}¦ is the name of a sage who is not mentioned in any of
the Saṃhitās,<sup>1</sup> but his descendants, the {%Gargāḥ Prāvareyāḥ,%}
are referred to in the Kāṭhaka Saṃhitā.<sup>2</sup> Garga himself does
not occur till the Sūtra period.<sup>3</sup>
<F>1) The authorship of Rv. vi. 47 is
attributed to Garga Bhāradvāja in the
Anukramaṇī.</F>
<F>2) xiii. 12. {%Cf.%} Weber, {%Indische Studien,%}
3, 374.</F>
<F>3) Garga-trirātra, Garga-tryaha, a
three-night or three-day feast of Garga.
See Āśvalāyana Srauta Sūtra, x. 2;
Śāṅkhāyana Śrauta Sūtra, xvi. 22. 2;
Kātyāyana Śrauta Sūtra, xxiii. 2, 8.</F>
