{@Kāla-kāñja.@}¦ — In the Atharvaveda<sup>1</sup> mention is made of the
Kālakāñjas as being in the sky. Both Roth<sup>2</sup> and Zimmer<sup>3</sup>
hold that some constellation is meant. But as the defeat of
the Kālakāñjas is one of Indra's exploits,<sup>4</sup> it is doubtful whether
any stress can be laid on that interpretation of the passage in
the Atharvaveda. Whitney<sup>5</sup> suggests that the three stars of
Orion are meant, Bloomfield<sup>6</sup> that the galaxy or the stars in
general are intended.
