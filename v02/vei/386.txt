{@Uru-kakṣa@}¦ occurs in only one passage of the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup>
where the sense of the word is much disputed. The reading
of the text is {%uruḥ kakṣo na gāṅgyaḥ,%} which may refer to a man,
Urukakṣa, ‘dwelling on the Ganges,’<sup>2</sup> or to a man, son of
Gaṅgā, or to a wood so called,<sup>3</sup> or may simply denote the
‘broad thicket on the Ganges.’<sup>4</sup>
