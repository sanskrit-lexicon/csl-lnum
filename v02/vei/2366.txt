{@Mayu@}¦ occurs in the list of victims at the Aśvamedha (‘horse
sacrifice’) in the Yajurveda Saṃhitās.<sup>1</sup> The commentator on
the Taittirīya Saṃhitā<sup>1</sup> explains the word as meaning either
an ‘ape’ ({%kiṃpuruṣa%}) or a ‘forest peacock’ ({%āraṇya-mayūra%}).
[Page2-134+ 48]
The former sense is supported by another passage of the
Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā,<sup>2</sup> where the Mayu, being a substitute for
the man, must be an ape. This sense also suits the word in
the other passage<sup>3</sup> where it occurs.
<F>1) Taittirīya Saṃhitā, v. 5, 12, 1; Vājasanevi Saṃhitā, xxiv. 31.</F>

Saṃhitā, iv. 2, 10, 1.</F>
