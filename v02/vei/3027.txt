{@Vṛjana,@}¦ according to Roth,<sup>1</sup> denotes in several passages of
the Rigveda<sup>2</sup> the ‘settlement’ or ‘village,’ the German ‘Mark’
and its inhabitants. Zimmer,<sup>3</sup> accepting this view, sees in
Vṛjana the ‘secure abode’ ({%kṣiti dhruvā%}) where the clan lives,<sup>4</sup>
the clan itself as a village community (like {@Grāma@}), and the
clan in war.<sup>5</sup> Geldner,<sup>6</sup> on the other hand, takes the literal
sense of Vṛjana ṭo be ‘net,’ developing all the other senses
from that idea, but the traditional view seems more natural.
