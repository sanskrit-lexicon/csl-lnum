{@Ajāta-śatru.@}¦ — He is mentioned as a King of Kāśī (Kāśya) in
the Bṛhadāraṇyaka<sup>1</sup> and Kauṣītaki<sup>2</sup> Upaniṣads, where he in-

Buddhist texts.<sup>3</sup>
