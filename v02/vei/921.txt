{@Gaura,@}¦ a species of ox ({%Bos gaurus%}), is frequently mentioned
with the {@Gavaya@} from the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> onwards.<sup>2</sup> As the Vāja-
saneyi Saṃhitā<sup>3</sup> expressly mentions wild ({%āraṇya%}) Gauras, they
[Page1-242+ 44]
must usually have been tame. The female, Gaurī, is also often
referred to.<sup>4</sup> The compound term Gaura-mṛga (‘the Gaura
wild beast’) is sometimes met with.<sup>5</sup>
