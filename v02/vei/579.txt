{@Kāpya@}¦ (‘descendant of Kapi’) is the patronymic of Sanaka
and Navaka, two obviously fictitious persons who served at
the Sattra (‘sacrificial session’) of the Vibhindukīyas in the
Jaiminīya Brāhmaṇa.<sup>1</sup> It is also the patronymic of Patañcala
in the Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upanisad.<sup>2</sup> See also {@Kaiśerya.@}
