<L>1911<pc>2-014<k1>pUrvavah<k2>pUrvavah
{@Pūrva-vah@}¦ is a term applied to the horse ({@Aśva@}) in the
Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa<sup>1</sup> and elsewhere.<sup>2</sup> It may either refer to a
horse fastened in front as a ‘leader,’ or merely mean ‘drawing
(a chariot) for the first time,’ as understood by the commentator
on the Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa.
<F>1) i. 1, 5, 6</F>
<F>2) Satapatha Brāhmaṇa, ii. 1, 4, 17; Kāṭhaka Saṃhitā, xiii. 3.
{%Cf.%} St. Petersburg Dictionary, {%s.v.%}</F>
