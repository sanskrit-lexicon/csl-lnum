{@Darbha@}¦ is the name of a grass in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> and later.<sup>2</sup>
In the Atharvaveda it is used for the calming of anger ({%manyu-%}
{%śamana%}),<sup>3</sup> and as an amulet for protection against the scattering
of one's hair or the striking of one's breast.<sup>4</sup> It is also said to
be ‘rich in roots’ ({%bhūri-mūla%}),<sup>5</sup> to possess a thousand leaves
({%sahasra-parṇa%}) and a hundred stalks ({%śata-kāṇḍa%}).<sup>6</sup>
