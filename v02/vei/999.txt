<L>999<pc>1-267<k1>Cardis<k2>Cardis
{@Chardis@}¦ occurs often in the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup> and occasionally later,<sup>2</sup>
denoting a secure dwelling-place. The word appears to be
incorrectly written, because the metre shows that the first
syllable is always short. Roth<sup>3</sup> accordingly suggested that
{@Chadis@} should be read instead. But Chadis means ‘roof,’ while
Chardis never has that sense. Bartholomae<sup>4</sup> is therefore prob-
ably right in suggesting some other form, such as Chaḍis.
