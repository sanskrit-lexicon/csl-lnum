<L>221<pc>1-055<k1>AYjana<k2>AYjana
{@Āñjana.@}¦ — A salve frequently referred to in the Atharvaveda,<sup>1</sup>
which came from Mount {@Trikakubh@}<sup>2</sup> in the Himālaya, and was
used to anoint the eyes.<sup>3</sup> The region of the {@Yamunā@}<sup>4</sup> is also
given as a possible place of origin, and the ointment is declared
as potent to remove jaundice, {@Yakṣma, Jāyānya,@} and other
diseases.<sup>5</sup> A female ointment-maker is mentioned in the list of
victims of the {@Puruṣamedha@} (‘human sacrifice’).<sup>6</sup>
<F>1) iv. 9; vi. 102, 3; ix. 6, 11; xix. 44.</F>
<F>2) Av. iv. 9, 9. 10; xix. 44, 6.</F>
