{@Dur-ṇāman,@}¦ ‘of evil name,’ is the designation in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup>
and the Atharvaveda<sup>2</sup> of a demon causing disease, or the
disease itself. The Nirukta<sup>3</sup> explains the words as meaning
a ‘worm,’ an interpretation which accords with the wide-
spread belief in disease-causing worms.<sup>4</sup> Later Durnāman
denotes ‘hæmorrhoids.’<sup>5</sup>
