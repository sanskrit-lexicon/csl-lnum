2. {@Vṛśa Jāna@}¦ (‘descendant of Jana’) is the name of a famous
Purohita, who was unfortunate enough, while with his royal
master, {@Tryaruṇa,@} to see a boy killed by the chariot which the
king drove too fast. He thereupon recalled the boy to life.
The story is told briefly in the Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa,<sup>1</sup> the
Śāṭyāyanaka,<sup>2</sup> the Tāṇḍaka,<sup>3</sup> was also narrated in the Bhāllavi
Brāhmaṇa,<sup>4</sup> and is preserved in the Bṛhaddevatā,<sup>5</sup> Sieg<sup>6</sup> has
endeavoured to trace the story in part in the Rigveda,<sup>7</sup> but
there is a consensus of opinion<sup>8</sup> against the correctness of
such a view.
