<L>347<pc>1-086<k1>udAja<k2>udAja
{@Ud-āja@}¦ is the word used in the Maitrāyaṇī Saṃhitā<sup>1</sup> to denote
the share of the booty of war taken by the king after a victory
({%saṃgrāmaṃ jitvā%}). This interpretation, which is that of
Delbrück,<sup>2</sup> is clearly correct, as against the older version,
‘march out,’ given by von Schroeder<sup>3</sup> and accepted by
Böhtlingk.<sup>4</sup> The Udāja thus corresponds precisely with the
Homeric <lang n="greek">γέρας</lang> This sense also suits Nirāja, the variant of
both the Kāṭhaka<sup>5</sup> and the Kapiṣṭhala<sup>6</sup> Saṃhitās.
