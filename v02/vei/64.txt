{@Anaḍ-vāh@}¦ (lit. ‘cart-drawer’). — This is the common<sup>1</sup> name of
oxen as employed for drawing carts ({@Anas@}). Such oxen were
normally castrated,<sup>2</sup> though not always. Female draft cattle
were also used ({%anaḍuhī%}), but rarely.<sup>3</sup> See also {@Go.@}
<F>1) Rv. x. 59, 10; 85, 10; iii. 53, 18;
Av. iii. 11, 5; iv. 11, 1, etc.; Aitareya
Brāhmaṇa, i. 14; Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa,
ii. 1, 4, 17, etc.</F>
<F>2) {%Cf.%} Kātyāyana Śrauta Sūtra, xv. 1,
5 ({%anaḍvān sāṇḍaḥ%}).</F>
<F>3) Av. iv. 11; Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa,
v, 3, 4, 11. 13.
