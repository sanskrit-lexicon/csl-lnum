<L>3390<pc>2-404<k1>Slezman<k2>Slezman
{@Śleṣman@}¦ means generally that with which parts of a thing
are joined together (from {%śliṣ,%} ‘join’): with reference to a hide,<sup>1</sup>
[Page2-405+ 52]
‘laces’ of some sort may be intended; to a chariot,<sup>2</sup> ‘bonds’
or ‘cords’ are probably meant; and to wood,<sup>3</sup> ‘glue’ is perhaps
the sense.
<F>1) Aitareya Brāhmaṇa, v. 32, 6; Jaiminīya Upanisad Brāhmaṇa, iii. 17, 3;
Chāndogya Upaniṣad, iv. 17, 4.</F>
<F>2) Kāṭhaka Saṃhitā, xxxiv. 9. {%Cf.%}
Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa, xvi. 1, 13,
where a chariot ({@Ratha@}) is called {%śleṣ-%}
{%mavant,%} ‘tied with ropes.’</F>
<F>3) Kauṣītaki Brāhmaṇa, vi. 12. {%Cf.%}
the Upaniṣads, cited in n. 1, and
Sāṅkhāyana Āraṇyaka, ii. 1, which
looks like a bad secondary version of
the passage in the Jaiminīya.</F>
