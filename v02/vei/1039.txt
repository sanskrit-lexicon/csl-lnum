{@Jātū-karṇya,@}¦ ‘descendant of {@Jātūkarṇa,@}’ is the patronymic
of several persons.
<div n="P"/>({%a%}) A pupil of {@Āsurāyaṇa@} and {@Yāska@} bears this name in
a Vaṃśa (list of teachers) of the Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad in
the Kāṇva recension.<sup>1</sup> In the Mādhyaṃdina<sup>2</sup> he is a pupil
of {@Bhāradvāja.@}
<div n="P"/>({%b%}) A Kātyāyanī-putra, ‘son of Kātyāyanī,’ bears this name
in the Śāṅkhāyana Āraṇyaka.<sup>3</sup>
<div n="P"/>({%c%}) A Jātūkarṇya is mentioned in the Kauṣītaki Brāhmaṇa<sup>4</sup>
as a contemporary of {@Alīkayu Vācaspatya@} and other sages.
<div n="P"/>({%d%}) Jātūkarṇya is in the Sūtras<sup>5</sup> frequently a patronymic of
teachers whose identity cannot be determined. The same
person or different persons may here be meant.
<F>1) ii. 6, 3; iv. 6, 3.</F>
<F>2) ii. 5, 21; iv. 5, 27.</F>
<F>3) viii. 10.</F>
<F>4) xxvi. 5 (Jātukarṇya in Lindner's
index, 159, is a misprint).</F>
<F>5) Aitareya Āraṇyaka, v. 3, 3; Śāṅkh-
āyana Srauta Sūtra, i. 2, 17; iii. 16,
14; 20, 19; xvi. 29, 6 ({@Jala@}); Kātyāyana
Srauta Sūtra, iv. 1, 27; xx. 3, 17;
xxv. 7, 34, etc.
