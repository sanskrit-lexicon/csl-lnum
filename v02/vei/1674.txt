{@Para Āṭṇāra@}¦ (‘descendant of Aṭṇāra’) appears in the later
Saṃhitās<sup>1</sup> and the Brāhmaṇas<sup>2</sup> as one of the ancient great
kings who won sons by performing a particular sacrifice. In
the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>3</sup> he is styled Hairaṇyanābha, ‘de-
scendant of Hiraṇyanābha,’ and in the Śāṅkhāyana Śrauta
Sūtra<sup>4</sup> he is called Para Āhlāra Vaideha, a fact testifying to
the close connexion of {@Kosala@} and {@Videha.@} A {@Yajña-gāthā,@}
or ‘sacrificial verse,’ there<sup>5</sup> cited mentions {@Hiraṇyanābha@}
{@Kausalya@} in connexion with Para.
