<L>793<pc>1-202<k1>kzatra<k2>kzatra<h>1
1. {@Kṣatra,@}¦ in the general sense of ‘dominion,’ ‘rule,’ ‘power,’
as exercised by gods and men, occurs frequently from the
Rigveda<sup>1</sup> onwards. The word is also found in the concrete
sense of ‘rulers’ in the Rigveda<sup>2</sup> and later;<sup>3</sup> but in no case
does it in the Rigveda certainly<sup>4</sup> mean what it regularly denotes
in the later Saṃhitās,<sup>5</sup> the ruling class as opposed to the priests
({@Brahman@}), the subject people ({@Viś, Vaiśya@}), and the servile
class ({@Śūdra@}). See also {@Kṣatriya.@} A Kṣatra-pati is several
times mentioned<sup>6</sup> as an equivalent of ‘king.’
