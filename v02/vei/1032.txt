{@Jalāṣa-bheṣaja,@}¦ ‘whose remedy is Jalāṣa,’ is an epithet of
Rudra in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> and the Atharvaveda.<sup>2</sup> The word
[Page1-280+ 54]
{@Jālāṣa@} occurs in a hymn of the Atharvaveda,<sup>3</sup> where it denotes
a remedy, perhaps, for a tumour or boil.<sup>4</sup> The commentator
on this passage and the Kauśika Sūtra<sup>5</sup> regard Jālāṣa as
meaning ‘urine,’ which seems a probable interpretation.<sup>6</sup> But
Geldner<sup>7</sup> thinks that rain-water, conceived as urine, is meant;
and the Naighaṇṭuka<sup>8</sup> identifies {%jalāṣa%} and {%udaka%} ‘water.’
