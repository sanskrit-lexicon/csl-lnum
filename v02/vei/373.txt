{@Upa-vāka@}¦ occurs in the Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā<sup>1</sup> and the Brāh-
maṇas<sup>2</sup> as a description of a species of grain, the {%Wrightia%}
{%antidysenterica,%} known later as Indra-yava. The commentator
Mahīdhara<sup>3</sup> simply glosses it with the more general term {@Yava.@}

According to the Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā, it formed the essential
element of gruel ({%karambha%}), and Upavāka groats ({%saktavaḥ%}) are
mentioned in the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa.<sup>4</sup>

ing’).</F>
