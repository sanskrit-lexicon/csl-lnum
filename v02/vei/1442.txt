{@Dhiṣaṇā,@}¦ according to the St. Petersburg Dictionary, denotes<sup>1</sup>
an implement used in preparing the Soma, ‘bowl’ or ‘vat,’
and by metonymy also the Soma draught itself.<sup>2</sup> The dual, by
[Page1-400+ 54]
a metaphor,<sup>3</sup> also expresses the ‘two worlds,’ heaven and earth.<sup>4</sup>
Hillebrandt,<sup>5</sup> however, thinks that the word properly means
earth,<sup>6</sup> in the dual heaven and earth,<sup>7</sup> in the plural the triad,
earth, atmosphere, and heaven,<sup>8</sup> while in some passages<sup>9</sup>
Dhiṣaṇā denotes the Vedi, the excavated ground used as an
altar. This is not, however, certain, while it seems clear that
the Vājasaneyi<sup>10</sup> and Taittirīya<sup>11</sup> Saṃhitās understand the
Dhiṣaṇās (dual) to be the planks over which the pressing of
the Soma took place ({%adhiṣavaṇa-phalake%}).<sup>12</sup> Pischel<sup>13</sup> sees in
Dhiṣaṇā a goddess of wealth akin to Aditi and the earth.
