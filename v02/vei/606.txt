{@Kāṣṭhā@}¦ seems to have the sense in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> of ‘course’
for a chariot race. It also means in the Rigveda<sup>2</sup> and later<sup>3</sup>
‘goal,’ either like the {@Kārṣman@} the turning place, or the final
goal ({%paramā kāṣṭhā%}).
<F>1) i. 37, 10; 65, 3; iv. 58, 7; vi. 46, 1;
vii. 93, 3; viii. 80, 8; ix. 21, 7.</F>
<F>2) x. 102, 9, is perhaps so to be taken.</F>
<F>3) Av. ii. 14, 6: Taittirīya Saṃhitā,
i. 6, 9, 3; Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā, ix. 13;
Aitareya Brāhmaṇa, iv. 7; Satapatha
Brāhmaṇa, xi. 5, 7, 2. etc.
