<L>552<pc>1-145<k1>kaSyapa<k2>kaSyapa
{@Kaśyapa@}¦ is the name of a sage who is mentioned only once
in the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup> but is a common figure in the later Saṃhitās.<sup>2</sup>
He is always of a mythical character, as belonging to the distant
past. According to the Aitareya Brāhmaṇa,<sup>3</sup> he anointed King
{@Viśvakarman Bhauvana,@} and in the Upaniṣads<sup>4</sup> he is mentioned
as a Ṛṣi. The Kaśyapas appear in connexion with {@Janam-@}
{@ejaya@} in the Aitareya Brāhmaṇa.<sup>5</sup>

<F>3) viii. 21; Satapatha Brāhmaṇa,
