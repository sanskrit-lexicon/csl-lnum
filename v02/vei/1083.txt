{@Jyā@}¦ is the regular word for ‘bowstring’ in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup>
and later.<sup>2</sup> The making of bowstrings was a special craft, as
is shown by the occurrence of the Jyā-kāra, or ‘maker of bow-
strings,’ among the victims at the Puruṣamedha, or human
sacrifice, in the Yajurveda.<sup>3</sup> The bowstring consisted of a
thong of ox-hide.<sup>4</sup> It was not usually kept taut,<sup>5</sup> but was
specially tightened when the bow was to be used.<sup>6</sup> The sound
of the bowstring ({%jyā-ghoṣa%}) is referred to in the Atharvaveda.<sup>7</sup>
