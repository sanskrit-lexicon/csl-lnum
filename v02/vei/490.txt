{@Auśija,@}¦ ‘descendant of Uśij,’ is a patronymic clearly applied
to {@Kakṣīvant@} in the Rigveda.<sup>1</sup> It is also applied to {@Ṛjiśvan,@}<sup>2</sup>
but Ludwig<sup>3</sup> thinks that the correct reading of the passage in
question is {%auśijasyarjiśvā,%} ‘Ṛjiśvan, son of Auśija.’ In one
verse<sup>4</sup> Auśija and Kakṣīvant are both mentioned, but in such a
way that two different persons must apparently be meant. In
other passages where the patronymic occurs alone, it is doubtful
who is meant, or whether a proper name at all is intended.<sup>5</sup>
Kakṣīvant Auśija appears also in the Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa<sup>6</sup>
and elsewhere.
