{@Gauri-vīti Śāktya@}¦ (‘descendant of {@Śakti@}’) or {@Gaurīviti,@} as
the name is also spelt,<sup>1</sup> is the Ṛṣi, or Seer, of a hymn of the
Rigveda,<sup>2</sup> and is frequently mentioned in the Brāhmaṇas.<sup>3</sup>
According to the Jaiminīya Brāhmaṇa,<sup>4</sup> he was Prastotṛ at the
Sattra, or sacrificial session, celebrated by the {@Vibhindukīyas@}
and mentioned in that Brāhmaṇa.
<F>1) Satapatha Brāhmaṇa, xii. 8, 3, 7;
Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa, xi. 5; xii. 13;
xxv. 7.</F>
<F>2) v. 29, 11.</F>
<F>3) Aitareya Brāhmaṇa, iii. 19; viii. 2
and see n. 1.</F>
<F>4) ii. 233 ({%Journal of the American%}
{%Oriental Society,%} 18, 38).

veda, 3, 126; Oldenberg, {%Zeitschrift der%}
