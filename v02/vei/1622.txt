{@Nair-ukta@}¦<sup>1</sup> in the Nirukta<sup>2</sup> denotes a man who knows the
true etymology of words, and explains their meaning accord-
ingly. Yāska's Nirukta is the classic work of this school, and
forms a commentary on an earlier Nirukta, the so-called
Naighaṇṭuka, a glossary consisting of five collections of Vedic
words.
<F>1) ‘One concerned with etymological
explanation’ ({%nir-ukta%}).</F>

xiii. 9.
