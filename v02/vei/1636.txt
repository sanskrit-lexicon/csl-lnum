{@Pakṣas@}¦ is found in the Atharvaveda<sup>1</sup> and the Kauṣītaki
Brāhmaṇa<sup>2</sup> meaning the ‘sides’<sup>3</sup> of a chariot. In the Kāṭhaka
Saṃhitā<sup>4</sup> and the Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa<sup>5</sup> it is used of the sides
of a hut or chamber ({@Śālā@}). In the Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā<sup>6</sup> it
means the ‘wing’ of a door. In the Kauṣītaki Brāhmaṇa<sup>7</sup> the
‘half’ of an army is so named, and in the Pañcaviṃśa Brāh-
maṇa<sup>8</sup> it means the ‘half’ of a month, or ‘fortnight.’ {%Cf.%}
