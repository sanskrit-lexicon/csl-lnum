{@Kṛka-vāku,@}¦ the ‘cock,’ being named in the Atharvaveda<sup>1</sup>
with sheep, goats, and other domesticated animals, was pre-
sumably tamed.<sup>2</sup> In the list of victims at the horse sacrifice
in the Yajurveda,<sup>3</sup> it appears as dedicated to Savitṛ: Yāska<sup>4</sup>
explains this by the fact that it declares the time of day
({%kālānuvāda%}). The commentator Mahīdhara<sup>5</sup> explains the
name by {%tāmra-cūḍa,%} ‘red-crested.’ It is of course onomato-
poetic (‘calling kṛka’).<sup>6</sup> See also {@Kukkuṭa.@}
