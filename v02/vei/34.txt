<L>34<pc>1-014<k1>ajina<k2>ajina
{@Ajina.@}¦ — This word denotes generally the skin of an animal
-{%e.g.,%} a gazelle,<sup>1</sup> as well as that of a goat ({@Aja@}).<sup>2</sup> The use
of skins as clothing is shown by the adjective ‘clothed in skins’
({%ajina-vāsin%}) in the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa,<sup>3</sup> and the furrier's trade
is mentioned in the Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā.<sup>4</sup> The Maruts also
wear deer-skins,<sup>5</sup> and the wild ascetics ({%muni%}) of a late Rigveda
hymn<sup>6</sup> seem to be clad in skins ({@Mala@}).
