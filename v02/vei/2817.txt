{@Varatrā@}¦ in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> and later<sup>2</sup> denotes a ‘thong’ or
‘strap.’ It was used to fasten the oxen to the yoke,<sup>3</sup> or perhaps
to fasten the yoke to the pole.<sup>4</sup> Or, again, it denotes<sup>5</sup> the
strap which was used in drawing up water from the well
({@Avata@}).
<F>1) iv. 57, 4 (of the plough), etc.</F>
<F>2) Av. xi. 3, 10; xx. 135, 13.</F>
<F>3) Rv. x. 60, 8; 102, 8; Geldner,
{%Vedische Studien,%} 2, 13.</F>
<F>4) This suits x. 60, 8, rather more
naturally, and is so taken by Zimmer,
{%Altindisches Leben,%} 248, 249.</F>
<F>5) Rv. x. 106, 5; Zimmer, {%op. cit.,%} 156.</F>
