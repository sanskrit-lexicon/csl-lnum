{@Brāhmaṇāc-chaṃsin@}¦ (‘reciting after the Brāhmaṇa — {%i.e.,%}
Brahman’) is the name of a priest in the Brāhmaṇas.<sup>1</sup> In the
technical division of the sacrificial priests ({@Ṛtvij@}) he is classed
with the Brahman,<sup>2</sup> but it is clear that he was really a Hotraka
or assistant of the Hotṛ.<sup>3</sup> According to Oldenberg,<sup>4</sup> he was
known to the Rigveda as {@Brahman.@} This is denied by
Geldner,<sup>5</sup> who sees in Brahman merely the ‘superintending
priest’ or the ‘priest.’

Brāhmaṇa, xxviii. 3; Taittirīya Brāh-

iv. 2, 3, 13, etc.</F>
