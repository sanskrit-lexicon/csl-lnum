<L>321<pc>1-080<k1>iBya<k2>iBya
{@Ibhya@}¦ occurs once in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> in the plural, when a king
is said to devour his Ibhyas as the fire the forest; and twice
in the Chāndogya Upaniṣad,<sup>2</sup> in one passage as the first
member of a compound, and in the other as either a proper
name or an adjective. Roth,<sup>3</sup> Ludwig,<sup>4</sup> and Zimmer<sup>5</sup> interpret
the word as ‘retainers’ in the Rigveda, but in the Chāndogya
Upaniṣad Roth thinks it means ‘rich.’ Pischel and Geldner<sup>6</sup>
accept the sense in all passages. Böhtlingk in his trans-
lation of the Chāndogya treats the word as simply a proper
name, ‘Ibhya's village’ ({%ibhya-grāma%}) and ‘Ibhya.’
