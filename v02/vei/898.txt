{@Godhā.@}¦ — ({%a%}) The sense of ‘bowstring’ seems certain in one
passage of the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup> and possible in another.<sup>2</sup> Roth<sup>3</sup>
also adopts this meaning in the only passage of the Atharva-
veda<sup>4</sup> where the word occurs.
<div n="P"/>({%b%}) In one passage of the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> the sense of ‘musical
instrument’ is recognized by Roth and by Hillebrandt<sup>2</sup> for
this word.
<div n="P"/>({%c%}) Elsewhere<sup>1</sup> an animal seems to be meant, perhaps the
‘crocodile,’ as Ludwig<sup>2</sup> and Weber<sup>3</sup> think; perhaps a large
‘lizard,’ as Roth and Zimmer<sup>4</sup> assume. An animal is probably
also meant in the Atharvaveda.<sup>5</sup>
