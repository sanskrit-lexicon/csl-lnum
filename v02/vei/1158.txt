<L>1158<pc>1-312<k1>tila<k2>tila
{@Tila@}¦ denotes in the Atharvaveda<sup>1</sup> and later<sup>2</sup> the sesamum
plant, and particularly its grains, from which a rich oil ({@Taila@})
was extracted. It is often<sup>3</sup> mentioned in connexion with {@Māṣa,@}
‘kidney bean.’ The Taittirīya Saṃhitā<sup>4</sup> attributes the bean
and the sesamum to the winter ({%hemanta%}) and the cool ({%śiśira%})
seasons. The stalk of the sesamum plant ({%tila-piñjī,%}<sup>5</sup> {%til-piñja%}<sup>6</sup>)
was used for fuel, and the seed was boiled in the form of
porridge ({%tilaudana%}<sup>7</sup>) for food.
