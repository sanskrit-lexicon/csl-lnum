{@Camasa@}¦ denotes a ‘drinking vessel,’ usually as employed for
holding {@Soma@} at the sacrifice. It is frequently mentioned from
the Rigveda onwards.<sup>1</sup> It was made of wood ({%vṛkṣa%}),<sup>2</sup> and is
hence called {%dru.%}<sup>3</sup> According to the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa,<sup>4</sup> it
was made of {@Udumbara@} wood.
