{@Sapta Sindhavaḥ,@}¦ ‘the seven rivers,’ occur only once in the
Rigveda as the designation of a definite country,<sup>1</sup> while else-
where<sup>2</sup> the seven rivers themselves are meant. Max Müller<sup>3</sup>
thinks that the five streams of the Panjab, with the Indus and
the {@Sarasvatī,@} are intended; others<sup>4</sup> hold that the {@Kubhā@} should
be substituted for the Sarasvatī, or that perhaps the Oxus<sup>5</sup>
must originally have been one of the seven. Zimmer<sup>6</sup> is prob-
ably right in laying no stress at all on any identifications;
‘seven’ being one of the favourite numbers in the Rigveda and
later.
