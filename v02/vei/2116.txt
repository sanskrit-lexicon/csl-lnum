<L>2116<pc>2-057<k1>Parvara<k2>Parvara
{@Pharvara,@}¦ a word occurring only once in the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup>
cannot be interpreted with certainty. It may mean a ‘field in
bloom.’<sup>2</sup> Sāyaṇa<sup>3</sup> explains it as ‘filler,’ and Grassmann as
perhaps a ‘sower.’<sup>4</sup>
<F>1) x. 106, 2.</F>
<F>2) {%Cf.%} Weber, {%Indische Studien,%} 17, 260.</F>
<F>3) In his commentary on Rv. x. 106, 2.
He explains the intensive form {%par-%}
{%pharat,%} occurring in the same hymn
(x. 106, 7), as from a verb meaning
to ‘fill.’</F>
<F>4) {%Wörterbuch, s.v.%}</F>
