{%dramma%}¦ (EI 25, 30; CII 4; SITI; BL), name of a coin;
originally derived from Greek {%drachma.%}
<div n="P">(IE 8-8), a silver coin, equal to one-sixteenth of a gold
{%niṣka%} according to the {%Līlāvalī.%}
<div n="P">{%Cf. pañciyaka-dramma%} (EI 30), {%Śrīmadādivarāha-dramma,%}
{%Vigraha-dramma, Vigrahapāla-dramma, Vigrahapāla-satka-dramma,%}
{%Vigrahapālīya-dramma, Vigrahatuṅgīya-dramma%} (IE 8-8; EI 1);
{%Bhīmapriya-dramma%} (IE 8-8); {%poruttha-dramma%} (EI 23).--(IE 8-8),
cf. {%gara-dramma, dāya-dramma, belliya-dramma%} (i. e. silver {%dramma%}),
etc., of Kannaḍa inscriptions; the name {%dramma%} was also applied
to the silver coin called {%purāṇa, dharaṇa%} or {%kārṣāpaṇa,%} though it
was often used as the name of copper coins; money. For gold
and ordinary (copper) {%dramma,%} cf. {%Ep. Ind.,%} Vol. XXX, p. 111.
