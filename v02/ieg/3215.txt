{%Mahāpratihāra, Mahāpratīhāra%}¦ (IE 8-2; EI 30; CII 3;
BL), head of the door-keepers of the palace or the king's
chamber, or of the capital; city; sometimes explained as ‘the
chief usher’ (CII 4). See {%Pratihāra.%}
<div n="P">({%HD%}), the superior officer above all door-keepers, the
great chamberlain. See {%Kādambarī,%} para. 97; {%Rājataraṅgiṇī,%} IV.
142; {%Ep. Ind.,%} Vol. X, p. 75; etc.
<div n="P">({%IE%} 8-2), one of the designations often included in the
{%pañca-mahāśabda,%} as indicated by the {%Rājataraṅgiṇī.%}
<div n="P">({%IE%} 8-3), sometimes also called {%Mahārāja, Mahāsāmanta,%}
{%Mahāpīlupati, Pañcakaraṇ-oparika, Pāṭy-uparika, Purapāl-oparika,%}
etc., additionally.
