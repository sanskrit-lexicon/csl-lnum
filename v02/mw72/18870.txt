{#cedi#}¦ {%cedi, ayas,%} m. pl., N. of a people who
lived in Bandela-khaṇḍa (Bundelkhand) and were re-
nowned in epic poetry for their attachment to ancient
laws and institutions; their capital was Śuktimatī, and
some of their kings were Vasu Upari-cara, Su-bāhu,
Dhṛṣṭa-ketu, Dama-ghoṣa, Śiśu-pāla, &c.; their
ancestor who was a son of Kaiśika or Uśika is also
called Cedi; their country is supposed by some to
be the same as the modern Chandail. {%—Cedi-pa,%}
{%as,%} m. a prince of the Cedis. {%—Cedi-pati, is,%} m. a
N. of Vasu Upari-cara; a N. of Śiśu-pāla. {%—Cedi-%}
{%bhūbhṛt, t,%} or {%cedi-rāja, as,%} m. a N. of Śiśu-pāla,
a king of the Cedayas slain by Kṛṣṇa; see {%śiśu-%}
{%pāla.%}
