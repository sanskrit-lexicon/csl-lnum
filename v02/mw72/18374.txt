{#cARakya#}¦ {%cāṇakya, as,%} m. (a patronymic fr.
{%caṇaka,%} N. of a Muni), N. of the sage Vātsyāyana;
N. of a celebrated Brāhman also called Viṣṇu-gupta,
the reputed author of a work on morals and the princi-
ples of government. He is said to have destroyed
the Nanda dynasty and to have made Candra-gupta
(q. v.) king, whose minister he became. From the
tone of his advice to princes he has been styled the
Machiavelli of India; ({%am%}), n., scil. {%śāstram,%} the
work of Cāṇakya, on morals and polity. {%—Cāṇakya-%}
{%mūlaka, am,%} n. a kind of radish; [cf. {%kauṭilya.%}]
{%—Cāṇakya-śloka, ās,%} m. pl. detached stanzas on
morals and the principles of government, supposed
to be written by the Brāhman Cāṇakya or compiled
by him.
