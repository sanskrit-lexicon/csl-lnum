{%Dasra, as, ā, am,%}¦ Ved. destroying, destructive,
overcoming enemies, accomplishing wonderful deeds,
giving marvellous aid; worthy to be seen, handsome,
beautiful; ({%as%}), m., N. of Pūṣan; N. of one of
the two Aśvins (the other being called Nāsatya); an
ass; a robber, thief, &c., see {%dasyu;%} ({%au,%} Ved. {%ā%}), m.
du., N. or epithet of the Aśvins, said to be so called

in the nom. and voc.); a N. of Indra-Viṣṇu; the
numeral two; ({%ās%}), m. pl. an epithet of the Maruts;
({%am%}), n. the cold season, hoar frost. In Sūrya-sid-
dhānta VIII. 9, {%dasrādīnām%} must be translated ‘of
the Nakṣatra Aśvinī, &c.,’ see {%dasra-devatā;%} [cf.
{%daṃsana, daṃsas, daṃsa, daṃsiṣṭha.%}] {%—Dasra-%}
{%devatā,%} f. the lunar constellation Aśvinī. {%—Dasra-%}
{%sū, ūs,%} f. ‘the mother of the Aśvins,’ N. of Sañjñā
